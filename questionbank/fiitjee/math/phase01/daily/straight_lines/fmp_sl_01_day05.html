  1. Identify the Equations of the Sides:
    • \( 7x + y - 10 = 0 \)
    • \( x - 2y + 5 = 0 \)
    • \( x + y + 2 = 0 \)
  2. Find the Vertices of the Triangle:
    • \( A(1, 3), B(-3, 1), C(2, -4) \)
  3. Find the Altitude from \( A(1,3) \) to BC \( (x + y + 2 = 0) \):
    • The slope of BC is \( -1 \), so the slope of the altitude from \( A \) is \( 1 \).
    • Given one point is A(1, 3)
    • Subtitute in y = mx + c to get c = 2
    • Equation: \( y = x + 2 \)
  4. Find the Altitude from \( B(-3, 1) \) to AC \( 7x + y - 10 = 0
    • The slope of AC is \( -7 \), so the slope of the altitude from \( B \) is \( \frac{1}{7} \).
    • Given one point is B(-3, 1)
    • Subtitute in y = mx + c to get c = \( \frac{10}{7} \)
    • Equation: \( 7y = x + 10 \)
  5. Find the Intersection of the Altitudes:
    • Solve \( y = x + 2 \) and \( 7y = x + 10 \):
    • \( \left( -\frac{2}{3}, \frac{4}{3} \right) \)

The orthocenter is \( \left( -\frac{2}{3}, \frac{4}{3} \right) \).

  1. Identify the Equations of the Sides:
    • \(3x - y - 5 = 0\)
    • \(x + 2y - 4 = 0\)
    • \(5x + 3y + 1 = 0\)
  2. Find the Vertices of the Triangle:
    • Solve \(3x - y - 5 = 0\) and \(x + 2y - 4 = 0\): \[ x = 2, \, y = 1 \quad \Rightarrow \quad A(2, 1) \]
    • Solve \(x + 2y - 4 = 0\) and \(5x + 3y + 1 = 0\): \[ x = 1, \, y = \frac{3}{2} \quad \Rightarrow \quad B(1, \frac{3}{2}) \]
    • Solve \(3x - y - 5 = 0\) and \(5x + 3y + 1 = 0\): \[ x = -1, \, y = -2 \quad \Rightarrow \quad C(-1, -2) \]
  3. Find the Midpoints of the Sides:
    • Midpoint of \( AB \): \[ \left( \frac{2 + 1}{2}, \frac{1 + \frac{3}{2}}{2} \right) = \left( \frac{3}{2}, \frac{5}{4} \right) \]
    • Midpoint of \( BC \): \[ \left( \frac{1 + (-1)}{2}, \frac{\frac{3}{2} + (-2)}{2} \right) = \left( 0, -\frac{1}{4} \right) \]
  4. Find the Equations of the Perpendicular Bisectors:
    • Perpendicular bisector of \( AB \):
      Slope of \( AB \) is \( \frac{\frac{3}{2} - 1}{1 - 2} = -\frac{1}{2} \)
      Perpendicular slope is \( 2 \)
      Equation: \[ y - \frac{5}{4} = 2 \left( x - \frac{3}{2} \right) \] Simplify: \[ y = 2x - \frac{1}{4} \]
    • Perpendicular bisector of \( BC \):
      Slope of \( BC \) is \( \frac{-2 - \frac{3}{2}}{-1 - 1} = \frac{-7}{-2} = \frac{7}{2} \)
      Perpendicular slope is \( -\frac{2}{7} \)
      Equation: \[ y + \frac{1}{4} = -\frac{2}{7}x \] Simplify: \[ y = -\frac{2}{7}x - \frac{1}{4} \]
  5. Find the Intersection of the Perpendicular Bisectors:
    • Solve \( y = 2x - \frac{1}{4} \) and \( y = -\frac{2}{7}x - \frac{1}{4} \):
    • Substitute \( y = 2x - \frac{1}{4} \) into \( y = -\frac{2}{7}x - \frac{1}{4} \): \[ 2x - \frac{1}{4} = -\frac{2}{7}x - \frac{1}{4} \] Simplify: \[ 2x = -\frac{2}{7}x \] \[ 14x = -2x \] \[ x = -\frac{1}{2} \] Substitute \( x = -\frac{1}{2} \) into \( y = 2x - \frac{1}{4} \): \[ y = 2(-\frac{1}{2}) - \frac{1}{4} = -1 - \frac{1}{4} = -\frac{5}{4} \]

The circumcenter is \( \left( -\frac{1}{2}, -\frac{5}{4} \right) \).
