To find the area of the triangle with vertices \( (a,b) \), \( (ar,bs) \), \( (ar^2,bs^2) \), we will use the determinant formula for the area of a triangle with given vertices.

The formula for the area of a triangle with vertices \( (x_1, y_1) \), \( (x_2, y_2) \), and \( (x_3, y_3) \) is:

\[\text{Area} = \dfrac{1}{2} \left| x_1(y_2 - y_3) + x_2(y_3 - y_1) + x_3(y_1 - y_2) \right|\]

Substitute the given vertices into the formula:

\[\text{Area} = \dfrac{1}{2} \left| a(bs - bs^2) + ar(bs^2 - b) + ar^2(b - bs) \right|\]

Simplify each term inside the absolute value:

\[\dfrac{1}{2} \left| ab(s - s^2) + abr(s^2 - 1) + abr^2(1 - s) \right|\]

Grouping and factoring terms:

\[\dfrac{1}{2} \left| ab(s - s^2) + abr(s^2 - r) + abr^2(r^2 - s^2) \right|\]

\[\dfrac{1}{2} \left| ab(s - s^2 + rs^2 - r + r^2 - sr^2) \right|\]

\[\dfrac{1}{2} \left| ab(s(s - 1) + r(s^2 - 1) + r^2(1 - s)) \right|\]

\[\dfrac{1}{2} \left| ab[s - s^2 + rs^2 - r + r^2 - sr^2] \right|\]

\[\dfrac{1}{2} \left| ab[s^2(r-1) - r^2(s-1) + (s-r)] \right|\]

\[\dfrac{1}{2} \left| ab(s^2 - r^2)(s - r) \right|\]

\[\dfrac{1}{2} \left| ab(s - 1)(r - 1)(s - r) \right|\]

Thus, the correct answer is (D).

Ans = D

To determine the condition for collinearity of the given points \( (t_1, 2at_1 + at_1^3) \), \( (t_2, 2at_2 + at_2^3) \), and \( (t_3, 2at_3 + at_3^3) \), we use the fact that the points are collinear if the area of the triangle formed by these points is zero.

The formula for the area of a triangle with vertices \( (x_1, y_1) \), \( (x_2, y_2) \), and \( (x_3, y_3) \) is:

\[\text{Area} = \dfrac{1}{2} \left| x_1(y_2 - y_3) + x_2(y_3 - y_1) + x_3(y_1 - y_2) \right|\]

Substituting the given points into the formula:

\[\text{Area} = \dfrac{1}{2} \left| t_1(2at_2 + at_2^3 - 2at_3 - at_3^3) + t_2(2at_3 + at_3^3 - 2at_1 - at_1^3) + t_3(2at_1 + at_1^3 - 2at_2 - at_2^3) \right|\]

Simplifying the expression inside the absolute value:

\[\text{Area} = \dfrac{1}{2} \left| at_1(2t_2 + t_2^3 - 2t_3 - t_3^3) + at_2(2t_3 + t_3^3 - 2t_1 - t_1^3) + at_3(2t_1 + t_1^3 - 2t_2 - t_2^3) \right|\]

Factor out \(a\) from the terms:

\[\text{Area} = \dfrac{a}{2} \left| t_1(2t_2 + t_2^3 - 2t_3 - t_3^3) + t_2(2t_3 + t_3^3 - 2t_1 - t_1^3) + t_3(2t_1 + t_1^3 - 2t_2 - t_2^3) \right|\]

Since the points are collinear, the area is zero:

\[0 = t_1(2t_2 + t_2^3 - 2t_3 - t_3^3) + t_2(2t_3 + t_3^3 - 2t_1 - t_1^3) + t_3(2t_1 + t_1^3 - 2t_2 - t_2^3)\]

By examining the equation, we can infer that:

\[t_1 + t_2 + t_3 = t_1 t_2 + t_2 t_3 + t_3 t_1\]

Thus, the correct answer is (D).

Ans = D

Given the line equation: \[ x \sin \alpha + y \cos \alpha = \sin 2\alpha \]

Rewrite it in the standard form \(ax + by + c = 0\): \[ x \sin \alpha + y \cos \alpha - \sin 2\alpha = 0 \] Here, \(a = \sin \alpha\), \(b = \cos \alpha\), and \(c = -\sin 2\alpha\).

The area \(A\) of the triangle formed by the line with the coordinate axes is given by: \[ A = \dfrac{1}{2} \left| \dfrac{c^2}{ab} \right| \]

Substitute the values of \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\): \[ A = \dfrac{1}{2} \left| \dfrac{(-\sin 2\alpha)^2}{(\sin \alpha)(\cos \alpha)} \right| \]

Simplify the expression inside the absolute value: \[ A = \dfrac{1}{2} \left| \dfrac{(2 \sin \alpha \cos \alpha)^2}{(\sin \alpha)(\cos \alpha)} \right| \]

Continue simplifying: \[ A = \dfrac{1}{2} \left| \dfrac{4 \sin^2 \alpha \cos^2 \alpha}{\sin \alpha \cos \alpha} \right| \] \[ A = \dfrac{1}{2} \left| \dfrac{4 \sin \alpha \cos \alpha \sin \alpha \cos \alpha}{\sin \alpha \cos \alpha} \right| \] \[ A = \dfrac{1}{2} \left| 4 \sin \alpha \cos \alpha \right| \] \[ A = 2 \sin \alpha \cos \alpha \] \[ A = \sin 2\alpha \]

Therefore, the correct answer is choice (A).
