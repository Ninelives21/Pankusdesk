Correct Answer: The lattice energy of a compound depends on the charge of the ions and the size of the ions involved. Lattice energy is directly proportional to the product of the charges of the ions and inversely proportional to the sum of the ionic radii.

Explanation:

  • MgO has a higher lattice energy than NaCl because:
    • The charge on the ions in MgO is greater (Mg²⁺ and O²⁻) compared to NaCl (Na⁺ and Cl⁻).
    • Although both Mg²⁺ and Na⁺ are cations, Mg²⁺ has a higher charge than Na⁺, and similarly, O²⁻ has a higher charge than Cl⁻.
    • The greater charges on the ions in MgO result in a stronger electrostatic attraction between the ions, leading to higher lattice energy.
  • Conclusion: The higher charge and smaller size of the ions in MgO compared to NaCl lead to a higher lattice energy for MgO.

Correct Answer: The lattice enthalpy of CaCl₂ is approximately –2870.8 kJ/mol.

Explanation:

  • The lattice enthalpy can be calculated using the Born-Haber cycle.
  • Formula:
    \[ \Delta H_{\text{lattice}} = \Delta H_{\text{formation}} - \left( \Delta H_{\text{sublimation}} + \Delta H_{\text{dissociation}} + \Delta H_{\text{ionization}} + 2 \times \Delta H_{\text{electron gain enthalpy}} \right) \]
  • Substituting the values:
    \[ \Delta H_{\text{lattice}} = -795 \, \text{kJ/mol} - \left(121 + 242.8 + 2422 - 710\right) \, \text{kJ/mol} \]
  • Simplified result:
    \[ \Delta H_{\text{lattice}} = -2870.8 \, \text{kJ/mol} \]
  • Conclusion: The lattice enthalpy of CaCl₂ is approximately –2870.8 kJ/mol.
