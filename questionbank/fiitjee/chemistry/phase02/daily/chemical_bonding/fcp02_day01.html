Ionic Mobility: In an aqueous solution, ionic mobility depends on the size of the hydrated ion. Smaller ions typically have lower mobility because they tend to be more heavily hydrated (surrounded by water molecules), which increases their effective size and reduces mobility. Larger ions are less hydrated and can move more freely, leading to higher mobility.

1. NaCl (Sodium chloride): Sodium ions (Na+) are relatively small and heavily hydrated, which reduces their mobility.

2. LiCl (Lithium chloride): Lithium ions (Li+) are even smaller than sodium ions and are even more heavily hydrated, making them have lower ionic mobility.

3. CsCl (Cesium chloride): Cesium ions (Cs+) are large, and therefore less hydrated, which gives them higher mobility in an aqueous solution.

4. BeCl2 (Beryllium chloride): Beryllium ions (Be2+) are small and have a +2 charge, leading to strong hydration and very low mobility.

Therefore, the correct answer is choice (C) CsCl.
