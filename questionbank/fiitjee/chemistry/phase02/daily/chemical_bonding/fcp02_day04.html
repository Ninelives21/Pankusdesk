Correct Answer: (B) \( C_6H_{12}O_6 \) (fructose), (C) \( H_2N-CO-NH_2 \) (urea)

Explanation: \( sp^2 \) hybridisation occurs when a carbon atom forms three sigma bonds and one pi bond (double bond) with other atoms. This hybridisation uses one s-orbital and two p-orbitals to form three \( sp^2 \) hybrid orbitals, leaving one unhybridized p-orbital for the pi bond.

  • Choice (A): \( CH_3OH \) (methanol) has all carbon atoms in an \( sp^3 \) hybridized state, as the carbon forms four sigma bonds (C-H and C-O bonds). Therefore, no \( sp^2 \) hybridized carbon is present.
  • Choice (B): \( C_6H_{12}O_6 \) (fructose) contains some carbon atoms involved in double bonds, specifically in the carbonyl group. The carbonyl group contains \( sp^2 \)-hybridized carbon, making this a correct choice.
  • Choice (C): \( H_2N-CO-NH_2 \) (urea) contains a carbonyl group (\( C=O \)), where the carbon forms a double bond with oxygen. This carbon is \( sp^2 \)-hybridized, making this another correct choice.
  • Choice (D): \( CCl_4 \) (carbon tetrachloride) has carbon forming four sigma bonds with chlorine atoms, which makes it \( sp^3 \)-hybridized. No \( sp^2 \) hybridisation is present in this molecule.
