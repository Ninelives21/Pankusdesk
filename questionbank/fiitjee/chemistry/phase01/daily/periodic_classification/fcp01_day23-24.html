To determine which of the ions Na-, Mg-, Al-, Si- is the least stable, we must analyze their electronic configurations and energy levels:

  • Na-: The electronic configuration of Na- is \(1s^2 2s^2 2p^6 3s^2\). Sodium naturally prefers to lose its 3s electron to achieve a noble gas configuration. Adding an electron to fill the 3s orbital is unfavorable, but not the most unstable because it stays within the same energy level.
  • Mg-: The electronic configuration of Mg- is \(1s^2 2s^2 2p^6 3s^2 3p^1\). Magnesium prefers to lose two electrons to form Mg2+ with a stable noble gas configuration. Adding an electron to the higher-energy 3p orbital is highly unfavorable, making Mg- the most unstable ion.
  • Al-: The electronic configuration of Al- is \(1s^2 2s^2 2p^6 3s^2 3p^2\). Aluminum prefers to lose three electrons, but the added electron results in a partially filled 3p orbital, making it slightly more stable than Mg-.
  • Si-: The electronic configuration of Si- is \(1s^2 2s^2 2p^6 3s^2 3p^3\). Silicon is more electronegative and the 3p^3 configuration is relatively stable due to the half-filled orbital, making Si- the most stable among the four ions.

Comparison: Mg- is the least stable ion because the extra electron occupies a higher-energy 3p orbital, which magnesium typically does not fill, resulting in significant instability. Na-, while unstable, does not introduce a new subshell. Al- and Si- are more stable due to their more favorable electronic configurations.

Therefore, the correct answer is Mg-.
