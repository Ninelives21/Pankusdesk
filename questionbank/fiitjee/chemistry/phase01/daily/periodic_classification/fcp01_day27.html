  • Nitrogen (N): Nitrogen has an electron configuration of 1s²2s²2p³. The p orbital is half-filled, which is a stable configuration due to exchange energy. Adding an extra electron would disrupt this stability by pairing up one of the p electrons, which is not energetically favorable. Thus, Nitrogen has a low electron affinity due to its half filled orbital.

Correct Answer: 3.76 K.Cal

Explanation:
  • The atomic weight of Fluorine (F) is 19 amu. The number of moles of Fluorine in 1 gram is calculated as:
    Number of moles = Mass / Molar mass = 1 gram / 19 grams/mole = 1/19 moles
  • The electron affinity (EA) of Fluorine is given as 3.1 eV. The energy released per mole of Fluorine in K.Cal is:
    Energy released per mole = 3.1 eV × 23.06 K.Cal/mole = 71.486 K.Cal/mole
  • The total energy released for 1 gram of Fluorine is:
    Total energy released = Energy per mole × Number of moles = 71.486 K.Cal/mole × 1/19 moles = 3.76 K.Cal

Explanation:

  • The energy released when an electron is added to a neutral Cl atom to form Cl– is 3.60 eV (Electron Affinity, EA).
  • For one Avogadro number of Cl atoms (6 × 1023 atoms), the total energy released is:
    Total Energy Released = 3.60 eV × 6 × 1023 atoms
  • The number of Cl atoms that can be ionized by this energy is calculated as:
    Number of Cl atoms ionized = (3.60 eV × 6 × 1023) / 13.0 eV
    Number of Cl atoms ionized = 1.66 × 1023 atoms

Correct Answer: (A) \(O^-\) ion will tend to resist the addition of another electron.

Explanation:
  • First Step (Exothermic): The first step involves the addition of one electron to an oxygen atom to form \(O^-\). This process is exothermic, meaning it releases energy because the oxygen atom has a high electron affinity and readily gains an electron.
  • Second Step (Endothermic): The second step involves the addition of another electron to the \(O^-\) ion to form \(O_2^{2-}\). This process is endothermic, meaning it requires energy input because the \(O^-\) ion, having already gained one electron, experiences repulsion from the incoming second electron. This makes it difficult to add another electron, thus requiring energy input.

  • Beryllium (Be): Beryllium has very low electron affinity due to its full 2s subshell, which makes it highly stable.
  • Nitrogen (N): Nitrogen has a lower electron affinity than carbon due to its half-filled p-orbital, which provides additional stability.
  • Carbon (C): Carbon has a moderate electron affinity, higher than nitrogen.
  • Oxygen (O): Oxygen has a higher electron affinity than nitrogen because it has a greater tendency to gain an electron, despite some electron-electron repulsion in the p-orbital.
  • Fluorine (F): Fluorine has the highest electron affinity among these elements due to its high electronegativity and small atomic size, leading to a strong attraction for an additional electron.

Correct Answer: (B) F

Explanation:

  • Electron Affinity: Electron affinity is the energy released when an electron is added to a neutral atom or ion in its gaseous state.
  • The ionization energy of F⁻ (the energy required to remove an electron from F⁻) is equal in magnitude to the electron affinity of F (the energy released when an electron is added to a neutral F atom to form F⁻). Correct Answer: (D) I < Br < F < Cl

Explanation:
  • Fluorine (F): Although fluorine is highly electronegative, its small size leads to significant electron-electron repulsion, slightly reducing its electron affinity compared to chlorine.
  • Chlorine (Cl): Chlorine has the highest electron affinity in the periodic table because it has a larger size than fluorine, leading to less electron-electron repulsion and thus a higher affinity for an additional electron.
  • Bromine (Br): Bromine, being larger than chlorine, has a lower electron affinity because the added electron is further from the nucleus and experiences less attraction.
  • Iodine (I): Iodine has the lowest electron affinity among the halogens because it is the largest atom, and the added electron is even further from the nucleus, experiencing even less attraction.

  • (D) Cl⁻ < K⁺ < Ca²⁺ < Sc³⁺: (P) Electron affinity, (R) Ionisation energy — This option corresponds to both electron affinity and ionization energy trends. As you move across a period, ionization energy increases, and electron affinity becomes more negative.
