Adding an extra electron would require placing it in a higher energy 2p orbital, which is not favorable. Therefore, Beryllium has a low electron affinity due to its fully filled orbital.
  • Nitrogen (N): Nitrogen has an electron configuration of 1s²2s²2p³. The p orbital is half-filled, which is a stable configuration due to exchange energy. Adding an extra electron would disrupt this stability by pairing up one of the p electrons, which is not energetically favorable. Thus, Nitrogen has a low electron affinity due to its half filled orbital.

Correct Answer: 3.76 K.Cal

Explanation:
  • The atomic weight of Fluorine (F) is 19 amu. The number of moles of Fluorine in 1 gram is calculated as:
    Number of moles = Mass / Molar mass = 1 gram / 19 grams/mole = 1/19 moles
  • The electron affinity (EA) of Fluorine is given as 3.1 eV. The energy released per mole of Fluorine in K.Cal is:
    Energy released per mole = 3.1 eV × 23.06 K.Cal/mole = 71.486 K.Cal/mole
  • The total energy released for 1 gram of Fluorine is:
    Total energy released = Energy per mole × Number of moles = 71.486 K.Cal/mole × 1/19 moles = 3.76 K.Cal

Correct Answer: Approximately 1.66 × 1023 Cl atoms

Explanation:
  • The energy required to remove an electron from Cl– (to ionize Cl–) is 13.0 eV (Ionization Potential, IP).
  • The energy released when an electron is added to a neutral Cl atom to form Cl– is 3.60 eV (Electron Affinity, EA).
  • For one Avogadro number of Cl atoms (6 × 1023 atoms), the total energy released is:
    Total Energy Released = 3.60 eV × 6 × 1023 atoms
  • The number of Cl atoms that can be ionized by this energy is calculated as:
    Number of Cl atoms ionized = (3.60 eV × 6 × 1023) / 13.0 eV
    Number of Cl atoms ionized = 1.66 × 1023 atoms

Correct Answer: 4.80 kcal

Explanation:
  • The atomic weight of Chlorine (Cl) is 35.5 amu. The number of moles of Chlorine in 2 grams is calculated as:
    Number of moles = Mass / Molar mass = 2 grams / 35.5 grams/mole ≈ 0.0563 moles
  • The electron gain enthalpy (EA) of Chlorine is given as 3.7 eV. The energy released per mole of Chlorine in kcal is:
    Energy released per mole = 3.7 eV × 23.06 kcal/mole = 85.322 kcal/mole
  • The total energy released for 2 grams of Chlorine is:
    Total energy released = Energy per mole × Number of moles = 85.322 kcal/mole × 0.0563 moles ≈ 4.80 kcal
