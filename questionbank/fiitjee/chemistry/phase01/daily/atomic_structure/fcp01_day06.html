SUBJECTIVE

To calculate the ratio of the \(1^{st}\) Bohr's orbit radius for \(He^+\) and \(Li^{2+}\) ions, follow these steps:

1. The Bohr radius \(r_n\) for an ion is given by the formula:

\[ r_n = \dfrac{n^2 \cdot a_0}{Z} \]

2. For the first orbit (\(n = 1\)):

\[ r_1 = \dfrac{a_0}{Z} \]

3. For \(He^+\) ion, \(Z = 2\):

\[ r_1(He^+) = \dfrac{a_0}{2} \]

4. For \(Li^{2+}\) ion, \(Z = 3\):

\[ r_1(Li^{2+}) = \dfrac{a_0}{3} \]

5. The ratio of the \(1^{st}\) Bohr's orbit radius for \(He^+\) and \(Li^{2+}\) ions is:

\[ \dfrac{r_1(He^+)}{r_1(Li^{2+})} = \dfrac{\dfrac{a_0}{2}}{\dfrac{a_0}{3}} = \dfrac{3}{2} \]

Therefore, the ratio of the \(1^{st}\) Bohr's orbit radius for \(He^+\) and \(Li^{2+}\) ions is \(\dfrac{3}{2}\).

To calculate the total energy of an electron in the H-atom given its kinetic energy, follow these steps:

1. In the H-atom, the total energy \( E_{\text{total}} \) of an electron is related to its kinetic energy \( K.E. \) by the formula:

\[ E_{\text{total}} = -K.E. \]

2. Given the kinetic energy \( K.E. = 4.55 \times 10^{-25} \, \text{J} \), the total energy is:

\[ E_{\text{total}} = -4.55 \times 10^{-25} \, \text{J} \]

Therefore, the total energy of the electron in the H-atom is \( -4.55 \times 10^{-25} \, \text{J} \).

To find the atomic number of the hydrogen-like species, follow these steps:

1. The ionization energy of a hydrogen-like species is given by: \[ E = 13.6 \times Z^2 \text{ eV} \] where \(Z\) is the atomic number.

2. Given the ionization energy of \(He^+\) (Z=2) is \(19.6 \times 10^{-18}\) J, we can calculate: \[ 19.6 = 13.6 \times 2^2 \]

3. Given the ionization energy of the unknown hydrogen-like species is \(44.1 \times 10^{-18}\) J, we can calculate: \[ 44.1 = 13.6 \times Z^2 \]

4. Solving for \(Z\): \[ Z^2 = \dfrac{44.1}{13.6} \implies Z \approx 3 \]

Therefore, the atomic number of the hydrogen-like species is 3.

To calculate the second ionization energy (I.E.) of He, follow these steps:

1. The ionization energy of a hydrogen-like species is given by:

\[ \text{I.E.} = Z^2 \times \text{I.E. of H} \]

where \( Z \) is the atomic number.

2. For He, \( Z = 2 \). Therefore, the second I.E. of He is:

\[ \text{I.E. of He} = 2^2 \times 21.79 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{J} \]

3. Calculate the value:

\[ \text{I.E. of He} = 4 \times 21.79 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{J} = 87.16 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{J} \]

Therefore, the second ionization energy of He is \( 87.16 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{J} \).

To determine the energy of the hydrogen atom in its first excited state, follow these steps:

1. We know that in the ground state, \( n = 1 \)

2. The total energy (T.E) is given by the formula: \[ \text{Total energy} = - \text{kinetic energy} = \dfrac{\text{potential energy}}{2} \]

3. For the ground state (n=1), the potential energy (P.E) is: \[ \text{P.E} = 2 \left[\text{Total Energy}\right] = 2 \left[-13.6 \, \text{eV} \right] = -27.2 \, \text{eV} \]

4. Given that the potential energy is assumed to be zero in this problem, we adjust it by adding 27.2 eV.

5. Since the kinetic energy remains unchanged in all energy states, the potential energy in all states will increase by 27.2 eV.

6. For the first excited state (n=2), the energy (E_2) is: \[ E_2 = -3.4 \, \text{eV} \, \text{[previously calculated]} \] Adjusting for the increase in potential energy: \[ E_2 = \left[-3.4 + 27.2\right] \, \text{eV} = 23.8 \, \text{eV} \]

Therefore, the correct answer is choice (C).

Ans = C

- \( n \) is the principal quantum number.

MATCH THE FOLLOWING

To solve this matching question, we need to understand the relationships between kinetic energy, potential energy, and total energy in the context of an atom:

1. For a bound electron, the total energy (Etotal) is the sum of its kinetic energy (T) and potential energy (V).

2. The kinetic energy is related to the potential energy by a factor, often -1/2, because the potential energy is negative for a bound system.

3. The relationship between potential energy and total energy can be summarized by:

    (a) Kinetic energy / Potential energy = -1/2

    (b) Potential energy + 2 x Kinetic energy = 0 (since potential energy is negative and kinetic energy is positive and equal in magnitude)

    (c) Potential energy / Total energy = 2

    (d) Kinetic energy / Total energy = -1

Based on these relationships, we can match the columns as follows:

(A) Kinetic energy / Potential energy = -1/2 -> Q

(B) Potential energy + 2 x Kinetic energy = 0 -> S

(C) Potential energy / Total energy = 2 -> P

(D) Kinetic energy / Total energy = -1 -> R

Therefore, the matches are as follows:

A - Q

B - S

C - P

D - R

Ans = A - Q; B - S; C - P; D - R
