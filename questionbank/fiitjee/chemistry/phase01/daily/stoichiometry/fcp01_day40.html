To determine how many grams of NaOH are needed to completely neutralize the HCl produced when 1 gram of H2 gas reacts with 2 grams of Cl2 gas, we follow these steps:

1. First, write the balanced chemical equation:

H2 + Cl2 → 2HCl

2. Then, calculate the moles of H2 and Cl2:

- Molar mass of H2: 2 g/mol
- Molar mass of Cl2: 71 g/mol

Given:
- Mass of H2 = 1 g
- Mass of Cl2 = 2 g

Moles of H2 = 1 g / 2 g/mol = 0.5 mol

Moles of Cl2 = 2 g / 71 g/mol ≈ 0.028 mol

Since Cl2 is the limiting reactant:

Moles of HCl produced = 0.028 mol × 2 = 0.056 mol

The neutralization reaction is:

HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H2O

The moles of NaOH needed to neutralize 0.056 moles of HCl are also 0.056 moles.

3. Calculate the mass of NaOH required:

- Molar mass of NaOH: 40 g/mol

Mass of NaOH = 0.056 mol × 40 g/mol = 2.24 g

Therefore, 2.24 grams of NaOH are needed to completely neutralize the HCl produced.

Na2CO3 + CaCl2 → CaCO3 + 2NaCl

- Molar mass of CaCO3: 100 g/mol

0.7 g of Na2CO3·nH2O is dissolved in 100 mL. 20 mL of this solution required 19.8 mL of 0.1 M HCl. To determine the value of n, follow these steps:

1. Write the balanced chemical equation:

Na2CO3 + 2HCl → 2NaCl + H2O + CO2

2. Calculate the moles of HCl used:

- Molarity of HCl = 0.1 M
- Volume of HCl = 19.8 mL = 0.0198 L
- Moles of HCl = Molarity × Volume = 0.1 M × 0.0198 L = 0.00198 moles

3. Determine the moles of Na2CO3 in 20 mL solution:

- Moles of Na2CO3 = 0.00198 moles of HCl / 2 = 0.00099 moles

4. Calculate the moles of Na2CO3·nH2O in 100 mL solution:

- Moles of Na2CO3 in 100 mL = 0.00099 moles × 5 = 0.00495 moles

5. Calculate the molar mass of Na2CO3·nH2O:

- Given mass = 0.7 g
- Molar Mass = Given Mass / Moles = 0.7 g / 0.00495 moles = 141.41 g/mol

6. Determine the value of n:

- Molar mass of Na2CO3 = 106 g/mol
- Molar mass of H2O = 18 g/mol
- 141.41 = 106 + 18n
- 18n = 35.41
- n = 35.41 / 18 ≈ 2

Therefore, the value of n is 2.
