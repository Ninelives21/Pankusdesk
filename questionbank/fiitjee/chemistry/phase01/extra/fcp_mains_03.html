Given:

The potential energy (\(U\)) of an electron in a hydrogen-like ion is given by:

\[ U = -\dfrac{Z e^2}{4 \pi \epsilon_0 r} \]

where:

\(Z\) is the atomic number (for \(\text{Li}^{2+}\), \(Z = 3\)),

\(e\) is the elementary charge,

\(\epsilon_0\) is the permittivity of free space,

\(r\) is the radius of the electron's orbit.

For the \(\text{Li}^{2+}\) ion, which has \(Z = 3\), the formula becomes:

\[ U = -\dfrac{3 e^2}{4 \pi \epsilon_0 r} \]

Therefore, the potential energy of the electron present in the ground state of the \(\text{Li}^{2+}\) ion is:

\[ U = -\dfrac{3 e^2}{4 \pi \epsilon_0 r} \]

Therefore, the correct answer is choice (D).

Ans = D

Given:

The number of revolutions per second (\(v\)) of an electron in an orbit is given by the formula:

\[ v \propto \dfrac{1}{n^3} \]

where \(n\) is the principal quantum number.

Given:

\[ v_2 = 8 \times v_n \]

Since \(v \propto \dfrac{1}{n^3}\), we can write:

\[ v_2 = \dfrac{k}{2^3} = \dfrac{k}{8} \]

\[ v_n = \dfrac{k}{n^3} \]

According to the given condition:

\[ \dfrac{k}{8} = 8 \times \dfrac{k}{n^3} \]

\[ \dfrac{1}{8} = \dfrac{8}{n^3} \]

\[ n^3 = 64 \]

\[ n = \sqrt[3]{64} \]

\[ n = 4 \]

Therefore, the correct answer is choice (C).

Ans = C
