SUBJECTIVE

To calculate the molarity of NaOH in the solution prepared by dissolving 4g in enough water to form 250 mL of the solution, follow these steps:

1. Determine the molar mass of NaOH:

- Molar mass of NaOH: Na (23 g/mol) + O (16 g/mol) + H (1 g/mol) = 40 g/mol

2. Calculate the moles of NaOH:

Moles of NaOH = Mass / Molar Mass = 4g / 40 g/mol = 0.1 mol

3. Convert the volume of the solution to liters:

Volume = 250 mL = 250 / 1000 = 0.25 L

4. Calculate the molarity (M) of the NaOH solution:

Molarity (M) = Moles of solute / Volume of solution in liters = 0.1 mol / 0.25 L = 0.4 M

Therefore, the molarity of the NaOH solution is 0.4 M.

OBJECTIVE

To determine the increasing order of e/m (charge to mass ratio) for electron (e), proton (p), neutron (n) and alpha particle (\(\alpha\)), consider the following:

1. Neutron (n) has zero charge, so its charge to mass ratio (e/m) is zero.

2. Alpha particle (\(\alpha\)) has a charge of \(+2e\) and a mass of 4 times that of a proton. Thus, its e/m ratio is \(\dfrac{2e}{4m_p} = \dfrac{e}{2m_p}\).

3. Proton (p) has a charge of \(+e\) and its mass is \(m_p\). Thus, its e/m ratio is \(\dfrac{e}{m_p}\).

4. Electron (e) has a charge of \(-e\) and its mass is \(m_e\). Thus, its e/m ratio is \(\dfrac{-e}{m_e}\). The absolute value of this ratio is significantly larger than that of a proton or alpha particle because the mass of an electron is much smaller.

5. Comparing the values, we get the following order: \(n < \alpha < p < e\).

Therefore, the correct answer is choice (D).

Ans = D
