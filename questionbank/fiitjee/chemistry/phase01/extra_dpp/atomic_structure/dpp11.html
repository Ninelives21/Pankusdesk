To determine the mass of 1 mole of electrons, we need to use the following information:

1. The mass of a single electron is approximately \( 9.109 \times 10^{-31} \) kg.

2. Avogadro's number, which is the number of particles in one mole of a substance, is approximately \( 6.022 \times 10^{23} \).

The mass of 1 mole of electrons can be calculated as follows:

\(\text{Mass of 1 mole of electrons} = (\text{mass of one electron}) \times (\text{Avogadro's number})\)

Substituting the values, we get:

\(\text{Mass of 1 mole of electrons} = (9.109 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}) \times (6.022 \times 10^{23})\)

\(\text{Mass of 1 mole of electrons} = 5.48 \times 10^{-7} \text{ kg}\)

To convert this mass into grams (since 1 kg = 1000 grams):

\(5.48 \times 10^{-7} \text{ kg} = 5.48 \times 10^{-4} \text{ grams}\)

This is approximately 0.55 mg, since 1 gram = 1000 milligrams.

Therefore, the correct answer is choice (A).

Ans = A

To determine the atomic number of B, we need to use the concept of isoneutronic nuclides, which have the same number of neutrons.

Given:

Mass number of A = 76

Atomic number of A = 32

The number of neutrons in A is calculated as:

\(\text{Number of neutrons in A} = \text{Mass number of A} - \text{Atomic number of A} = 76 - 32 = 44\)

Since nuclide B is isoneutronic with nuclide A, it also has 44 neutrons. Given the mass number of B:

Mass number of B = 77

The atomic number of B is calculated as:

\(\text{Atomic number of B} = \text{Mass number of B} - \text{Number of neutrons in B} = 77 - 44 = 33\)

Therefore, the correct answer is choice (A).

Ans = A

To calculate the frequency and wave number of a radiation having wavelength 600 nm, we use the following formulas:

1. The speed of light (\(c\)) is \(3 \times 10^8 \, \text{m/s}\).

2. The wavelength (\(\lambda\)) is given as 600 nm. First, we convert this to meters:

\(600 \, \text{nm} = 600 \times 10^{-9} \, \text{m} = 6 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{m}\).

3. The frequency (\(\nu\)) is calculated using the formula:

\(\nu = \dfrac{c}{\lambda}\)

Substituting the values:

\(\nu = \dfrac{3 \times 10^8 \, \text{m/s}}{6 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{m}}\)

\(\nu = 5 \times 10^{14} \, \text{Hz}\)

4. The wave number (\(\tilde{\nu}\)) is calculated using the formula:

\(\tilde{\nu} = \dfrac{1}{\lambda}\)

Substituting the values:

\(\tilde{\nu} = \dfrac{1}{6 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{m}}\)

\(\tilde{\nu} = 1.67 \times 10^6 \, \text{m}^{-1}\)

Therefore, the frequency of the radiation is \(5 \times 10^{14} \, \text{Hz}\) and the wave number is \(1.67 \times 10^6 \, \text{m}^{-1}\).

To find the frequency and wavenumber of yellow light with a wavelength of 580 nm, we use the following formulas:

1. The speed of light (\(c\)) is \(3 \times 10^8 \, \text{m/s}\).

2. The wavelength (\(\lambda\)) is given as 580 nm. First, we convert this to meters:

\(580 \, \text{nm} = 580 \times 10^{-9} \, \text{m} = 5.8 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{m}\).

3. The frequency (\(\nu\)) is calculated using the formula:

\(\nu = \dfrac{c}{\lambda}\)

Substituting the values:

\(\nu = \dfrac{3 \times 10^8 \, \text{m/s}}{5.8 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{m}}\)

\(\nu = 5.17 \times 10^{14} \, \text{Hz}\)

4. The wavenumber (\(\tilde{\nu}\)) is calculated using the formula:

\(\tilde{\nu} = \dfrac{1}{\lambda}\)

Substituting the values:

\(\tilde{\nu} = \dfrac{1}{5.8 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{m}}\)

\(\tilde{\nu} = 1.72 \times 10^6 \, \text{m}^{-1}\)

Therefore, the frequency of the yellow light is \(5.17 \times 10^{14} \, \text{Hz}\) and the wavenumber is \(1.72 \times 10^6 \, \text{m}^{-1}\).

To find the fraction of an atom that is occupied by the nucleus, we need to compare their volumes.

1. The volume of an atom can be approximated as a sphere with radius \(r_{\text{atom}} = 10^{-8}\) cm:

\[ V_{\text{atom}} = \dfrac{4}{3} \pi (10^{-8})^3 \]

2. The volume of the nucleus can be approximated as a sphere with radius \(r_{\text{nucleus}} = 10^{-13}\) cm:

\[ V_{\text{nucleus}} = \dfrac{4}{3} \pi (10^{-13})^3 \]

3. The fraction of the atom's volume that is occupied by the nucleus is given by:

\[ \dfrac{V_{\text{nucleus}}}{V_{\text{atom}}} = \dfrac{\dfrac{4}{3} \pi (10^{-13})^3}{\dfrac{4}{3} \pi (10^{-8})^3} = \left(\dfrac{10^{-13}}{10^{-8}}\right)^3 = \left(10^{-5}\right)^3 = 10^{-15} \]

Therefore, the fraction of the atom occupied by the nucleus is \(10^{-15}\).

Ans = \(10^{-15}\)
