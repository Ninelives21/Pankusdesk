SUBJECTIVE

To calculate the molarity of NaOH in the solution prepared by dissolving 4g in enough water to form 250 mL of the solution, follow these steps:

1. Determine the molar mass of NaOH:

- Molar mass of NaOH: Na (23 g/mol) + O (16 g/mol) + H (1 g/mol) = 40 g/mol

2. Calculate the moles of NaOH:

Moles of NaOH = Mass / Molar Mass = 4g / 40 g/mol = 0.1 mol

3. Convert the volume of the solution to liters:

Volume = 250 mL = 250 / 1000 = 0.25 L

4. Calculate the molarity (M) of the NaOH solution:

Molarity (M) = Moles of solute / Volume of solution in liters = 0.1 mol / 0.25 L = 0.4 M

Therefore, the molarity of the NaOH solution is 0.4 M.

To calculate the molality of the solution, follow these steps:

1. Determine the mass of the solution:

Density = 1.25 g/mL
Volume = 1 L = 1000 mL
Mass of the solution = Density × Volume = 1.25 g/mL × 1000 mL = 1250 g

2. Calculate the moles of NaCl in the solution:

Molarity (M) = 3 M
Volume = 1 L
Moles of NaCl = Molarity × Volume = 3 mol/L × 1 L = 3 mol

3. Determine the mass of NaCl in the solution:

Molar mass of NaCl = 58.5 g/mol
Mass of NaCl = Moles × Molar mass = 3 mol × 58.5 g/mol = 175.5 g

4. Calculate the mass of the solvent (water) in the solution:

Mass of the solution = 1250 g
Mass of NaCl = 175.5 g
Mass of water = Mass of solution - Mass of solute = 1250 g - 175.5 g = 1074.5 g = 1.0745 kg

5. Calculate the molality (m) of the solution:

Molality (m) = Moles of solute / Mass of solvent in kg = 3 mol / 1.0745 kg ≈ 2.79 m

Therefore, the molality of the NaCl solution is approximately 2.79 m.

(a) To calculate the molarity of a solution of ethanol in water with a mole fraction of ethanol being 0.040, follow these steps:

1. Assume 1 mole of solution:

- Mole fraction of ethanol (Xethanol) = 0.040
- Mole fraction of water (Xwater) = 1 - Xethanol = 1 - 0.040 = 0.960

2. Calculate the moles of ethanol and water:

- Moles of ethanol = Xethanol × 1 mole = 0.040 mole
- Moles of water = Xwater × 1 mole = 0.960 mole

3. Calculate the mass of water:

- Molar mass of water (H2O) = 18 g/mol
- Mass of water = Moles of water × Molar mass of water = 0.960 mole × 18 g/mol = 17.28 g

4. Calculate the volume of water (assuming the density of water to be 1 g/mL):

- Volume of water = Mass of water / Density of water = 17.28 g / 1 g/mL = 17.28 mL = 0.01728 L

5. Calculate the molarity of ethanol:

- Molarity (M) = Moles of solute / Volume of solution in liters = 0.040 mole / 0.01728 L ≈ 2.31 M

Therefore, the molarity of the ethanol solution is approximately 2.31 M.

To determine the concentration of NO3- ions in the mixture, follow these steps:

1. Calculate the moles of AgNO3 and NaCl:

- Moles of AgNO3 = 0.1 M × Volume

- Moles of NaCl = 0.2 M × Volume

2. Since equal volumes of both solutions are mixed, let's assume the volume of each solution is V liters.

3. Total volume of the mixture = V + V = 2V liters.

4. Total moles of NO3- ions in the mixture come only from AgNO3:

- Moles of NO3- = 0.1V

5. Concentration of NO3- ions in the mixture = Total moles / Total volume:

- Concentration = (0.1V) / (2V) = 0.05 M

Therefore, the concentration of NO3- ions in the mixture is 0.05 M.

To find the correct way to express the concentration of NaCl in the solution, follow these steps:

1. Calculate the mass fraction:

Mass fraction = mass of solute / (mass of solute + mass of solvent)
Mass fraction = 10 g / (10 g + 250 g) = 10 g / 260 g ≈ 0.0385 (which rounds to 0.04)

2. Calculate the mole fraction:

Molar mass of NaCl = 58.44 g/mol
Moles of NaCl = 10 g / 58.44 g/mol ≈ 0.171 mol
Moles of water = 250 g / 18.015 g/mol ≈ 13.88 mol
Mole fraction of NaCl = moles of NaCl / (moles of NaCl + moles of water)
Mole fraction = 0.171 mol / (0.171 mol + 13.88 mol) ≈ 0.0122

3. Calculate the molality:

Molality (m) = moles of solute / mass of solvent in kg

Mass fraction = 0.04
Mole fraction = 0.0122
Molality = 0.684 mol/kg

To determine the molarity of chloride ion in the final solution when equal volumes of 3.5 M CaCl2 and 3 M NaCl are mixed, follow these steps:

1. Write the dissociation equations for the salts:

CaCl2 → Ca2+ + 2Cl-
NaCl → Na+ + Cl-

2. Determine the contribution of each solution to the chloride ion concentration:

- Molarity of Cl- from CaCl2: 3.5 M CaCl2 produces 2 × 3.5 M = 7 M Cl-
- Molarity of Cl- from NaCl: 3 M NaCl produces 3 M Cl-

3. Calculate the final molarity of Cl-:

When equal volumes are mixed, the concentrations are averaged. So, the final concentration of Cl- will be:

Final molarity of Cl- = (7 M + 3 M) / 2 = 5 M

Therefore, the molarity of chloride ion in the final solution is 5 M.

Ans = 5 M
