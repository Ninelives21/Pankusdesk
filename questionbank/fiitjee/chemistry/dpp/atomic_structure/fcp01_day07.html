SUBJECTIVE

To calculate the velocity of the electron in \( \text{Li}^{2+} \) in the second orbit, follow these steps:

The velocity of an electron in the nth shell of the hydrogen-like atom is given by:

\[ v = \dfrac{2.18 \times 10^6 \times Z}{n} \text{ m·s}^{-1} \]

For \( \text{Li}^{2+} \): \( Z = 3 \)

For n = 2 (second orbit):

\[ v = \dfrac{2.18 \times 10^6 \times 3}{2} \text{ m·s}^{-1} \]

Calculate the velocity:

\[ v = 3.27 \times 10^6 \text{ m·s}^{-1} \]

Therefore, the velocity of the electron in \( \text{Li}^{2+} \) in the second orbit is \( 3.27 \times 10^6 \text{ m·s}^{-1} \).

Wave number \( \tilde{\nu} \) for a transition (n + 1) → n:

\[ \tilde{\nu} = \dfrac{1}{\lambda} = R \left[ \dfrac{1}{n^2} - \dfrac{1}{(n + 1)^2} \right] Z^2 \]

\[ \Rightarrow \dfrac{1}{\lambda} = \left( \dfrac{2n + 1}{n^2 (n + 1)^2} \right) R Z^2 \]

For \( n \gg 1 \), we have:

\[ 2n + 1 \approx 2n \quad \text{and} \quad (n + 1)^2 \approx n^2 \]

Therefore:

\[ \dfrac{1}{\lambda} = \left( \dfrac{2n}{n^2 \cdot n^2} \right) R Z^2 = \dfrac{2 R Z^2}{n^3} \]

Since \( \nu = \dfrac{c}{\lambda} \), we get:

\[ \nu = \dfrac{2 c R Z^2}{n^3} \]

Thus, \( \nu \propto n^{-3} \).

The velocity of an electron in the nth shell of the hydrogen-like atom is given by: \[ v = \dfrac{Z e^2}{2 n \hbar} \]

For the second orbit (n = 2), the velocity \(v_2\) is: \[ v_2 = \dfrac{Z e^2}{2 \cdot 2 \hbar} = \dfrac{Z e^2}{4 \hbar} \]

For the fourth orbit (n = 4), the velocity \(v_4\) is: \[ v_4 = \dfrac{Z e^2}{2 \cdot 4 \hbar} = \dfrac{Z e^2}{8 \hbar} \]

Therefore, the ratio of the velocities is: \[ \dfrac{v_4}{v_2} = \dfrac{\dfrac{Z e^2}{8 \hbar}}{\dfrac{Z e^2}{4 \hbar}} = \dfrac{1}{2} \]

Thus, the velocity in the fourth orbit \(v_4\) is: \[ v_4 = \dfrac{x}{2} \]
