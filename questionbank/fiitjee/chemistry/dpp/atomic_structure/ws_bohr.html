Explanation:

To solve this problem, we need to consider the energy of the \(\alpha\)-particle as it approaches the nucleus of a copper atom. We will use the concept of electrostatic potential energy and kinetic energy.

1. Potential Energy (U): The potential energy of the \(\alpha\)-particle due to the nucleus is given by Coulomb's law:

\[ U = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \cdot \frac{Z_1 Z_2 e^2}{r} \]

where:
       \( Z_1 = 2 \) (charge of the \(\alpha\)-particle)
       \( Z_2 = 29 \) (atomic number of copper)
       \( e = 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \) C (elementary charge)
       \( r = 10^{-12} \) m (distance from the nucleus)
       \( \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} = 9 \times 10^9 \) N m²/C² (Coulomb constant)

Plugging in the values, we get:

\[ U = 9 \times 10^9 \times \frac{2 \times 29 \times (1.6 \times 10^{-19})^2}{10^{-12}} \]

2. Kinetic Energy (K): The kinetic energy of the \(\alpha\)-particle is given by:

\[ K = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 \]

For the \(\alpha\)-particle to arrive at a distance \(10^{-12}\) m from the nucleus, its initial kinetic energy should be equal to the potential energy at that distance (assuming the initial potential energy is zero).

Setting \( K = U \), we have:

\[ \frac{1}{2}mv^2 = U \]

\[ v^2 = \frac{2U}{m} \]

3. Calculation:

First, we calculate the potential energy:

\[ U = 9 \times 10^9 \times \frac{2 \times 29 \times (1.6 \times 10^{-19})^2}{10^{-12}} \]

\[ U = 9 \times 10^9 \times \frac{2 \times 29 \times 2.56 \times 10^{-38}}{10^{-12}} \]

\[ U = 9 \times 10^9 \times \frac{2 \times 29 \times 2.56 \times 10^{-26}}{1} \]

\[ U = 9 \times 10^9 \times 148.48 \times 10^{-26} \]

\[ U = 9 \times 10^9 \times 1.4848 \times 10^{-24} \]

\[ U = 13.3632 \times 10^{-15} \]

\[ U = 1.33632 \times 10^{-14} \text{ J} \]

Finally, using the kinetic energy equation:

\[ v^2 = \frac{2U}{m} \]

The mass of an \(\alpha\)-particle (\(m\)) is approximately \(6.64 \times 10^{-27}\) kg.

\[ v^2 = \frac{2 \times 1.33632 \times 10^{-14}}{6.64 \times 10^{-27}} \]

\[ v^2 = \frac{2.67264 \times 10^{-14}}{6.64 \times 10^{-27}} \]

\[ v^2 = 4.024 \times 10^{12} \]

\[ v = \sqrt{4.024 \times 10^{12}} \]

\[ v \approx 2 \times 10^6 \text{ m/s} \]

Therefore, the correct answer is (b) \(2 \times 10^6 \text{ m/s}\).
