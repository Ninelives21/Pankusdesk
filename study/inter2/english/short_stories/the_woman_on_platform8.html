Short Stories: The Woman on Platform No. 8

By Ruskin Bond

Paragraph Answers

Answer the following in about 100 words each.

  1. Describe the character of Arun, the boarding school boy?

    The story “The Woman on Platform No. 8” was written by Ruskin Bond. He is an Indian author of British descent. He has written several novels, short stories and books for children.

    The story narrates the experiences of a young boy. All the events in the story are seen from Arun’s point of view. Arun is a twelve-year-old boy studying in a Boarding school. He is confident and Independent and is not afraid to travel alone at midnight. He keenly observes people and understands their feelings. Though he is suspicious of the woman in white sari, he notices her sadness. Her soft voice, kindness and concern make him trust her. He relaxes and shares his details with her. He responds to the care and affection showered on him by the lady. He forms an impression of people not by their appearance but by their behaviour. He respects elderly people. Though he hates Satish’s mother, he doesn’t reveal it or be discourteous to her.

    “Appreciate the beauty in simplicity, sincerity and true affection.”

  2. What did Satish’s mother advise him about strangers? How did Arun and Satish react to her advice?

    The story “The Woman on Platform No. 8” was written by Ruskin Bond. He is an Indian author of British descent. He has written several novels, short stories and books for children.

    This is an interesting story about a young twelve year old boy by name Arun studying in a boarding school. The boy is confident and independent and is not afraid to travel alone at midnight. He happened to meet a strange woman at the Railway station. He was suspicious of the woman initially but was comfortable with her after noticing her sadness, soft voice, kindness and concern for him. When Arun was on the platform with the strange woman his friend Satish and his mother arrived at the platform. Satish’s mother was a large, imposing woman. She remarked it was such a bother to wait for the train in the middle of the night and one couldn’t let children wait there alone as anything could happen to a boy at a big station. There were so many dishonest people in the world. She advised them to be careful with strangers. Satish’s mother looked sternly at Arun and advised him not to talk or be friendly with strangers when he was alone. Arun did not like his friend Satish’s mother who clearly thought that his mother and he came from a very poor family.

    “Strangers can become best friends just as easy as best friends can become strangers.”

  3. What made Arun call the strange woman ‘Mother’ at the end?

    The story “The Woman on Platform No. 8” was written by Ruskin Bond. He is an Indian author of British descent. He has written several novels, short stories and books for children.

    This is an interesting story about a young boy. Arun was a twelve year old boy studying in a boarding school. The boy was confident and independent and was not afraid to travel alone at midnight. He met a strange woman at the Railway station. He was suspicious of the woman initially but was comfortable with her after noticing her sadness, soft voice, kindness and concern for him. The strange woman took Arun to a shop and ordered samosas, tea and jalebies. He began to relax and take an interest in the kind woman. When he started talking about his school friends and his likes and dislikes she listened patiently. She was also confident that Arun could travel alone which made Arun feel proud. Arun was impressed with her gentle behaviour and her genuine concern for him, though she seemed poor. He liked the simplicity of her dress, her deep soft voice and the serenity of her face. Moreover in the course of their conversation, the lady told Satish’s mother that she was Arun’s mother. When Arun looked straight into the eyes of the woman she smiled in a gentle, understanding way. All these incidents made Arun call her ‘mother’.

    “Recognise people with pretensions, Pride and arrogance.”

  4. Give any four reasons to explain Why Arun developed instant liking for the strange Woman in a white sari on Platform No. 8.

    The story “The Woman on Platform No. 8” was written by Ruskin Bond. He is an Indian author of British descent. He has written several novels, short stories and books for children.

    This is an interesting story about a young boy. Arun is a twelve-year old boy studying in a boarding school. The boy is confident and Independent and is not afraid to travel alone at midnight. He met a strange woman at the Railway station. He was suspicious of the woman initially but was comfortable with her after noticing her sadness, soft voice, kindness and concern. The strange woman took Arun to a shop and ordered samosas, tea and jalebies. He began to relax and forgot the fact that she was a stranger. When Satish’s mother met the lady she gently said that Arun was her son. There was no sign of embarrassment and she was smiling when she said it. Satish’s mother told Arun that he should be wary of strangers and there were many dishonest people in the world. She even added that children should not wait for the train in the middle of the night. But the strange woman said Arun could travel alone. Arun was grateful to her for saying that. Arun was impressed with her dignity, that commanded respect though she seemed poor. He liked her for the simplicity of her dress, her deepsoft voice and the serenity of her face. While the train was leaving the platform Arun looked straight into the eyes of the woman and the woman smiled in a gentle, understanding way. These are the reasons why Arun developed a liking for her and made him call her ‘mother’.
