By R.K. Narayan

Paragraph Answers

Answer the following in about 100 words each.

  1. Sketch the character of Dr. Raman.

He won many awards like the Padma Bhushan, the Padma Vibhushan, Sahitya Academy Fellowship and A.C. Benson Medal. More importantly, he continues to win the hearts of readers from all over the globe.

    Dr. Raman is the lead character in the story ‘The Doctor’s Word.’ The writer, R.K. Narayan, makes this clear in the title itself. Dr. Raman is an experienced practising medical man. He is an expert in his profession. His patients hold him in high regard. He is more a man of deeds than of words. He maintains a high standard of ethics. He never believes in soothing lies. He is truthful, hardworking and very dedicated and never left any stone unturned to save the lives of his patients. He works hard for hours, days or weeks till he wrests his prize from Yama’s hands. He respects human relations and values friendship. He is an outstanding doctor and a great human being.

People always visited Dr. Raman only when the patient was on his last legs. It was because of his high fee and more importantly they liked to avoid the fact that the time had come to call in Dr. Raman. They also believed that something bad was going to happen for certain. Still, Dr. Raman never thought that it was his business to provide any unnecessary hope when as a matter of course Nature would tell them the truth in a few hours. However, if he glimpsed the faintest sign of hope in his patient, he rolled up his sleeves and stepped into the area of activity. It might be hours, days or weeks, but he never withdrew till he wrested the prize from Yama’s hands.

He won many awards like the Padma Bhushan, the Padma Vibhushan, Sahitya Academy Fellowship and A.C. Benson Medal. More importantly, he continues to win the hearts of readers from all over the globe.

    Dr. Raman is the lead character in the story ‘The Doctor’s Word.’ The writer, R.K. Narayan, makes this clear in the title itself. One morning, Gopal’s son informs Dr. Raman at his clinic that his father is ill. He at once visits Gopal’s house. Gopal lies in bed as if in sleep. We understand from the doctor’s words that Gopal’s condition is rather hopeless. Dr. Raman asks Gopal’s wife why they couldn’t have sent a word to him about his ill health. He swings into action at once. He takes out an injection tube, the needle and injects the drug into Gopal’s body. Even after an hour of doctor’s keen observation, Gopal remains motionless. When Gopal shows a slight improvement, Dr. Raman ignores it by saying it is a false flash-up, very common in such situations. On sensing this, Gopal becomes very anxious and asks the doctor how long he is going to live. He asks thus as he has to sign the will. He can’t leave his property unsettled which will mean endless misery for his wife and children. Reminding Dr. Raman how harmful Subbaiah and his gang are, Gopal asks Dr. Raman to let him sign the will before it is too late.

    The gripping short story ‘The Doctor’s Word’ was penned by R.K. Narayan, the most widely read writer of Indian literature in English. He wrote short stories, novels and essays. He won many awards like the Padma Bhushan, the Padma Vibhushan, Sahitya Academy Fellowship and A.C. Benson Medal. More importantly, he continues to win the hearts of readers from all over the globe.

    Dr. Raman is the lead character in the story ‘The Doctor’s Word.’ The writer, R.K. Narayan, makes this clear in the title itself. Dr. Raman knows pretty well about Gopal’s family affairs. He is well aware how ruthless Subbaiah and his gang are. If Gopal dies without signing the will, it will be a great misery to Gopal’s wife and children. This situation of Gopal’s family reminds him of the catchphrase of the people of Malgudi that he is not a mere doctor expressing an opinion, but a judge pronouncing a verdict. Then he says to himself, “If my word can save his life, he shall not die,” he at once decides to violate his usual practice of revealing the truth and do a piece of acting before a patient. He simulates a feeling and conceals his judgment, so as to save his friend’s life and says to Gopal, “Don’t worry about the will now. You are going to live. Your heart is absolutely sound.” His words have a dramatic effect as Gopal survives the attack. “All is well that ends well”.

    ‘Words have magical power. They can either bring the greatest joy or the greatest grief’
