Short Stories: A Gift for Christmas

By O. Henry

Paragraph Answers

Answer the following in about 100 words each.

  1. “Love, Sacrifice and generosity are the essential elements for happy living.” Explain this statement with reference to the story A Gift for Christmas.

    O. Henry is an American short-story writer known for his wit, humour and story telling technique. O. Henry’s works include the famous story “The Ransom of Red Chief.” The story ‘A Gift for Christmas’ is taken from O. Henry’s collection, ‘The Four Million.’

    The theme of the story is that the greatest gift of all is unconditional love. The story highlights the valuable possessions of Mankind. The story narrates the tale of a young couple. Jim and Della long to give each other meaningful Christmas presents. The couple is constrained by their meagre budget. Della decides to have her hair cut and sold. She buys a platinum watch-chain for Jim. And Jim sells his gold watch to buy expensive combs for Della. Thus both of them show the true meaning of love, sacrifice and generosity by giving up their best possessions.

    “The value of a gift is not in its price, but rather in its sentiment.”

  2. Analyse the character of Della.

    O. Henry is an American short-story writer known for his wit, humour and story telling technique. O. Henry’s works include the famous story ‘The Ransom of Red Chief.’ The story ‘A Gift for Christmas’ is taken from O. Henry’s collection ‘The Four Million.’

    The theme of the story ‘A Gift for Christmas’ is that the greatest gift of all is unconditional love. The story highlights the valuable possessions of mankind like love, sacrifice and generosity. The story narrates the tale of a young couple. Jim and Della long to give each other meaningful Christmas presents. The couple is constrained by their meagre budget. Della sells her beautiful long hair in order to buy a platinum watch chain for Jim’s antique gold watch and Jim sells his watch to buy Della a set of combs. Henry brings out the importance of certain virtuous human characteristics. In the beginning of the story, Della could do nothing except to throw herself on the dirty little couch and cry. But immediately behaves like a mature woman having decided to sell her long, flowing hair to buy Jim a present. When Jim entered the house, Della, like a loving wife longed for his attention as she was scared of his anger. When Della opened the package brought by Jim, we could visualize the scream of joy followed by a quick feminine change to tears. She can adjust herself easily according to circumstances. A wonderful character indeed is Della!

    “Great achievement is usually born of great sacrifice and is never the result of selfishness.”

  3. Sketch the Character of Jim.

    O. Henry is an American short-story writer known for his wit, humour and story telling technique. O. Henry’s works include the famous story ‘The Ransom of Red Chief.’ The story ‘A Gift for Christmas’ is taken form O. Henry’s collection ‘The Four Million.’

    The theme of the story is that the greatest gift of all is unconditional love. The story narrates the tale of a young couple, Jim and his wife Della. Jim looked thin and very serious. He was only twenty-two – too young to shoulder the responsibility of a family. The couple long to give each other meaningful Christmas presents. But they constrained by their meagre budget. Jim’s gold watch and Della’s long beautiful hair are what they consider their treasures. Della sold her long flowing hair and bought a platinum watch chain for him. Jim was also facing the same financial crunch. He could not decide what to buy for Della. Finally, he decided to buy a set of combs, with such turmoil in mind, he atlast decided to buy a set of combs. The set of combs, that Della had seen in a Broadway window and liked so much. Jim was loving and wise enough to buy the set of combs as a gift for Della. Poor man! Though he was in need of a new overcoat and a pair of gloves, he decided to buy the combs by selling his Gold watch illustrates his love for Della.

    “Giving is better than receiving but giving and losing is all that matters.”

  4. ‘A Gift for Christmas’ is an execute of O. Henry’s Comic irony. Justify.

    O. Henry is an American short-story writer known for his wit, humour and story telling technique. O. Henry’s works include the famous story ‘The Ransom of Red Chief.’ The story ‘A Gift for Christmas’ is taken from O. Henry’s collection ‘The Four Million’.

    The theme of the story is that the greatest gift of all is unconditional love. The story highlights the valuable possessions of Mankind. The story tells the tale of a young couple. Jim and Della long to give each other meaningful Christmas presents. The irony which Henry presents in his short story teaches the reader about sacrifice and true love. Henry uses verbal irony after revealing that Della had only $1.87 for gifts. Della could only flop down on the shabby little couch and weep bitterly. This indicates that life is made up of sobs, sniffles and smiles. A situation in the story is made humorous or suspense filled when the author makes the reader know more about it than a character. This is what is called dramatic irony. The situation is ironic for Della because she wanted a pair of tortoise shell combs, but when she was gifted the same she had no long hair to style it into curls. In the same way, when Jim received a platinum watch chain for his watch, there is no watch at all! Jim and Della who are a ‘made for each other couple’ exhibit unconditional love through their praise worthy actions.

    “There is nothing that love cannot achieve, and there is nothing that love cannot sacrifice.”
