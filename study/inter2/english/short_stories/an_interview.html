Short Stories: An Interview

By Richard Gordon

Paragraph Answers

Answer the following in about 100 words each.

  1. The narrator thought that his interview was superfluous. Why?

    Richard Gordon was an English surgeon. He wrote numerous novels and screen plays for television and films. His ‘Doctor Novels’ - eighteen comic works in a series were very successful. The short story “An Interview” is witty and apparently autobiographical. It discusses the narrator’s experience of facing an interview with the Dean of a medical school. The style is racy and simple.

    The narrator came to attend an interview with Dr. Lionel Loftus, the dean of St Swithin’s medical school. He sat nervously in the waiting area. He was then encountered by an old man, the secretary of the medical school who scanned him carefully and asked a few questions. Unlike what was expected, the Dean also didn’t ask any questions related to medicine. He asked only about the narrator’s rugby experience, with which the Dean seemed impressed. As a result, the narrator got admitted into St Swithin’s. Later, he came to know all that was unnecessary. The Dean generally admitted students whom the secretary approved of and rejected those whose looks the secretary didn’t like.

    Mr. Richard Gordon went to meet the Dean after an encounter with the secretary. The narrator was worried and tensed about his admission. Mr. Lionel Loftus, the secretary had given an excuse for keeping the narrator waiting and offered him a seat. Now, The Dean began asking the narrator questions about whether he was from a public school, whether he could afford the fee, etc. He also asked whether the narrator played Rugby and his position in the game. The Dean was interested in the candidate’s position at three quarter in Rugby as the hospital was full of forwards. It looked as if the Dean was impressed with the young and energetic narrator who could do his duties well if given a chance.

    The narrator wanted to meet the Dean, Dr. Lionel Loftus. So, he went to the office, sat in the waiting room, worried about his admission. After some time, an old man, so thin, so pale, entered the room slowly and informed Mr. Gordon that he was not the dean but the secretary of the medical college. He said that he had seen thousands of students pass through the school where some of them turned out good and some of them bad. He also said that he had seen the Dean himself come up for admission. The secretary put a few questions to the narrator as though he wanted to test his abilities. He asked if he was from a public school, played rugby, etc. Towards the end of the story, we understand that it is the secretary who decided whether a candidate is to be admitted or rejected.

  4. “His face suddenly lightened……..” Do you think the Dean was really happy with the narrator? Why?

    Richard Gordon was an English surgeon. He wrote numerous novels and screen plays for television and films. His ‘Doctor Novels’- eighteen comic works in a series were very successful. The short story “An Interview” is witty and apparently autobiographical. It discusses the narrator’s experience of facing an interview with the Dean of a medical school. The style is racy and simple.

    The Dean interviewed the narrator by asking him about his school, whether he could afford the fee, and his position in the Rugby team, etc. The narrator was puzzled because neither the secretary nor the Dean asked him any question related to medicine. The Dean seemed to be satisfied by the answers given by the narrator. He took pleasure in giving admission to Mr. Gordon at St. Swithins and congratulated him. The narrator wondered how he had been selected based on the questions asked. Later, he found out that the interview was superfluous since the Dean relied entirely on the opinion of his secretary and rejected the candidates whose looks the secretary disliked.
