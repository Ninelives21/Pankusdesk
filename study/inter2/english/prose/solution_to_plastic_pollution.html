By Dr. Rajagopalan Vasudevan

Question and Answers

Answer the following questions in about 100 words each.

  1. Describe the deportment of Dr. Vasudevan as the Professor of TCE.

    Dr. Rajagopalan Vasudevan was born in Tamil Nadu. After receiving his doctorate in Science in 1974, he joined TCE as a member of the faculty. From then on, he dedicated his research to the management of plastic waste. “Solution to Plastic Pollution” is about his research project. Dr. Vasudevan was a Professor at Thiagarajar College of Engineering. Though he was a Professor of Chemistry, his deportment seemed like that of a Sanskrit Pandit. He is popularly known as Madurai’s “Plastic Road Man”.

    Always sporting a striking namam on his forehead, he was as much at ease when he experimented with chemicals in the science lab as when he held beakers over flames under a peepul tree while talking with a group of students at lunch break, teaching them shlokas from the Bhagavad Gita. He could easily be mistaken for a Sanskrit pandit if you heard him quote the holy text as a solution to any problem in the world. Mesmerized students listened to him in rapt attention. He considered the Gita a manual for leading life and voluntarily conducted classes uninterrupted for 36 years. He maintained a low profile. He was a man with a mission and “Clean India” was his campaign.

    Dr. Vasudevan developed a very simple but effective technology. He transformed plastic waste into a material that replaced a small portion of bitumen used in road construction. Garbage dumps are the inexhaustible sources for this innovation. Dr. Vasudevan tells us how he adds value to waste by using it in laying all-weather roads. After the city’s rain-fed potholes transitioned to smooth roads within the campus of TCE, it was indeed a treat. No wonder he is known as Madurai’s “Plastic Road Man”. His interest in the subject began when he heard an erroneous remark that plastic dissolved in water. He started mixing some waste plastic in heated bitumen, and the rest is history. His innovation serves the twin purpose of increasing road quality and solving the problem of plastic disposal.

He is popularly known as Madurai’s “Plastic Road Man”.

    Dr. Rajagopalan Vasudevan’s innovation has many advantages. To lay one kilometer of plastic road 3.75 meters wide, one tonne of bitumen and one tonne of waste plastic are required. A normal road requires ten tonnes of bitumen for each kilometer. Hence, a plastic road reduces the cost involved and saves one tonne of bitumen for every kilometer laid. Any form of plastic waste can be shredded and used.

    The other benefits are zero maintenance, simple technology, spot use of plastic waste, and waterproof roads that can last up to 100 years. They also have higher load-carrying capacity, double the road strength, and a new surface without cracks and potholes. The list is endless. Hats off to this simple man who understood how and where his knowledge could be implemented.
