Prose: Guilty (One-Act Play)

By Horace Gardiner and Bonneviere Arnaud

Question and Answers

Answer the following questions in 100 words each.

  1. Describe the importance of Mrs. Moore’s role in ‘Guilty’.

    The delightful one-act play, ‘Guilty’ authored by Horace Gardiner and Bonneviere Arnaud, is didactic and imparts moral instruction indirectly. People consider Horace and Arnaud as a single author for the apparent reason of the non-availability of any literary work by them individually. Horace Gardiner is a well known essayist, literary critic and a university professor while Bonneviere Arnaud had 176 library holdings to his credit.

    The play ‘Guilty’ is a fascinating one-act play which is humorous as well as expressive. Mrs. Moore contributes a good part to this didactic element. As such her part in the play is significant. She is the first to appear in the play, symbolizing her importance. She talks about dignity of labour forcefully. She also emphasizes the fact that all kinds of jobs need good employees. Mrs. Moore stresses the need on the part of the employees to be loyal to their employers. She, however, has her own share of flaws. Her comment that she is glad that she has no sons, speaks of her bad taste. She pretends to know everything and seems to sympathies with others. Thus, she serves as a model – “How Not To Be”. Her report about the robbery sets the tone of the play and carries it forward in the right direction.

  2. Ma Aryan puts what she thinks is two and two together and makes four out of it. Discuss the developments that lead (or, rather mislead) Ma Ryan into making this mistake.

    The delightful one-act play, ‘Guilty’ authored by Horace Gardiner and Bonneviere Arnaud, is didactic and imparts moral instruction indirectly. People consider Horace and Arnaud as a single author for the apparent reason of the non-availability of any literary work by them individually. Horace Gardiner is a well known essayist, literary critic and a university professor while Bonneviere Arnaud had 176 library holdings to his credit.

    Ma Ryan is the mother of the lead character in the play, Jim Ryan. Jim works as a night watchman at the Van King Warehouse though he is an engineering graduate for want of a better job. One morning, soon after Jim leaves the warehouse, a robbery takes place in which a diamond pendant is stolen from the office safe of the warehouse. In a dramatic turn of events he finds it at a lunch car on his way home and carries it in the pocket of his sweater. Ma Ryan who finds it in his pocket, puts two and two together and makes four out of it i.e. she takes it for granted that her son must have stolen the article from the warehouse. But as the story progresses it dawns on her that her son, in fact, found it at a lunch car on his way home and he has decided to give it back to its rightful owner. Thus, Ma Ryan’s putting two and two together and making four out of it misleads her by making her suspect her son.

  3. Sketch the character of Jim in the light of Ma Ryan’s comments like: It is the same! But how did it get into Jim’s pocket?

    The delightful one-act play ‘Guilty’ is humorous and didactic to the core. Ma Ryan is the mother of the lead character, Jim in the play. Jim works as a night watchman at the Van King Warehouse though he is an engineering graduate, for want of a better job. One evening their neighbor Mrs. Moore shows her a newspaper report about the robbery of a diamond pendant at the Van King’s Warehouse along with its picture. At the same time Mrs. Ryan happens to find the same, the very same jewel in Jim’s pocket. Without a second thought Ma Ryan puts two and two together and makes four out of it i.e. she misunderstands her son to be a thief. But the fact remains that Jim finds it at a lunch car on his way home and carries home the same putting it in the pocket of his sweater. Thus Jim is proved to be innocent and loyal to his employer, and is rightly rewarded for his honesty.

    The delightful one-act play ‘Guilty’ is humorous and didactic to the core. Ma Ryan is the mother of Jim Ryan, the lead character in the play. Jim works as a night watchman at the Van King Warehouse though he is an engineering graduate, for want of a better job. One day he finds a diamond pendant at a lunch car on his way home. On learning about the robbery at the warehouse he was working for Jim predicts that it might be the same jewel robbed at the warehouse. So he invites Mr. Van King, its owner to his house to hand it over to him, if it happens to be the same article. But by then Jim’s mother finds it in the pocket of his sweater. It looks the same as she has seen in the newspaper. So she reaches the conclusion that Jim must have been involved in the theft of the diamond and instructs her son to hand it over to Mr. Van King, its owner. At the same time, she warns her son to escape or the police will put him in jail. Jim is puzzled at her odd behaviour and concludes that she is talking like someone in a fog without any sense. These words spoken by Jim are not to be taken seriously as his mother is not aware of the reality of the situation.
