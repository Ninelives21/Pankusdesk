    Russell says that there are several ways to avoid being dogmatic. The first way is making a keen observation so as to get rid of the bias. Hence, to know what other people think, one has to be aware of what they think. This can be done by going on a vacation and talking to people with different ideas. If we cannot travel, we have to seek out people with whom we disagree or read a newspaper with a contrary opinion. The third is arguing with an imaginary character who has a different point of view. The fourth one is to deal with one’s sense of self-worth, which is the hardest. To overcome conceit, we must remember that we live for a short while on a small planet in a vast cosmos.

  2. Travel is an excellent educator. Explain with reference to Russell’s essay “How to Avoid Foolish Opinions.”

    The essay “How to Avoid Foolish Opinions” was written by Bertrand Russell. He was a multifaceted personality. He was an advocate for reason and rational attitude.

    According to Russell, travelling is profitable in diminishing the narrow-minded viewpoint. He cautions the readers that we may remain biased in our opinion if we are unaware of different opinions. Russell says when he was young, he travelled to different countries like France, Germany, Italy, and the United States. He found it profitable to reduce his narrow-minded view. He advises us, if we cannot travel, we have to seek out people with whom we disagree and read a newspaper that belongs to our opponents. If the people and the newspaper seem mad or wicked, remind yourself that you seem so to them. He opines that both parties may be right, but both cannot be wrong.

    “Avoiding stupidity is easier than seeking brilliance.”

  3. According to Russell, what is the only way to tackle self-pride?

    The essay “How to Avoid Foolish Opinions” was written by Bertrand Russell. He was a multifaceted personality. He was an advocate for reason and rational attitude.

    Russell warns us to be wary of opinions that flatter our self confidence. We see both men and women who are firmly convinced of the superiority of their own gender. If you are a man, you point out that most poets and men of science are male. If you are a woman, you retort to say that most men are criminals. We also set a standard of values for our own nation to prove it is superior to other nations. The question is insoluble. But our self-pride conceals this fact from most people. It is more difficult to deal with the self-esteem of man as a man because one cannot argue out the matter with some non-human mind. To overcome this human conceit, we must remember we live for a short time on a small planet in a vast cosmos.

    “A little foolishness, enough to enjoy life, and a little wisdom to avoid the errors, that will do.”

  4. What does Bertrand Russell say about a person getting angry about a difference of opinion?

    The essay “How to Avoid Foolish Opinions” was written by Bertrand Russell, an English writer. This is a thought-provoking essay which reveals intense human interest.

    Russell admits that if an opinion contrary to your own makes you angry, it means you are subconsciously aware of having no good reason for thinking as you do. If someone says that two and two are five or that Iceland is on the equator, you feel pity rather than anger unless you know so little of Arithmetic or Geography that his opinion shakes your own contrary conviction. The most prevalent controversies are those about matters where there is no good evidence either way. Hence, Russell advises us to be on our guard when we come across a difference of opinion because, on examination, we find that our belief is going beyond evidence.
