Poetry: Awake

By Sarojini Naidu

Question and Answers

Answer the following in about 100 words each.

  1. Substantiate the critical comment that the poem Awake is a patriotic lyric.

    The poem ‘Awake’ is written by Sarojini Naidu. She is fondly called the ‘Nightingale of India’. She was a child prodigy. She started writing poems in English when she was just twelve. As a writer, she was both prolific and profound. She was the first woman to be the Governor of a state in independent India. The poem is a patriotic song. It is a plea for action and unity.

    Yes, Sarojini Naidu’s song ‘Awake’ has been acclaimed as a patriotic lyric. The word ‘lyric’ comes from the Greek expression lyre – a musical instrument. A lyric is a poem in which a single speaker expresses emotions and thoughts. It is noteworthy for its musical quality and rhythm. And its theme is lofty. The poem ‘Awake’ has all these qualities in abundance. It is rich in musical element. It expresses strong nationalistic feelings. Thus, the content is patriotic and hence noble. With its profound theme and artistic and lyrical form, the poem is entitled to be applauded as a patriotic lyric.

  2. How do the children implore the Mother in the poem ‘Awake’?

    The poem ‘Awake’ is written by Sarojini Naidu. She is fondly called the ‘Nightingale of India’. She was a child prodigy. She started writing poems in English when she was just twelve. As a writer, she was both prolific and profound. She was the first woman to be the Governor of a state in independent India. The poem is a patriotic song. It is a plea for action and unity.

    Nation is personified as our mother. All Indians become her children. They implore the mother to wake up from the shackles of slavery and bondage of sorrow. They assure her that the dream of freedom will become a reality. Hence she should break the woes of bondage. They beg her to help them serve her and worship her. They want her to bless them so that their holy cause may triumph. They have inherited her moral and spiritual courage. They desire to restore her past splendour. Such is the dedication of her children and they want her to respond to their call.

  3. How do Indians plan to set their mother again in the forefront of glory?

    The poem ‘Awake’ is written by Sarojini Naidu. She is fondly called the ‘Nightingale of India’. She was a child prodigy. She started writing poems in English when she was just twelve. As a writer, she was both prolific and profound. She was the first woman to be the Governor of a state in independent India. The poem is a patriotic song. It is a plea for action and unity.

    The children of mother India are devoted to her. They are ever ready to serve her. First, they want her to break the chains of slavery. They have inherited her pride, her moral and spiritual courage. They pledge to safeguard her and they will not abandon her come what may. Through their united efforts, they would make her glorious. The followers of different religions would come together and their devoted efforts shall be sufficient to emancipate her from the present subjugation. They affirm that their mother is their queen and goddess and as true servants they would unitedly celebrate her victory.

  4. What do the children of all creeds promise their mother separately and collectively?

    The poem ‘Awake’ is written by Sarojini Naidu. She is fondly called the ‘Nightingale of India’. She was a child prodigy. She started writing poems in English when she was just twelve. As a writer, she was both prolific and profound. She was the first woman to be the Governor of a state in independent India. The poem is a patriotic song. It is a plea for action and unity.

    People of all faith pledge to come together and reinstate the pride and power India once basked in. The Hindus swear that they will worship her by offering flowers and safeguard her pride. The Parsees promise that the fire of hope which burns in their hearts will be dedicated to her service. The Mussalmans declare that they would defend her like the sword of the love that is used to protect their religion. The Christians promise that they will have the same faith in her as they have in their religion. People of all creeds shall unite to raise aloft the glory and honour of their mother, their queen and goddess and see that it is never soiled. The poem is a soul-stirring appeal to all Indians to keep aside religious differences and work unitedly for a common cause.
