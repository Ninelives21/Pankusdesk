    “Goodbye Party for Miss Pushpa T.S” is a satirical poem by Nissim Ezekiel. He satirizes the way in which Indians speak English through this poem. The title of the poem says that it is about a farewell party. The speaker announces in the very beginning that the purpose of that meeting is to bid farewell to Miss Pushpa. They all want to wish her a pleasant journey. The speaker delivers a formal farewell speech. He praises Miss Pushpa’s nature. He refers to her helpful qualities. He appreciates her concern for friends. She is beautiful not only because of her charms but also her honesty. She always has a beautiful smile on her face. The poet keeps praising Miss Pushpa’s good and amicable nature. He invites other friends to speak about Miss Pushpa. Thus, the poem has all the qualities typical of a farewell address.
