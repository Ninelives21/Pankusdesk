Poetry: On the Grasshopper and Cricket

By John Keats

Question and Answers

Answer the following in about 100 words each.

  1. What is the theme of the poem “On the Grasshopper and Cricket”?

    John Keats, a Romantic poet, is the author of the poem “On the Grasshopper and Cricket” in which he celebrates the music of the earth and the ever-present symphony of nature. This poem with 14 lines is called a sonnet. The first part (octave) talks about the Grasshopper and the second part of six lines is devoted to the Cricket.

    Nature is the central focus of the poem. The poet says that the poetry of the earth never ceases. The Grasshopper and Cricket are used as symbols. Seasons may come and go, but nature never fails to inspire us with its songs. When birds stop singing in extreme heat, the earth is filled with the songs of the Grasshopper. He sings endlessly, but when tired, rests under some pleasant weed. During winter, birds stop singing. The silence can be frightening. Frost spreads its blanket over nature. Invariably, a shrill sound comes from beneath the stones, and it is the Cricket singing. The Cricket’s song restores warmth.

    John Keats, a Romantic poet, is the author of the poem “On the Grasshopper and Cricket” in which he celebrates the music of the earth and the ever-present symphony of nature. This poem with 14 lines is called a sonnet. The first part (octave) talks about the Grasshopper and the second part of six lines is devoted to the Cricket.

She is a spontaneous source of optimism and happiness. Both the Grasshopper and the Cricket are the representative voices of nature’s music. Both offer a soothing effect during the extremities of climate. The Grasshopper’s song balances the extreme heat of summer and Cricket’s song is both comforting and pleasing during winter. Both can produce a characteristic, loud, and chirping sound. Through a joyous and luxurious setting, the poem celebrates all phases of nature.
