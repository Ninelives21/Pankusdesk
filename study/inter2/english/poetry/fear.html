Fear | English Senior IPE

Poetry: Fear

Question and Answers

Answer the following in about 100 words each.

  1. What is the central idea of the poem ‘Fear’?

    The poem ‘Fear’ is written by Kahlil Gibran. The philosophical poem portrays the universal fear that everyone faces in life. The poet shares his profound wisdom about overcoming ‘Fear’ through the point of view of a river.

    The starting line itself introduces the central theme of the poem ‘Fear’. The river’s fear of merging in the ocean refers to a number of human emotions. The fear of changing consciousness, the terror of moving forward and the anxiety of losing oneself. The poem ‘Fear’ gives us a glimpse of how a river feels when it travels through mountains and plains to merge with an ocean. The poet personifies the river as a woman to infuse life. There is always a desire to ‘go back’ which is impossible in existence. Finally, Gibran concludes the poem with a strong message that one should accept the fact that there is no other option except to move forward — overcoming fear.

  2. What does ‘nobody can go back’ mean in the poem ‘Fear’? Explain from your point of view.

    The poem ‘Fear’ is written by Kahlil Gibran. The philosophical poem portrays the universal fear that everyone faces in life. The poet shares his profound wisdom about overcoming ‘Fear’ through the point of view of a river.

    The river trembles with fear at the sight of the vast ocean. She tries to flow back in the path she has travelled. But she realizes at last that going back is impossible and moving ahead is inevitable. Human beings fear moving forward at some point in life and try to ‘go back’ escaping from the adverse circumstances. The fear of changing consciousness, the terror of moving forward and the anxiety of losing oneself are the reasons which make them ‘go back’. They learn that going back is impossible and moving ahead is inevitable in the existence of life. So, all of us have to take the risk of facing challenging situations to overcome the various hurdles in life.

  3. How can one overcome fear? Explain.

    The poem ‘Fear’ is written by Kahlil Gibran. The philosophical poem portrays the universal fear that everyone faces in life. The poet shares his profound wisdom about overcoming ‘Fear’ through the point of view of a river.

    Everyone meets fear at some point in life. It may be the fear of moving forward, the terror of losing our personal identity or our personal belongings, being forgotten in this universe, death, change, and so on. So, in order to escape from the clutches of fear, one tries to traverse in the path of the past. But going back is impossible and moving ahead is inevitable. In this process of escaping from fear they learn the reality; the reality of facing the risk and overcoming fear. One has to face the ups and downs in life, thereby converting fear into our greatest strength to achieve success in life.

  4. What does the line “the river needs to take the risk of entering the ocean” mean? Discuss.

    The poem ‘Fear’ is written by Kahlil Gibran. The philosophical poem portrays the universal fear that everyone faces in life. The poet shares his profound wisdom about overcoming ‘Fear’ through the point of view of a river.

    Gibran refers to the river as ‘she’ to infuse life into the river. The river is beautifully personified adopting many human traits. The river trembles with fear at the sight of a vast ocean after traversing many difficult paths in her life. She tries to flow back in order to escape from entering the ocean. But going back and dwelling in the past again is impossible in the existence of everyone’s life. In a similar way, moving ahead is inevitable. Finally, after a great inner conflict, she realizes that “She needs to take the risk of entering the ocean.” She understands that it is not the risk of losing her identity; instead, it is the truth of becoming a part of something great and vast.
