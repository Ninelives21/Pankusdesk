    • BITSAT chapter / topic / subtopic: Gravitation → Gravitational Potential Energy - Page P84
      Gravitation → Gravity - Page P82
    • The body starts from rest, so initial kinetic energy is zero.
    • The height above Earth's surface is \(R\), and Earth's radius is also \(R\). Therefore, the initial distance from Earth's centre is: \(R+R=2R\).
    • At the surface of Earth, the distance from Earth's centre is: \(R\).
    • Gravitational potential energy at distance \(r\) from Earth's centre is: \(U=-\dfrac{GMm}{r}\).
    • Initial potential energy: \(U_i=-\dfrac{GMm}{2R}\).
    • Final potential energy: \(U_f=-\dfrac{GMm}{R}\).
    • Decrease in potential energy: \(U_i-U_f=-\dfrac{GMm}{2R}-\left(-\dfrac{GMm}{R}\right)\).
    • So: \(U_i-U_f=\dfrac{GMm}{2R}\).
    • This decrease in potential energy becomes kinetic energy: \(\dfrac{1}{2}mv^2=\dfrac{GMm}{2R}\).
    • Cancelling \(\dfrac{m}{2}\): \(v^2=\dfrac{GM}{R}\).
    • Since \(g=\dfrac{GM}{R^2}\), we have \(GM=gR^2\).
    • Therefore: \(v^2=\dfrac{gR^2}{R}=gR\).
    • Hence: \(v=\sqrt{gR}\).
    • Final result: Velocity on reaching Earth's surface \(=\sqrt{gR}\).
    • Simple memory tip: For large heights comparable to Earth's radius, do not use constant-\(g\) formula directly. Use gravitational potential energy \(-\dfrac{GMm}{r}\).
    • Why the other options are wrong: \(\sqrt{2gR}\), \(\sqrt{\dfrac{3}{2}gR}\), and \(\sqrt{4gR}\) do not come from the correct gravitational potential energy difference between \(2R\) and \(R\).
    • Exam shortcut: If height is comparable to Earth's radius, use energy conservation with centre-distance \(r\), not \(mgh\).
