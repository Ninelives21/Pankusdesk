  1. Chemical kinetics: scope of chapter (01:49)
    (Back to topic index)
    • Chemical kinetics (physical chemistry) deals with three core parts:
      • Rate of reaction (ROR)
      • Factors affecting rate (temperature, concentration, pressure, etc.)
      • Mechanism of reaction (stepwise pathway)
    • Chapter roadmap (high-level): ROR → factors (Arrhenius equation, order) → rate law → molecularity → mechanism.
  2. Rate of reaction: definition (05:47)
    (Back to topic index)
    • Rate of reaction measures how fast a reaction proceeds via concentration change with time.
      \( \text{Rate} \propto \dfrac{\Delta(\text{concentration})}{\Delta t} \)
    • Practical idea:
      • Reactants: concentration decreases with time.
      • Products: concentration increases with time.
  3. Very fast vs slow vs moderate (07:10)
    (Back to topic index)
    • Very fast reactions (often ionic) are too fast to measure practically.
      • Example (precipitation): NaCl + AgNO\(_3\) → AgCl(s) + NaNO\(_3\)
      • Occurs in microseconds; practical rate measurement is not feasible.
    • Very slow reactions (take months/years) have little practical use for kinetics calculations.
      • Example: rusting of iron (very slow).
    • Moderate-rate (often molecular) reactions are the main focus of kinetics.
      • Example (Haber-type): N\(_2\) + 3H\(_2\) → 2NH\(_3\) (bond breaking + bond forming takes finite time)
  4. Average rate: stoichiometry rule (12:48)
    (Back to topic index)
    • For a general reaction:
      \( aA + bB \rightarrow cC + dD \)
    • Average rate of reaction is defined consistently using stoichiometric coefficients:
      \( \text{Rate} = -\dfrac{1}{a}\dfrac{\Delta[A]}{\Delta t} = -\dfrac{1}{b}\dfrac{\Delta[B]}{\Delta t} = +\dfrac{1}{c}\dfrac{\Delta[C]}{\Delta t} = +\dfrac{1}{d}\dfrac{\Delta[D]}{\Delta t} \)
    • Sign rule:
      • Reactants: negative sign (since \(\Delta[\text{reactant}]<0\)).
      • Products: positive sign (since \(\Delta[\text{product}]>0\)).
    • Key rule: always divide by the stoichiometric coefficient to get a single consistent rate value.
  5. Rate of appearance vs disappearance (16:43)
    (Back to topic index)
    • Rate of appearance (ROA) of a product \(P\):
      \( \text{ROA of }P = \left|\dfrac{\Delta[P]}{\Delta t}\right| \)
    • Rate of disappearance (ROD) of a reactant \(R\):
      \( \text{ROD of }R = \left|\dfrac{\Delta[R]}{\Delta t}\right| \)
    • Important distinction:
      • ROA/ROD are magnitudes (reported positive by definition).
      • No stoichiometric division rule is forced for ROA/ROD (it is simply the species’ own concentration-change rate).
  6. Units of rate of reaction (19:22)
    (Back to topic index)
    • Since \( \text{Rate} \sim \dfrac{\text{concentration}}{\text{time}} \), common unit:
      \( \text{mol L}^{-1}\text{ s}^{-1} \)
    • For gaseous systems, concentration may be expressed via partial pressure, so rate unit can be:
      \( \text{pressure per time} \) (e.g., Pa s\(^{-1}\)).
  7. Practice: average rate + ROA/ROD (21:22)
    (Back to topic index)
    • Practice set (answers are shown immediately after each question).
      1. Question 1
        For the reaction (reconstructed from coefficients stated):
        \( 4\text{NH}_3 + 5\text{O}_2 \rightarrow 4\text{NO} + 6\text{H}_2\text{O} \)
        Write the average rate expression in terms of NH\(_3\), O\(_2\), NO, H\(_2\)O.
        • \( \text{Rate} = -\dfrac{1}{4}\dfrac{\Delta[\text{NH}_3]}{\Delta t} = -\dfrac{1}{5}\dfrac{\Delta[\text{O}_2]}{\Delta t} = +\dfrac{1}{4}\dfrac{\Delta[\text{NO}]}{\Delta t} = +\dfrac{1}{6}\dfrac{\Delta[\text{H}_2\text{O}]}{\Delta t} \)
        • \( \text{ROA of NO} = \left|\dfrac{\Delta[\text{NO}]}{\Delta t}\right| \), \( \text{ROD of O}_2 = \left|\dfrac{\Delta[\text{O}_2]}{\Delta t}\right| \)

      2. Question 2
        \( \text{N}_2 + 3\text{H}_2 \rightarrow 2\text{NH}_3 \)
        Given: 2 mol of N\(_2\) disappear in 1 min, vessel volume = 1 L. Find:
        (i) Rate of reaction, (ii) ROD of H\(_2\), (iii) ROA of NH\(_3\).
        • Concentration change for N\(_2\): \( \left|\dfrac{\Delta[\text{N}_2]}{\Delta t}\right| = \dfrac{2}{60} = \dfrac{1}{30}\ \text{mol L}^{-1}\text{ s}^{-1} \)
        • Rate of reaction: \( \text{Rate} = \left|\dfrac{\Delta[\text{N}_2]}{\Delta t}\right| = \dfrac{1}{30}\ \text{mol L}^{-1}\text{ s}^{-1} \)
        • From stoichiometry: \( \text{Rate} = \dfrac{1}{3}\left|\dfrac{\Delta[\text{H}_2]}{\Delta t}\right| \Rightarrow \text{ROD of H}_2 = 3\times\dfrac{1}{30} = \dfrac{1}{10}\ \text{mol L}^{-1}\text{ s}^{-1} \)
        • \( \text{Rate} = \dfrac{1}{2}\left|\dfrac{\Delta[\text{NH}_3]}{\Delta t}\right| \Rightarrow \text{ROA of NH}_3 = 2\times\dfrac{1}{30} = \dfrac{1}{15}\ \text{mol L}^{-1}\text{ s}^{-1} \)

      3. Question 3
        \( 2\text{N}_2\text{O}_5 \rightarrow 4\text{NO}_2 + \text{O}_2 \)
        Given: ROA of NO\(_2\) = 2 mol L\(^{-1}\) s\(^{-1}\). Find:
        (i) Rate of reaction, (ii) ROD of N\(_2\)O\(_5\).
        • \( \text{Rate} = \dfrac{1}{4}\dfrac{d[\text{NO}_2]}{dt} = \dfrac{1}{4}\times 2 = \dfrac{1}{2}\ \text{mol L}^{-1}\text{ s}^{-1} \)
        • \( \text{Rate} = \dfrac{1}{2}\left|\dfrac{d[\text{N}_2\text{O}_5]}{dt}\right| \Rightarrow \text{ROD of N}_2\text{O}_5 = 2\times\dfrac{1}{2} = 1\ \text{mol L}^{-1}\text{ s}^{-1} \)
  8. Instantaneous rate + slope idea (33:46)
    (Back to topic index)
    • Instantaneous rate is the rate at a particular instant (very small time interval).
      Replace \(\Delta\) by \(d\): \( \Delta t \to dt \)
    • For \( 2A + 3B \rightarrow 4C \):
      \( \text{Rate} = -\dfrac{1}{2}\dfrac{d[A]}{dt} = -\dfrac{1}{3}\dfrac{d[B]}{dt} = +\dfrac{1}{4}\dfrac{d[C]}{dt} \)
    • Graph connection:
      • \( \dfrac{d(\text{concentration})}{dt} \) is the slope of the tangent at that time.
      • For reactants, slope is typically negative (decreasing with time); for products, slope positive (increasing).
  9. Practice: rate from slope (39:18)
    (Back to topic index)
    • Practice set (rate from slope or given angle).
      1. Question 4
        \( \text{N}_2 + 3\text{H}_2 \rightarrow 2\text{NH}_3 \)
        From the H\(_2\) concentration–time graph between \((t=2\,\text{s},\,10)\) and \((t=4\,\text{s},\,6)\), find:
        (i) rate of reaction, (ii) rate of disappearance of N\(_2\), (iii) rate of appearance of NH\(_3\).
        • Slope: \( \dfrac{d[\text{H}_2]}{dt} = \dfrac{6-10}{4-2} = -2\ \text{mol L}^{-1}\text{ s}^{-1} \)
        • \( \text{Rate} = -\dfrac{1}{3}\dfrac{d[\text{H}_2]}{dt} = -\dfrac{1}{3}(-2) = \dfrac{2}{3}\ \text{mol L}^{-1}\text{ s}^{-1} \)
        • \( \text{ROD of N}_2 = \left|\dfrac{d[\text{N}_2]}{dt}\right| = 1\times \text{Rate} = \dfrac{2}{3}\ \text{mol L}^{-1}\text{ s}^{-1} \)
        • \( \text{ROA of NH}_3 = \left|\dfrac{d[\text{NH}_3]}{dt}\right| = 2\times \text{Rate} = \dfrac{4}{3}\ \text{mol L}^{-1}\text{ s}^{-1} \)

      2. Question 5
        \( 2P + 3Q \rightarrow R \)
        Given: Q vs time graph is a straight line and tangent makes \(135^\circ\) with +x axis. Find:
        (i) rate of reaction, (ii) ROA of R, (iii) ROD of P.
        • \( \dfrac{d[Q]}{dt} = \tan 135^\circ = -1\ \text{mol L}^{-1}\text{ s}^{-1} \)
        • \( \text{Rate} = -\dfrac{1}{3}\dfrac{d[Q]}{dt} = -\dfrac{1}{3}(-1) = \dfrac{1}{3}\ \text{mol L}^{-1}\text{ s}^{-1} \)
        • \( \text{ROA of R} = 1\times \text{Rate} = \dfrac{1}{3}\ \text{mol L}^{-1}\text{ s}^{-1} \)
        • \( \text{ROD of P} = 2\times \text{Rate} = \dfrac{2}{3}\ \text{mol L}^{-1}\text{ s}^{-1} \)

      3. Question 6
        \( 2A + B \rightarrow 3C \)
        At \(t=5\) s, the tangent on the B vs time curve makes \(150^\circ\) with +x axis. Find:
        (i) rate of reaction at \(t=5\) s, (ii) ROD of A at \(t=5\) s.
        • \( \dfrac{d[B]}{dt} = \tan 150^\circ = -\tan 30^\circ = -\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\ \text{mol L}^{-1}\text{ s}^{-1} \)
        • \( \text{Rate} = -\dfrac{d[B]}{dt} = \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\ \text{mol L}^{-1}\text{ s}^{-1} \)
        • \( \text{ROD of A} = 2\times \text{Rate} = \dfrac{2}{\sqrt{3}}\ \text{mol L}^{-1}\text{ s}^{-1} \)
        • (Extra) \( \text{ROA of C} = 3\times \text{Rate} = \dfrac{3}{\sqrt{3}} = \sqrt{3}\ \text{mol L}^{-1}\text{ s}^{-1} \)
