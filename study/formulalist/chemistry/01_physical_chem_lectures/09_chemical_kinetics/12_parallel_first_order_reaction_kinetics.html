  1. Parallel reactions setup and exam focus (00:05)
    (Back to topic index)
    • Parallel (side) reaction model:
      \( A \xrightarrow{k_1} B \) and \( A \xrightarrow{k_2} C \)
    • Typical exam problems use first-order parallel reactions:
      \( r_1 = k_1[A] \), \( r_2 = k_2[A] \)
    • Targets to derive/use:
      • Effective order (overall remains first order in \(A\))
      • Effective rate constant \(k_{\text{eff}}\)
      • Effective half-life \(t_{1/2,\text{eff}}\)
      • Effective activation energy \(E_{a,\text{eff}}\)
      • \([A](t), [B](t), [C](t)\)
      • Product ratio and % composition (major/minor product idea)
  2. Effective rate law and effective rate constant (05:12)
    (Back to topic index)
    • For parallel consumption of \(A\), total disappearance rate adds:
      \( -\frac{d[A]}{dt} = r_1 + r_2 \)
    • With first-order branches:
      \( -\frac{d[A]}{dt} = k_1[A] + k_2[A] = (k_1+k_2)[A] \)
    • Therefore effective rate constant:
      \( k_{\text{eff}} = k_1 + k_2 \)
    • Effective order with respect to \(A\) remains 1 (same form as first-order decay).
  3. Effective half-life relations (09:05)
    (Back to topic index)
    • For any first-order process:
      \( t_{1/2} = \frac{0.693}{k} \)
    • For parallel first-order branches:
      \( t_{1/2,\text{eff}} = \frac{0.693}{k_1+k_2} \)
    • If individual half-lives are given (\(t_{1/2,1}\) for \(k_1\), \(t_{1/2,2}\) for \(k_2\)):
      \( k_1=\frac{0.693}{t_{1/2,1}} \), \( k_2=\frac{0.693}{t_{1/2,2}} \)
    • Substitute into \(k_{\text{eff}}=k_1+k_2\) to get:
      \( \frac{1}{t_{1/2,\text{eff}}} = \frac{1}{t_{1/2,1}} + \frac{1}{t_{1/2,2}} \)
    • Extension to \(n\) parallel branches:
      \( k_{\text{eff}}=\sum_{i=1}^{n} k_i \) and \( \frac{1}{t_{1/2,\text{eff}}}=\sum_{i=1}^{n}\frac{1}{t_{1/2,i}} \)
  4. Effective activation energy using Arrhenius (12:24)
    (Back to topic index)
    • Arrhenius form for each branch:
      \( k_1 = A_1 e^{-E_{a1}/RT} \), \( k_2 = A_2 e^{-E_{a2}/RT} \)
    • Effective rate constant:
      \( k_{\text{eff}} = k_1 + k_2 \)
    • Define effective Arrhenius parameters:
      \( k_{\text{eff}} = A_{\text{eff}} e^{-E_{a,\text{eff}}/RT} \)
    • Result for effective activation energy (weighted by rate constants):
      \( E_{a,\text{eff}} = \frac{k_1E_{a1} + k_2E_{a2}}{k_1+k_2} \)
    • Exam-use tip: typically \(k_1, k_2, E_{a1}, E_{a2}\) are provided directly; plug into the formula.
  5. Concentration of A vs time for parallel first-order (17:33)
    (Back to topic index)
    • Differential equation for \(A\):
      \( -\frac{d[A]}{dt} = (k_1+k_2)[A] \)
    • Integrate using \([A]=[A]_0\) at \(t=0\):
      \( \ln\!\left(\frac{[A]}{[A]_0}\right) = -(k_1+k_2)t \)
    • Exponential form (reactant concentration vs time):
      \( [A](t) = [A]_0 e^{-(k_1+k_2)t} \)
    • Graph idea: \([A]\) decreases exponentially with time (standard first-order decay shape).
  6. Concentration of B and C vs time (22:44)
    (Back to topic index)
    • Formation rate of \(B\):
      \( \frac{d[B]}{dt} = k_1[A] \)
    • Substitute \(=0\):
      \( [B](t) = \frac{k_1[A]_0}{k_1+k_2}\left(1-e^{-(k_1+k_2)t}\right) \)
    • Similarly for \(C\), with \(=0\):
      \( [C](t) = \frac{k_2[A]_0}{k_1+k_2}\left(1-e^{-(k_1+k_2)t}\right) \)
    • Long-time limit (\(t \to \infty\)):
      • \( [A]\to 0 \) (approaches 0 asymptotically)
      • \( [B]_{\infty}=\frac{k_1}{k_1+k_2}[A]_0 \)
      • \( [C]_{\infty}=\frac{k_2}{k_1+k_2}[A]_0 \)
  7. Product ratio and percentage yield (28:01)
    (Back to topic index)
    • Ratio of product concentrations is time-independent (for this model):
      \( \frac{[B](t)}{[C](t)} = \frac{k_1}{k_2} \)
    • Interpretation: larger rate constant branch gives larger product amount (major vs minor product idea).
    • If more than two parallel products exist:
      \( [B]:[C]:[D] = k_1:k_2:k_3 \)
    • Percentage of \(B\) in total products:
      \( \%B = \frac{[B]}{[B]+[C]}\times 100 = \frac{k_1}{k_1+k_2}\times 100 \)
    • Percentage of \(C\) in total products:
      \( \%C = \frac{k_2}{k_1+k_2}\times 100 \)
    • Quick exam usage: if \(k_1=2~s^{-1}\), \(k_2=5~s^{-1}\):
      • \( \%B = \frac{2}{7}\times 100 \)
      • \( \%C = \frac{5}{7}\times 100 \)
