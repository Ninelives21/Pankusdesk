  1. Introduction
    - Meaning of chemical bond

  2. Types of Chemical Bonds

    1. Interatomic Bonds
      - Bond between two atoms
      - These are strong bonds ~ 200–400 kJ/mol

      - Types are:
      (a) Ionic bonds – metal donates, non-metal accepts
      (b) Covalent – sharing of electrons
      (c) Coordinate – actually a type of covalent bond where both electrons are donated by the same atom
      (d) Metallic – bond between atoms of a metallic substance

    2. Intermolecular Bonds
      - Bonds between two molecules
      - Typically weaker than interatomic ~ 2–40 kJ/mol

      - Types are:
      (a) Van der Waal’s forces (5 types)
      (b) Hydrogen bond
        2 types – (i) Inter (ii) Intra molecular

  6. Theories of Covalent Bonds
    1. VBT = Valence Bond Theory
        - Tells how the sharing of e⁻ happens
        - σ, π bonds
    2. VSEPR = Valence Shell Electron Pair Repulsion
        - Tells about shapes
    3. MOT = Molecular Orbital Theory
        - Orbitals present in molecules
        - Will tell if a substance is magnetic or not
        - Bond strength
    4. Hybridization = mixing of orbitals

  7. Cause of Chemical Bonding (Combination)
    (a) Old theory → to achieve octet or noble gas configuration for stability
    (b) New theory – to lower P.E. of the system
    (c) Let’s take example of two atoms A, B
    ○ Let Nₐ, Nᵦ be their nuclei; eₐ, eᵦ be their electrons
    - There are two types of forces at play here:
    (1) Repulsion between Nₐ and Nᵦ and between eₐ and eᵦ
    (2) Attraction between Nₐ and eᵦ and between Nᵦ and eₐ
    - Initially attraction > repulsion, so the two atoms come close till repulsion is strong
    - The distance between the two nuclei at this point is called bond length
    - Shows that when P.E. is minimum, attraction is less than or equal to repulsion
