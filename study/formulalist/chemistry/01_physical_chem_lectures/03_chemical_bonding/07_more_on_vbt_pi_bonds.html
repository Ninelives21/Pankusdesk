    1. Intro To P-Pi, O-Pi | P-Pi P-Pi Bonds
      • Summary of previous theory on VBT
      • \(\sigma\) bond is made with axial overlapping
      • s–s, s–p, p–p (first pp bond)
      • \(\pi\) bond is made with lateral overlapping
      • p–p, p–d, d–d
      • If there is a single bond between two atoms it is a \(\sigma\) bond
      • If double bond, the first is \(\sigma\), second is \(\pi\)
      • If triple bond, first is \(\sigma\), rest two are \(\pi\)
      • There is more overlapping in \(\sigma\), so it is a stronger bond

    2. Types Of \(\pi\) Bonds: \(p_\pi\)–\(p_\pi\), \(p_\pi\)–\(d_\pi\), \(d_\pi\)–\(d_\pi\)
      1. \(p_\pi\)–\(d_\pi\)
        +ve of p bond dumbell will overlap with one of the dumbell's of the d bond , provided electrons have opposite spin ; and similarly with -ve.
      2. \(d_\pi\)–\(d_\pi\)
        +ve of one of the dumbells of d will overlap with +ve of one of the dumbells of the d-orbital of the second atom having opposite spin ; and similarly with –ve signs.
      3. \(p_\pi\)–\(p_\pi\)
        +ve of p-orbital of one atom overlaps with +ve of p-orbital of the second atom with opposite spin ; and same with –ve.
      • \(p_\pi\)–\(p_\pi\) is strongest of the three as there is maximum overlapping.

    3. Question 1 : How Many \(p_\pi\)–\(p_\pi\) And \(p_\pi\)–\(d_\pi\) Bonds Are In \(\mathrm{SO_2}\) ?
      S : 16 -> \(1s^2\ 2s^2\ 2p^6\ 3s^2\ 3p^4\)
      Valence shell orbitals: (diagram)
      • S has only two vacant orbitals (half-filled orbitals) but it is forming four bonds
      • So use the first excited state of sulphur
      • Use the vacant d-orbitals (in the ground state these are vacant)
      O : 8 -> \(1s^2\ 2s^2\ 2p^4\)
      Valence shell orbital : \(2s^2\ 2p^4\)
      • Each Oxygen can form one p–p and one p–d bond with the excited S-atom.

    4. Question 2 : How Many \(p_\pi\)–\(p_\pi\) And \(p_\pi\)–\(d_\pi\) Bonds Are In Are In \(\mathrm{SO_3}\)
      Ground state S : (diagram) -> \(3s^2\ 3p^4\)
      1st excited state : (diagram) -> \(3s^1\ 3p^3\ 3d^1\)
      2nd excited state : (diagram) -> \(3s^1\ 3p^3\ 3d^2\)
      • S needs to form 6 bonds, so it needs to go into second excited state
      • 3s and 3p electrons jump to 2 vacant orbitals of 3d
      • Now we have 6 half-filled orbitals
      1st O : \(2p\) of O with \(3s\) of S \(\Rightarrow\) \(2p\)–\(3s\) \(\sigma\) ; \(2p\) of O with \(3p\) of S \(\Rightarrow\) \(2p\)–\(3p\) \(\pi\)
      2nd O : \(2p\) of O with \(3p\) of S \(\Rightarrow\) \(2p\)–\(3p\) \(\sigma\) ; \(2p\) of O with \(3d\) of S \(\Rightarrow\) \(2p\)–\(3d\) \(\pi\)
      3rd O : \(2p\) of O with \(3p\) of S \(\Rightarrow\) \(2p\)–\(3p\) \(\sigma\) ; \(2p\) of O with \(3d\) of S \(\Rightarrow\) \(2p\)–\(3d\) \(\pi\)
      • First \(\sigma\) of all three O atoms will be formed using up 1s and 2p of S
      • Next \(\pi\) of all three O atoms will be formed using up 3p and 3d of S
      Answer \(p_\pi\)–\(p_\pi = 3\) , \(p_\pi\)–\(d_\pi = 2\)

    6. Question 4 : Find Number Of \(p_\pi\)–\(p_\pi\) And \(p_\pi\)–\(d_\pi\) Bonds In \(\mathrm{H_2SO_4}\)
      • S in ground state : \(1s^2\ 2s^2\ 2p^6\ 3s^2\ 3p^4\)
      • Since it is forming 6 bonds, it needs 6 half filled orbitals
      • it needs to get into second excited state
      • \(1s^2\ 2s^2\ 2p^6\ 3s^1\ 3p^3\ 3d^2\)
      1. O\(_1\) s–p \(\sigma\)-bond
      2. O\(_2\) p–p \(\sigma\)-bond
      3. O\(_3\) p–p \(\sigma\)-bond
      4. O\(_4\) p–p \(\sigma\)-bond
      5. O\(_2\) (or any) p–d \(\pi\)-bond
      6. O\(_4\) (or any) p–d \(\pi\) bond
      Ans (PW got it wrong!) \(p_\pi\)–\(p_\pi = 0\) , \(p_\pi\)–\(d_\pi = 2\)
