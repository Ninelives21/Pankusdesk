      1. Introduction To VSEPR With Examples Of CH\(_4\), NH\(_3\), H\(_2\)O
        • Find hybridisation of the following using \(Z=\tfrac12(\text{no. of valence }e^-+(\text{−ve charge on molecule})-(\text{+ve charge on molecule})+\text{monovalent atoms }[H,Cl,F,I,Br])\)
        • (i) CH\(_4\): \(Z=\tfrac12(4+4)=4\)
        • (ii) NH\(_3\): \(Z=\tfrac12(5+3)=4\)
        • (iii) H\(_2\)O: \(Z=\tfrac12(6+2)=4\)
        • All three have \(Z=4\Rightarrow\) hybridisation \(=sp^3\Rightarrow\) geometry is tetrahedral (angle \(109^\circ28'\))
        • But we observe that this angle is only present in CH\(_4\); in case of NH\(_3\), the angle between the bond pairs is \(107^\circ\) instead of \(109^\circ28'\), and \(105^\circ\) in case of H\(_2\)O
        • this is because of repulsion due to lone pairs that are present in NH\(_3\) and H\(_2\)O. NH\(_3\) has one l.p and H\(_2\)O has 2 l.p.
        • The geometry is same (tetrahedral) for all, but the shape has changed.
        • Geometry is based on (lp + bp) but shape (which is arrangement of atoms around the c) is only dependent on bp.
        • VSEPR is the cause of this change in shape.
        • there are three sets of bonds which repel each other: (1) b.p - b.p (2) b.p - l.p (3) l.p - l.p. This repulsion is different for each of these.

      2. VSEPR Theory
        1. Electron pairs are always repelling each other and try to remain as far apart as possible
        2. Different electron pairs have different orders of repulsion: \(l.p-l.p>l.p-b.p>b.p-b.p\)
        3. If there is no l.p, there is no VSEPR; Geometry will be the same as shape; else they are not equal
        4. Geometry depends on \(l.p+b.p\ (=Z)\); shape depends on only \(b.p\)
        5. To see shape, hide the lone pair and observe only the bond pairs.

      4. Examples For \(Z=2,3\)
        Find the number of lone pair, hybridisation, geometry and shape for the following:

        1. SO\(_3\)
          \(Z=\tfrac12(6)=3\Rightarrow sp^2\Rightarrow\) trigonal planar
          \(Z=lp+bp\)
          As there are 3 atoms attached to S, no. of \(bp=3\Rightarrow lp=3-3=0\)
          \(\Rightarrow\) shape \(=\) geometry \(=\) trigonal planar.

        2. HNO\(_3\)
          (actually NO\(_3^-\) as H is attached to O and not N)
          \(Z=\tfrac12(5+1)=3\Rightarrow sp^2\Rightarrow\) trigonal planar
          \(bp=3, lp=0\)
          shape \(=\) geometry \(=\) trigonal planar

        3. SO\(_2\)
          \(Z=\tfrac12(6+\varnothing)=3\Rightarrow sp^2\Rightarrow\) trigonal planar
          \(bp=2, lp=1\)
          shape \(\neq\) geometry
          shape \(=\) Bent / Angular / V
          the angle between the two bond pair will be \(<120^\circ\) the angle between \(l.p-b.p>120^\circ\)

      6. Examples For \(Z=4\)
        Find the number of lone pair, hybridisation, geometry and shape for these:-

        1. (1) CH\(_4\)
          \(Z=\tfrac12(4+4)=4\Rightarrow sp^3\Rightarrow\) tetrahedral
          \(bp=4, lp=0\Rightarrow\) shape \(=\) geometry \(=\) tetrahedral

        2. NH\(_3\)
          \(Z=\tfrac12(5+3)=4\Rightarrow sp^3\Rightarrow\) tetrahedral
          \(bp=3\Rightarrow lp=1\)
          shape \(=\) pyramidal

        3. H\(_2\)O
          \(Z=\tfrac12(6+2)=4\Rightarrow sp^3\Rightarrow\) tetrahedral
          \(bp=2, lp=2\Rightarrow\) shape \(=\) Bent / V / Angular
          \( \theta_1 (lp-lp) > \theta_2 (lp-bp) > \theta_3 (bp-bp) \)

        4. CH\(_3^-\)
          \(Z=\tfrac12(4+1+3)=4\Rightarrow\) \(sp^3\Rightarrow\) tetrahedral
          \(bp=3, lp=1\)
          shape \(=\) pyramidal
