DANIELL CELL - ELECTROCHEMICAL / GALVANIC / VOLTAIC CELL

YOUTUBE LECTURE LINK
TOPICS IN THIS LECTURE : (WITH TIMESTAMPS)

  1. Experiment
    • Zn rod reduced weight.
    • Blue colour of solution fades.
    • Red precipitate forms.
    • Rise in temperature.

    Reactions / inference

    • Zn → Zn2+ + 2e−
    • Cu2+ + 2e− → Cu
    • Spontaneous process → ΔG < 0

  2. Electrochemical cell / Galvanic / Voltaic cell
    • Daniel cell (Zn–Cu cell) is an example of a galvanic cell.
    • Galvanic cell converts chemical energy → electrical energy.

  3. Daniel cell
    • Ammeter deflects in the direction of current.
    • Over time, charge builds up in half-cells; a salt bridge prevents this.

    Half-cell reactions & conventions

    • Left (oxidation, anode, negative): Zn → Zn2+ + 2e−
    • Right (reduction, cathode, positive): Cu2+ + 2e− → Cu

    LOAN mnemonic

    • L — Left half-cell
    • O — Oxidation half-cell
    • A — Anode half-cell
    • N — Negative half-cell

  4. Salt bridge
    • U-tube filled with an inert electrolyte in semi-liquid state.
    • Common choices:
      • KCl
      • K2SO4
      • KNO3
      • NH4Cl

  5. Functions of salt bridge
    1. Maintains electrical neutrality of the half-cells.
    2. Allows flow of electrons (completes the circuit).
    3. Minimizes liquid–liquid junction potential.

  6. Cell representation

    M(s) | Mn+(aq) || Mn+(aq) | M(s)

    • Left — oxidation
    • Right — reduction
