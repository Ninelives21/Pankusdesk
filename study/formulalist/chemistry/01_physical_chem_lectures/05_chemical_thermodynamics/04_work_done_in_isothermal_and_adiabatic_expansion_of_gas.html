  1. Roadmap: work in different thermodynamic processes (00:10)
    (Back to topic index)
    • Goal: derive/remember work expressions for common thermodynamic processes in gaseous systems (useful for time-limited exams).
    • Processes covered: isochoric, isobaric, isothermal (reversible/irreversible), adiabatic (reversible/irreversible).
    • Core idea: work depends on how pressure varies with volume during the process.
  2. Sign convention for gas expansion vs compression (01:46)
    (Back to topic index)
    • For PV work in the sign convention used here: \(W=-P_{\text{ext}}\Delta V\) (valid when \(P_{\text{ext}}\) is constant).
    • Expansion: \(\Delta V>0 \Rightarrow W<0\) (work done by the gas; system loses energy via work).
    • Compression: \(\Delta V<0 \Rightarrow W>0\) (work done on the gas; system gains energy via work).
  3. Isochoric and isobaric work: quick results + general integral (03:25)
    (Back to topic index)
    • Isochoric (constant volume): \(\Delta V=0 \Rightarrow W=0\).
    • Isobaric (constant external pressure): \(W=-P_{\text{ext}}\Delta V\) is directly applicable only when pressure is constant.
    • General work formula (always valid):
      \(W=-\int_{V_1}^{V_2} P_{\text{ext}}\, dV\)
    • This integral form applies to isothermal, adiabatic, isochoric, isobaric, and polytropic processes (choose the correct \(P_{\text{ext}}(V)\) relation).
  4. Isothermal process basics: Boyle’s law + \(P\)-\(V\) curve (07:03)
    (Back to topic index)
    • Isothermal process: temperature remains constant (\(T=\) constant).
    • For an ideal gas at constant \(T\): \(P\propto \frac{1}{V}\) and \(P_1V_1=P_2V_2\) (Boyle’s law).
    • On a \(P\) vs \(V\) graph, the isothermal curve is a rectangular hyperbola (pressure decreases as volume increases).
    • Two cases to study: reversible isothermal and irreversible isothermal expansion.
  5. Reversible isothermal expansion: key formula + meaning (08:22)
    (Back to topic index)
    • Reversible isothermal work (ideal gas):
      \(W=-nRT\ln\!\left(\frac{V_2}{V_1}\right)\)
    • Base conversion:
      \(W=-2.303\,nRT\log_{10}\!\left(\frac{V_2}{V_1}\right)\)
    • Symbols: \(n\) = moles, \(R\) = gas constant, \(T\) in kelvin, \(V_1\to V_2\) = volume change.
  6. Reversible isothermal derivation using \(W=-\int P_{\text{ext}}\,dV\) (15:32)
    (Back to topic index)
    • Reversible setup idea: quasi-static change using infinitesimal load changes on a frictionless piston so the system remains near equilibrium (\(P\approx P_{\text{ext}}\) at each step).
    • For isothermal requirement: boundaries are taken as good conductors so heat can flow from surroundings to keep \(T\) constant during expansion.
    • Because pressure is not constant, use the general formula:
      \(W=-\int_{V_1}^{V_2} P_{\text{ext}}\, dV\)
    • In reversible (quasi-equilibrium) steps: \(P_{\text{ext}}\approx P\) (gas pressure), and for ideal gas \(P=\frac{nRT}{V}\).
    • Substitute and integrate:
      \(W=-\int_{V_1}^{V_2}\frac{nRT}{V}\,dV=-nRT\ln\!\left(\frac{V_2}{V_1}\right)\)
    • Log property used: \(\ln A-\ln B=\ln\!\left(\frac{A}{B}\right)\).
  7. Useful isothermal variants: pressure form and replacing \(nRT\) (20:26)
    (Back to topic index)
    • Using Boyle’s law \(P_1V_1=P_2V_2\), the ratio:
      \(\frac{V_2}{V_1}=\frac{P_1}{P_2}\)
    • Equivalent reversible isothermal forms:
      \(W=-nRT\ln\!\left(\frac{P_1}{P_2}\right)\)
      \(W=-2.303\,nRT\log_{10}\!\left(\frac{P_1}{P_2}\right)\)
    • If \(nRT\) is not directly given, use \(PV=nRT\) at the same (constant) temperature:
      Replace \(nRT\) by \(P_1V_1\) or \(P_2V_2\) (both are equal for isothermal ideal-gas case).
    • Example substitution structure:
      \(W=-P_1V_1\ln\!\left(\frac{V_2}{V_1}\right)\) or \(W=-P_2V_2\ln\!\left(\frac{V_2}{V_1}\right)\)
  8. Irreversible isothermal expansion: free expansion vs constant \(P_{\text{ext}}\) (25:06)
    (Back to topic index)
    • Irreversible process: not quasi-static; system is not in equilibrium throughout; pressure does not vary “smoothly” during the change.
    • Case 1: free expansion into vacuum:
      \(P_{\text{ext}}=0 \Rightarrow W=-\int P_{\text{ext}}\,dV=0\)
    • Case 2: expansion against a constant external pressure (external pressure much smaller than gas pressure, not infinitesimally close):
      \(W=-P_{\text{ext}}(V_2-V_1)=-P_{\text{ext}}\Delta V\)
    • Shortcut rule used here: if the process is explicitly irreversible, use \(W=-P_{\text{ext}}\Delta V\) (and set \(P_{\text{ext}}=0\) for vacuum).
  9. Adiabatic process basics: \(Q=0\), Poisson relation, \(\gamma\) values (28:52)
    (Back to topic index)
    • Adiabatic process: no heat exchange with surroundings, \(Q=0\).
    • Practical model: walls are perfectly insulating (no heat enters or leaves the system).
    • Poisson relation (adiabatic, ideal gas):
      \(P_1V_1^{\gamma}=P_2V_2^{\gamma}\)
    • \(\gamma=\frac{C_P}{C_V}\) (depends on gas type).
    • Typical \(\gamma\) values (as stated):
      • Monatomic: \(\gamma\approx 1.66\)
      • Diatomic: \(\gamma\approx 1.4\)
      • Triatomic: \(\gamma\approx 1.33\)
  10. Adiabatic \(P\)-\(V\) curve: steeper than isothermal (32:57)
    (Back to topic index)
    • On a \(P\) vs \(V\) plot, adiabatic curve is steeper than the isothermal curve (pressure falls faster with volume in adiabatic expansion).
    • Because \(P\) changes continuously with \(V\), the constant-pressure shortcut \(W=-P_{\text{ext}}\Delta V\) is not usable for reversible adiabatic paths.
    • Use the general work integral:
      \(W=-\int_{V_1}^{V_2} P_{\text{ext}}\, dV\)
  11. Reversible adiabatic work: final results and derivation outline (34:40)
    (Back to topic index)
    • Final result (reversible adiabatic, ideal gas):
      \(W=\frac{nR}{\gamma-1}(T_2-T_1)\)
    • Equivalent pressure–volume form:
      \(W=\frac{P_2V_2-P_1V_1}{\gamma-1}\)
    • Derivation skeleton (from the integral):
      • Start with \(W=-\int_{V_1}^{V_2} P\, dV\) (reversible \(\Rightarrow P_{\text{ext}}\approx P\)).
      • From Poisson relation: \(PV^{\gamma}=K\) (constant for the adiabatic path).
      • So \(P=\frac{K}{V^{\gamma}}\), giving \(W=-\int_{V_1}^{V_2}\frac{K}{V^{\gamma}}\,dV\).
      • Integrate power form to reach:
        \(W=\frac{K}{\gamma-1}\left(V_2^{1-\gamma}-V_1^{1-\gamma}\right)\)
      • Rewrite using \(K=P_1V_1^{\gamma}=P_2V_2^{\gamma}\) to simplify to:
        \(W=\frac{P_2V_2-P_1V_1}{\gamma-1}\)
      • Finally use \(PV=nRT\) to convert:
        \(P_2V_2-P_1V_1=nR(T_2-T_1)\)
    • Interpretation note: in adiabatic processes temperature changes, so \(T_1\neq T_2\).
  12. Irreversible adiabatic shortcut: use \(-P_{\text{ext}}\Delta V\) (42:22)
    (Back to topic index)
    • Shortcut rule stated: if the process is explicitly irreversible, use:
      \(W=-P_{\text{ext}}\Delta V\)
    • For vacuum expansion (any irreversible free expansion): \(P_{\text{ext}}=0\Rightarrow W=0\).
    • Process classification reminder: reversible vs irreversible definitions are addressed in earlier introductory material (conceptual prerequisite).
