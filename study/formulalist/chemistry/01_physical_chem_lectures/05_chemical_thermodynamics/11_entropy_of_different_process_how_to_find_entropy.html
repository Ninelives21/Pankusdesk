  1. Entropy Change for a Gas Between Two States (00:25)
    • Consider a gas moving from state 1 to state 2 in a \(P\)-\(V\) diagram.
    • Initial state: \( (P_1, V_1, T_1) \)
    • Final state: \( (P_2, V_2, T_2) \)
    • Since the thermodynamic state changes, molecular randomness changes.
    • Therefore entropy changes.
    • Entropy is a state function, meaning it depends only on the initial and final states.
  2. Using First Law to Express \( dQ \) (04:25)
    • Basic entropy relation:
    • \( dS = \frac{dQ}{T} \)
    • Integrating:
    • \( \Delta S = \int \frac{dQ}{T} \)
    • But \( dQ \) may not be known.
    • Use first law of thermodynamics:
    • \( dU = dQ + dW \)
    • Therefore:
    • \( dQ = dU - dW \)
  3. General Entropy Formula for an Ideal Gas (10:00)
    • Internal energy change for ideal gas:
    • \( dU = nC_v dT \)
    • Work done:
    • \( dW = -P dV \)
    • Substitute into entropy equation:
    • \( dS = \frac{nC_v dT}{T} + \frac{P dV}{T} \)
    • Using ideal gas equation:
    • \( PV = nRT \)
    • \( P = \frac{nRT}{V} \)
    • After integration:
    • \( \Delta S = nC_v \ln\left(\frac{T_2}{T_1}\right) + nR \ln\left(\frac{V_2}{V_1}\right) \)
  4. Entropy Change in Isothermal Process (18:26)
    • Isothermal process: \( T = \text{constant} \)
    • Therefore \( dT = 0 \)
    • Entropy change reduces to:
    • \( \Delta S = nR \ln \left(\frac{V_2}{V_1}\right) \)
    • Using Boyle’s law:
    • \( P_1V_1 = P_2V_2 \)
    • So:
    • \( \Delta S = nR \ln\left(\frac{P_1}{P_2}\right) \)
  5. Entropy Change in Isochoric Process (20:40)
    • Isochoric process: volume constant.
    • \( dV = 0 \)
    • Entropy change becomes:
    • \( \Delta S = nC_v \ln \left(\frac{T_2}{T_1}\right) \)
  6. Entropy Change in Adiabatic Process (21:29)
    • Adiabatic process: \( dQ = 0 \)
    • Entropy relation:
    • \( \Delta S = \int \frac{dQ}{T} = 0 \)
    • Thus entropy remains constant.
  7. Entropy Change in Isobaric Process (21:50)
    • Isobaric process: constant pressure.
    • Heat supplied:
    • \( dQ = nC_p dT \)
    • Entropy change:
    • \( \Delta S = nC_p \ln\left(\frac{T_2}{T_1}\right) \)
  8. Numerical: Heating Gas in Closed Container (24:27)
    • Given: gas heated from \(0^\circ C\) to \(273^\circ C\).
    • Convert to Kelvin:
    • \( T_1 = 273K \)
    • \( T_2 = 546K \)
    • Closed container → constant volume.
    • Formula used:
    • \( \Delta S = nC_v \ln\left(\frac{T_2}{T_1}\right) \)
    • Final entropy change ≈ \(25\ J/mol\ K\).
  9. Numerical: Entropy from PV Graph (31:00)
    • Graph represents an isothermal process.
    • Because:
    • \( P_1V_1 = P_2V_2 \)
    • Entropy formula:
    • \( \Delta S = nR \ln\left(\frac{V_2}{V_1}\right) \)
    • Substitute given values to estimate entropy change.
  10. Entropy Change During Phase Transition (36:53)
    • During phase change temperature remains constant.
    • Therefore entropy formula simplifies:
    • \( \Delta S = \frac{\Delta Q}{T} \)
    • For melting:
    • \( \Delta S_{fusion} = \frac{\Delta H_{fusion}}{T_m} \)
    • For vaporization:
    • \( \Delta S_{vap} = \frac{\Delta H_{vap}}{T_b} \)
  11. Numerical: Melting Point from Entropy of Fusion (41:23)
    • Given:
    • \( \Delta H_f = 1.435\ kcal/mol \)
    • \( \Delta S = 5.26\ cal/mol\ K \)
    • Formula:
    • \( \Delta S = \frac{\Delta H_f}{T_m} \)
    • Rearranging:
    • \( T_m = \frac{\Delta H_f}{\Delta S} \)
    • Result ≈ \(273K\) → \(0^\circ C\).
  12. Numerical: Entropy Change When Ice Melts (45:22)
    • Entropy formula:
    • \( \Delta S = \frac{\Delta H_f}{T} \)
    • Given melting enthalpy of ice.
    • Calculate entropy per mole.
    • Convert to entropy per gram.
    • Final result ≈ \(1.33\ J/g\ K\).
