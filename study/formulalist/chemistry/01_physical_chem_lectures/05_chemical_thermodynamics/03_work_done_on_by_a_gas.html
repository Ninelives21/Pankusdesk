  1. Energy exchange: heat vs work (00:58)
    (Back to topic index)
    • A thermodynamic system can exchange energy with surroundings in two ways:
      \( \text{Heat (}q\text{)} \) and \( \text{Work (}w\text{)} \).
    • Heat transfer occurs only when there is a temperature difference between system and surroundings.
    • Work is a mechanical mode of energy transfer (involves force/pressure causing displacement/volume change).
  2. Gas work formula and sign convention (03:16)
    (Back to topic index)
    • For a gaseous system (mechanical work against external pressure):
      \( w = -P_{\text{ext}}\Delta V \)
    • Meaning of symbols:
      • \(P_{\text{ext}}\): external pressure opposing expansion/compression
      • \(\Delta V = V_2 - V_1\): change in volume
    • Sign summary:
      • Expansion: \( \Delta V > 0 \Rightarrow w < 0 \)
      • Compression: \( \Delta V < 0 \Rightarrow w > 0 \)
  3. Piston model: expansion vs compression (05:00)
    (Back to topic index)
    • Consider gas in a cylinder fitted with a frictionless piston; external pressure \(P_{\text{ext}}\) acts on piston.
    • If \(P_{\text{gas}} > P_{\text{ext}}\): gas expands and pushes piston outward.
      • \(\Delta V > 0\)
      • Work is done by the gas on surroundings
      • Energy leaves the gas via work \(\Rightarrow w < 0\)
    • If \(P_{\text{ext}} > P_{\text{gas}}\): gas is compressed.
      • \(\Delta V < 0\)
      • Work is done on the gas by surroundings
      • Energy enters the gas via work \(\Rightarrow w > 0\)
    • Interpretation rule (gas viewpoint):
      • \(w < 0\): system (gas) does work on surroundings
      • \(w > 0\): surroundings do work on system
  4. Derivation: \(W = -P_{\text{ext}}\Delta V\) (09:38)
    (Back to topic index)
    • Start from mechanical work:
      \( w = \vec{F}\cdot\vec{s} = Fs\cos\theta \)
    • For a piston of cross-sectional area \(A\):
      • Pressure definition: \( P = \frac{F}{A} \Rightarrow F = PA \)
      • Small piston displacement: \( s = \Delta x \)
      • Volume change: \( \Delta V = A\Delta x \)
    • In expansion, force due to \(P_{\text{ext}}\) acts opposite to displacement (\(\theta = 180^\circ\)):
      \( w = (P_{\text{ext}}A)(\Delta x)\cos 180^\circ = -P_{\text{ext}}(A\Delta x) = -P_{\text{ext}}\Delta V \)
    • Memorize the usable result:
      \( w = -P_{\text{ext}}\Delta V \) (for constant external pressure)
  5. Unit conversion: atm·L to joule (14:24)
    (Back to topic index)
    • Work in gas problems often appears as \( \text{atm·L} \) from \(P\) in atm and \(V\) in liters.
    • Conversion to SI unit:
      \( 1\ \text{atm·L} = 101.3\ \text{J} \)
    • Example idea: gas expands by \(0.5\ \text{L}\) against \(1\ \text{atm}\).
      • \(\Delta V = +0.5\ \text{L}\)
      • \( w = -P_{\text{ext}}\Delta V = -(1)(0.5) = -0.5\ \text{atm·L}\)
      • \( w = -0.5 \times 101.3\ \text{J} \) (final numeric value in joule)
  6. Variable pressure: \(W = -\int P\,dV\) and area under \(P\)-\(V\) curve (18:56)
    (Back to topic index)
    • The formula \( w = -P_{\text{ext}}\Delta V \) is valid only when external pressure is constant.
    • If pressure varies during the process, take a small volume change \(dV\) where pressure is approximately constant:
      \( dw = -P\,dV \)
    • Total work from \(V_1\) to \(V_2\):
      \( w = -\int_{V_1}^{V_2} P\,dV \)
    • Graph meaning:
      • Magnitude of work equals the area under the \(P\)-\(V\) curve between \(V_1\) and \(V_2\).
      • Sign is decided by expansion/compression (use \(\Delta V\) sign or physical meaning).
    • Special case: if \(P\) is constant, integration reduces to:
      \( w = -P(V_2 - V_1) = -P\Delta V \)
  7. Graph example A→B: trapezium area method + sign (24:04)
    (Back to topic index)
    • When the path is a straight line on a \(P\)-\(V\) graph, work magnitude is the area under the line (a trapezium).
    • For A(1 L, 2 atm) → B(3 L, 4 atm):
      • \(\Delta V = 3 - 1 = 2\ \text{L}\)
      • Area (trapezium) \(= \frac{1}{2}(P_1 + P_2)\Delta V = \frac{1}{2}(2 + 4)\times 2 = 6\ \text{atm·L}\)
    • Sign:
      • Volume increases (expansion) \(\Rightarrow w < 0\)
      • So \( w = -6\ \text{atm·L} \) and in joule \( w = -6 \times 101.3\ \text{J}\)
  8. Cyclic process ABCA: work in segments; work is a path function (27:44)
    (Back to topic index)
    • For a process on a \(P\)-\(V\) graph, work in each segment equals signed area under that segment.
    • Segment AB (constant volume): \(\Delta V = 0 \Rightarrow w_{AB} = 0\)
    • Segment BC (constant pressure line at \(6\ \text{atm}\) from \(1\) L to \(3\) L):
      • Area \(= 6 \times 2 = 12\ \text{atm·L}\)
      • Expansion \(\Rightarrow w_{BC} = -12\ \text{atm·L}\)
    • Segment CA (slanted back from \(3\) L to \(1\) L):
      • Area under CA is a trapezium with parallel sides \(2\) and \(6\) (atm) and width \(2\) (L)
      • Magnitude \(= \frac{1}{2}(2 + 6)\times 2 = 8\ \text{atm·L}\)
      • Compression \(\Rightarrow w_{CA} = +8\ \text{atm·L}\)
    • Net work in the cycle:
      \( w_{\text{ABCA}} = w_{AB} + w_{BC} + w_{CA} = 0 - 12 + 8 = -4\ \text{atm·L} \)
    • Key conclusion:
      • In a cyclic process, state variables return to initial values (e.g., \(P\), \(V\)), so their net change is \(0\).
      • Work does not necessarily become \(0\) in a cycle \(\Rightarrow\) work is not a state function; it is a path function.

      2. Question 2 Using the same cycle, explain why work is a path function and not a state function.
  9. Shortcut: cyclic work = enclosed area; clockwise vs anticlockwise sign (33:16)
    (Back to topic index)
    • Shortcut for a complete cyclic process on a \(P\)-\(V\) diagram:
      \( |w_{\text{cycle}}| = \text{Area enclosed by the cycle} \)
    • Sign convention (as taught here):
      • Clockwise cycle \(\Rightarrow w_{\text{cycle}} < 0\)
      • Anticlockwise cycle \(\Rightarrow w_{\text{cycle}} > 0\)
    • For a triangular cycle:
      • \(\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2}\times \text{base}\times \text{height}\)
      • Apply sign based on direction of traversal.
  10. Practice graph ABCDA: segment work + cycle work (35:39)
    (Back to topic index)
    • Useful zero-work rule:
      • On a vertical line (constant volume), \(\Delta V = 0 \Rightarrow w = 0\).
    • For the shown rectangle-like cycle:
      • \(w_{AB} = 0\) (constant volume)
      • \(w_{BC}\): area under BC (a rectangle); expansion \(\Rightarrow\) negative
      • \(w_{CD} = 0\) (constant volume)
      • \(w_{DA}\): area under DA; compression \(\Rightarrow\) positive
    • Net work in cycle:
      • Add segment works, or directly use enclosed area magnitude.
      • For a rectangle: \( |w_{\text{cycle}}| = \text{length}\times \text{breadth} \) (in atm·L if axes are atm and L)
      • Apply sign based on clockwise/anticlockwise direction.
