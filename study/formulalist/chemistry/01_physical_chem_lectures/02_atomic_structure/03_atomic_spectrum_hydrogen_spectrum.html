  1. Bohr's Emmision Spectrum

    1. Bohr's Experiment : heated a gas in a glass tube and observed that rays of only some wavelengths were emitted.
    2. He called this discrete/line spectrum.
    3. Upon getting heated, the electrons of the gas absorb the energy and jump to a higher energy level.
    4. The energy of the different energy levels of Hydrogen gas is:
      1. n = 1, \(-\) 13.6 eV
      2. n = 2, \(-\) 3.4 eV
      3. n = 3, \(-\) 1.51 eV
      4. n = 4, \(-\) 0.85 eV
    5. Electron always tries to remain in the lowest energy level.
    6. \(\therefore\) electron returns back to its lowest energy form, emitting energy in the form of radiation.
    7. This is called the Emission spectrum
