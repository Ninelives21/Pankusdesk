  1. Discovery of Electron

    1. The very first atomic model was the Dalton's Atomic model(1805) => the smallest fundamental particle is the "atom".
    2. Dalton's atomic model failed when the discovery of electron by J.J. Thomson took place.
      • Electron was discovered using a cathode ray tube.
      • Properties of the cathode rays discovered in the above experiment:
        1. They are negatively charged.
        2. They travel in straight lines.
        3. They possess kinetic energy.
        4. They are made up of small particles.
        5. They are deflected towards the +ve plate in electromagnetic field.
        6. They are deflected in magnetic field.
        7. They produce fluorescence
        8. They produce X-rays.
        9. They undergo ionization.
        10. On changing the gas in the cathode ray tube or the material of the electrodes, these particles displayed the same properties (remain unchanged).
      • From the above properties we can say that these particles are fundamental.
      • These were called "electrons".
    4. J. J. Thomson measured charge by mass ratio of cathode rays, \(\dfrac{e}{m}\) = 1.75 * \(\text{10}^{11}\) \(\dfrac{C}{Kg}\).
    5. Milikan oil drop experiment -> charge of electron = - 1.6 * \(\text{10}^{-19}\) C
    6. Mass of electron = 9.1 \(\text{10}^{-31}\) Kg


  2. Discovery of Proton

    1. The credit for the discovery of proton goes to E. Goldstein.
    2. He also used a discharge tube, increased the pressure a bit (invreased the gas a bit), and used a perforated cathode.
    3. What he got were "Anode rays" = "Canal rays" = "Coloured rays".
    4. Anode rays are not protons.
    5. These are actually positive gaseous ions (after losing electrons ), as opposed to cathode rays which are electrons emitted from cathode.
    6. Properties of these rays :
      1. They are made up of charged (+ve) particles.
      2. They travel in straight lines.
      3. They possess kinetic energy (though less than cathode rays)/
      4. They are made up of small particles.
      5. They are deflected towards the -ve plate in electromagnetic field.
      6. They are deflected in magnetic field.
      7. They produce fluorescence
      8. They depend upon nature of the gas taken in the discharge tube.
    7. Charge to mass ratio in case of anode rays will depend on the nature of gas.
    8. Charge to mass ratio was highest with the lightest gas, Hydrogen.
    9. Hydrogen cation = proton
    10. charge of proton = + 1.6 * \(\text{10}^{-19}\) C
    11. Mass of proton = 1.625 \(\text{10}^{-27}\) Kg
    12. Mass of proton = 1837 times mass of electron.


  3. JJ Thomson's Atomic Model

    1. This was the first atomic model and it talked about subatomic particles.
    2. This is the "plum pudding" or the "watermelon" model.
    3. Electons are embedded in a positive sphere.
    4. Atom is electrically neutral.


  4. Rutherford's Atomic Model

    1. Alpha particle scattering experiment and observations from it.
    2. Aplha particle = Helium nucleus = +2 charge and 4amu mass
    3. Due to the alpha particle experiment, Rutherford got the credit for discovery of neutron.
    4. This led to the Rutherford's Atomic Model :
      1. Most part of the atom is hollow.
      2. The positive charge resides in a very small space.
      3. This small space was termed as "nucleus".
      4. The positive charge is heavy.
      5. Number of protons = number of electrons in a neutral atom.
      6. The size of the nucleus is very small compared to the size of the atom.
      7. Size of atom : size of nucleus = \(\text{10}^{-10}\) : \(\text{10}^{-15}\); atom = 100000 times nucleus in size.
      8. Electrons revolve in closed circular orbits around the nucleus (analoguous to the solar system).
      9. The above theory was established as electron is pulled to the nucleus due to electostatic force of attraction but not accelerating into it due to centripetal force.
      10. \(\text{F}_e\) = \(\text{F}_c\) where \(\text{F}_e\) = electrostatic force and \(\text{F}_c\) = centripetal force.
      11. Limitations of Rutherford's model :
        1. This model could not explain stability of atom
          • Maxwell's theory : any accelerating charged particle emits radiation and loses energy.
          • This counters Rutherford's as electron will collide with the nucleus and atom will be destroyed.
        2. This model could not explain discrete(line) spectrum of atom.
