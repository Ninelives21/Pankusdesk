BOHR'S POSTULATES

  1. Atom has a centre called Nucleus.
  2. Electron revolves only in fixed circular orbits with fixed energy and fixed velocity.
  3. Quantisation Condition: Electrons revolve only in those circular orbits for which the angular momentum (\(L\)) is an integral multiple of \(\dfrac{h}{2\pi}\), or \( L = n \dfrac{h}{2\pi} \)
    where \( h \) is Planck's constant \( (6.626 \times 10^{-34} \, \text{J} \cdot \text{s}) \)
    n → shell no
    n = 1 (K), n = 2 (L), n = 3 (M), n = 4 (N), etc
    • The orbital angular momentum of an electron is the vector quantity defined by the cross product of its position vector and its linear momentum.
    • \( \vec{L} = \vec{r} \times \vec{p} \) (momentum)
    • \( \vec{L} = \vec{r} \times m\vec{v} \)
    • \( \vec{L} = r m v \sin \theta = m v r \sin 90^\circ = m v r \)
      where the vector \( \vec{r} \) represents the position vector of the electron relative to a chosen origin, typically the nucleus of the atom.
    • \( L = mvr \)
    • \( mvr = \dfrac{nh}{2\pi} \) → Equation 1
  4. While revolving, the electrostatic force between the electron (\( e^- \)) and nucleus provides centripetal force.
    \( F_e = F_c \), where \( F_e \) is electrostatic force and \( F_c \) is centripetal force
    • \( F_e = \dfrac{1}{4 \pi \epsilon_0} \dfrac{q_1 q_2}{r^2} \)
      where \( q_1 \) and \( q_2 \) are the charges of the electron and nucleus, \( r \) is the distance between them, and \( \epsilon_0 \) is the permittivity of free space.
    • \( F_c = \dfrac{mv^2}{r} \)
      where \( m \) is the mass of the electron, \( v \) is the velocity of the electron, and \( r \) is the radius of the circular orbit.
    • \( \dfrac{1}{4 \pi \epsilon_0} \dfrac{Ze^2}{r^2} = \dfrac{mv^2}{r} \) → Equation 2
    • Note: Charge on electron is -e and that on proton is +Ze, where Z is the atomic number of the element
  5. While revolving in a particular orbit, an electron neither gains nor loses energy. Energy of an orbit/shell is fixed. Shells → Stationary Energy Levels.
