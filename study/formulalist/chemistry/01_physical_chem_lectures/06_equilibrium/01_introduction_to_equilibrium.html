  • Chemical equilibrium intro

    1. Types of chemical reactions related to equilibrium

      • Equilibrium topic is in two parts
        • Chemical Equilibrium (5 lectures on this)
        • Ionic Equilibrium (8 lectures on this)
      • Definition: Chemical equilibrium is the state in which both the reactants and products are present in concentrations which have no further tendency to change with time.
      • Two types of (any) equilibrium:
        1. Static : example - equal and opposite forces on an object.
        2. Dynamic : example - Water filling in a vessel at the same rate as it is draining from a hole at the bottom.
      • Chemical Equilibrium is DYNAMIC and not Static
      • Equilibrium in the two types of chemical reactions:
        1. Irreversible - Only forward \(\rightarrow\) No Equilibrium in case of irreversible reactions;
          example
          • Acids + Bases -> Salt + water
          • Precipitaion reactions
            \(\text{AgNO}_3 + \text{NaCl} \rightarrow \text{AgCl(ppt)}\downarrow + \text{NaNO}_3\)
          • Combustion reactions
            \(\text{C} + \text{O}_2 \rightarrow \text{CO}_2 + \text{Heat}\)
        2. Reversible reactions - move both forwards and backwards;
          • Initially only forward and after a while backward reaction also starts till the rate of both forward and backwards are the same; the reaction reaches equilibrium.
          • Ex 1 : \(\text{N}_2 + \text{3H}_2 \leftrightarrow \text{2NH}_3\)
          • Ex 2 : \(\text{2SO}_2 + \text{O}_2 \leftrightarrow \text{2SO}_3\)
          • Ex 3 : \(\text{PCl}_5 + Heat \leftrightarrow \text{PCl}_3 + \text{Cl}_2\)
          • Ex 4 : \(\text{2NO} \leftrightarrow \text{N}_2 + \text{O}_2\)
          • Ex 5 : \(\text{H}_2 + \text{I}_2 \leftrightarrow \text{2HI}\)
