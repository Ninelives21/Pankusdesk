  1. Mole & Avogadro Number
    Mole = a number = \(6.022 \times 10^{23}\)
    = Avogadro Number \((A_N)\)
    ex: 1 mole \(O_2\) atom = \(6.022 \times 10^{23}\) \(O_2\) atoms
    ex: 1 mole \(N_2\) molecules = \(6.022 \times 10^{23}\) \(N_2\) molecules
    ex: 1 mole of \(H_2O\) = \(6.022 \times 10^{23}\) \(H_2O\) molecules

  2. Mole Formulae
    (1) No. of moles = mass given / atomic/molecular mass
    (2) No. of moles = Given volume at STP / 22.4 L (or 22.4 \(dm^3\))
    (3) No. of moles = Given # molecules/atoms / \(A_N\)

  4. Stoichiometry
    • Related Chemical Equations (that are balanced)
      • ex1: \(CH_4 + 2\,O_2 \rightarrow CO_2 + 2\,H_2O\)
      • \(\Rightarrow\) 1 mole of \(CH_4\) reacts with 2 moles of \(O_2\)
      • to give 1 mole of \(CO_2\) & 2 moles of \(H_2O\)
      • Q: If 16 g of methane is burnt, find mass of \(CO_2\) produced.
      • \(\Rightarrow\) 16 gm of \(CH_4\) = 1 mole of \(CH_4\)
      • \(\Rightarrow\) 1 mole of \(CO_2\) is produced
      • \(\Rightarrow\) 44 gm of \(CO_2\) is produced.
      • Example 2: Find volume of \(CO_2\) produced at STP in the above.
      • 1 mole of \(CO_2\) = 22.4 L

  5. Questions on Stoichiometry :

      Moles of \(CaCO_3 = \dfrac{50}{100} = 0.5\) mole

      2 moles of butane gives 8 moles of \(CO_2\)
      \(0.1\,M\) of butane \(= 0.1 \times \dfrac{8}{2} = 0.4\,M\) of \(CO_2\)
      mass of \(CO_2\) produced \(= 0.4 \times 44 = 17.6\) gm.
    3. (Solution link on youtube)

      2 \(M\) \(C_4H_{10} = 13\) mole of \(O_2\)
      \(0.1\,M\) \(C_4H_{10} = \dfrac{13}{2} \times 0.1\) of \(O_2\)
      \(= \dfrac{13}{2} \times 0.1 \times 32\) g of \(O_2\)
    4. (Solution link on youtube)

      \(= 0.5 \times (124)\)

    5. (Solution link on youtube)

      5.6 L of \(NO_2\) is \(0.25\,M\) of \(NO_2\)
