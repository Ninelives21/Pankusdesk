  1. Mole & Avogadro Number
    Mole = a number = \(6.022 \times 10^{23}\)
    = Avogadro Number \((A_N)\)
    ex: 1 mole \(O_2\) atom = \(6.022 \times 10^{23}\) \(O_2\) atoms
    ex: 1 mole \(N_2\) molecules = \(6.022 \times 10^{23}\) \(N_2\) molecules
    ex: 1 mole of \(H_2O\) = \(6.022 \times 10^{23}\) \(H_2O\) molecules

  2. Mole Formulae
    (1) No. of moles = mass given / atomic or molecular mass
    (2) No. of moles = Given volume at STP / 22.4 L (or 22.4 \(dm^3\))
    (3) No. of moles = Given number of molecules(or atoms) / \(A_N\)

  4. Stoichiometry
    • Related Chemical Equations (that are balanced)
      • ex1: \(CH_4 + 2\,O_2 \rightarrow CO_2 + 2\,H_2O\)
      • \(\Rightarrow\) 1 mole of \(CH_4\) reacts with 2 moles of \(O_2\)
      • to give 1 mole of \(CO_2\) & 2 moles of \(H_2O\)
      • Q: If 16 g of methane is burnt, find mass of \(CO_2\) produced.
      • \(\Rightarrow\) 16 gm of \(CH_4\) = 1 mole of \(CH_4\)
      • \(\Rightarrow\) 1 mole of \(CO_2\) is produced
      • \(\Rightarrow\) 44 gm of \(CO_2\) is produced.
      • Example 2: Find volume of \(CO_2\) produced at STP in the above.
      • 1 mole of \(CO_2\) = 22.4 L

  6. Empirical Formula, Molecular Formula, and % Age Composition

    1. % Composition

      To find the % of an element in a compound:
      \( \% \text{Composition} = \dfrac{\text{mass of that element in compound}}{\text{mass of compound}} \times 100 \)

      Examples on % composition

      1. Q1: Find % of oxygen in water, \( H_2O \)
        A1 = \( \dfrac{16}{18} \times 100 \)

      2. Q2: Find % of Cl in NaCl
        A2 = \( \dfrac{35.5 \times 100}{35.5 + 23} = \dfrac{35.5 \times 100}{58.5} \)

      3. Q3: Find % of \( O_2 \) in \( H_2SO_4 \)
        A3 = \( \dfrac{64}{2 + 32 + 64} \times 100 \)

      4. Q4: Find % of water in \( MgSO_4 \cdot 7H_2O \)
        A4 = \( \dfrac{7(18)}{(24 + 32 + 64) + 7(18)} \times 100 \)

  7. Molecular Formula & Empirical Formula
    • Molecular formula tells how many actual individual atoms are in a compound
    • Empirical formula — gives the simplest ratio between the component atoms
    Ex 1: Ethane:
    Molecular formula = \(C_2H_6\)
    Empirical formula = \(CH_3\)

    Ex 2: Glucose:
    Molecular formula = \(C_6H_{12}O_6\)
    Empirical formula = \(CH_2O\)

    Ex 3: Butane:
    Molecular formula = \(C_4H_{10}\)
    Empirical formula = \(C_2H_5\)

  8. Relation Between Molecular & Empirical Formula
    • Find ‘\(n\)’. \(n \times\) empirical formula \(=\) molecular formula
    • Ex Ethane, \(n = 2\)
    • \(2(CH_3) = C_2H_6\)

      • Also:
    • \(n \times\) empirical wt \(=\) molecular weight
    • Ex Ethane \(\Rightarrow 2(12 + 3) = 30\)
