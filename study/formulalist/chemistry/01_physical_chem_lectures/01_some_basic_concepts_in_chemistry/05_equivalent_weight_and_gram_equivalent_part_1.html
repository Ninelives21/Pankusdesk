  1. Equivalent weight

    1. Equivalent weight = \(\dfrac{\text{Molecular Weight}} {\text{Valency Factor}}\)
    2. Valency Factor :

      • For acids, valency Factor = basicity
        = how many \(\text{H}^+\) ions will it give in solution

        Example, basicity of \(\text{H}_2{SO}_4\) = 2
        \(\therefore\) for \(\text{H}_2{SO}_4\), valency factor = 2

        But, bear in mind that valency factor also depends on the reaction.

        For example, in the below reaction
        \(\text{H}_2{SO}_4\) + \(\text{NaOH}\) \(\rightarrow\) \(\text{NaH}{SO}_4\) + \(\text{H}_2{O}\), basicity = 1

      • For bases, valency Factor = acidity
        = how many \(\text{OH}^-\) ions will it give in solution

        Example, acidity of \(\text{Al(OH)}_3\) = 3
        \(\therefore\) For \(\text{Al(OH)}_3\), valency factor = 3

      • For salts, valency Factor = total +ve charge on the cation (or total -ve charge on anion).
        = charge on the cation X number of atoms of the cation

        Example, total +ve charge on the cation \(\text{Al}^{3+}\) of \(\text{Al}_2{({SO}_4)}_3\) = (2+) X 3
        \(\therefore\) For \(\text{Al}_2{({SO}_4)}_3\), valency factor = 6

  2. Gram Equivalent

    1. Gram equivalent weight (GEW) = \(\dfrac{\text{Given mass}} {\text{Equivalent weight}}\)
    2. From formula of Equivalent weight, gram equivalent = \(\dfrac{\text{Given mass X Valency }} {\text{Molecular weight}}\)
    3. We know that number of moles = \(\dfrac{\text{Given mass}} {\text{Molecular weight}}\)
    4. \(\therefore\) Gram equivalent = Num of moles X Valency factor

    5. In any chemical reaction, the gram equivalent moles of the reactants will always be the same.

      Example: \(\text{H}_2{SO}_4\) + \(\text{2NaOH}\) \(\rightarrow\) \(\text{Na}_2{SO}_4\) + \(\text{H}_2{O}\)
      We know that GEW = Num of moles X Valency factor
      GEW of \(\text{H}_2{SO}_4\) = 2 X 1 = 2
      GEW of \(\text{2NaOH}\) = 1 X 2 = 2
      GEW of both \(\text{H}_2{SO}_4\) and \(\text{2NaOH}\) is 2


    6. Questions on Equivalent mass / Gram equivalent :


          • Molecular mass of NaOH = 23 + 16 + 1 = 40

          • Gram equivalent = \(\dfrac{53}{53}\) = 1 GEW
