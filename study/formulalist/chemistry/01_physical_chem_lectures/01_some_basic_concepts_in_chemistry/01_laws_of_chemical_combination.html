  2. Law of conservation of mass

    1. Proposed by Antony Lavoiser.
    2. Sum of mass of reactants = sum of mass of products
    3. In other words, mass can neither be created, nor destroyed.
    4. Example 1
      1. Write balanced equation of the reaction.
      2. Find mass of each using mole concept.
      3. Verify total mass of products = total mass of reactants

  3. Law of Definite Proportions

    1. Proposed by Proust.
    2. A given chemical compound always contains the same elements in the exact same proportions by mass.

  4. Law of Multiple Proportions.

    1. Proposed by Dalton.
    2. When two elements combine with each other to form more than one compound, the weights of one element that combine with a fixed weight of the other are in a ratio of small whole numbers.

  5. Gay Lusaac's Law

    1. When gases react, they do so in volume which bears a simple whole number ratio to one another and to the volume of products formed, if gaseous (under the same temperature and pressure conditions).

    2. The balanced chemical equation for the combustion of methane (CH₄) is:

      \( CH_4 + 2O_2 \rightarrow CO_2 + 2H_2O \)

      From the balanced equation, we can see that one mole of methane reacts with two moles of oxygen to produce one mole of carbon dioxide and two moles of water.

      In terms of volume, one volume of methane reacts with two volumes of oxygen to produce one volume of carbon dioxide (assuming all gases are at the same temperature and pressure).

      So, if 100 mL of methane is burned:

      • The volume of oxygen required is 2 * 100 mL = 200 mL.
      • The volume of carbon dioxide produced is 100 mL.

  6. Avogadro's Hypothesis

    1. Equal volumes of all gases, at the same temperature and pressure, have the same number of molecules.
    2. In other words, volume of a gas is directly proportional to number of molecules.

  7. Law of Reciprocal Proportions (Extra Law)

    1. when two elements combine with a fixed amount of a third element, the ratio of their masses is either the same or a whole number multiple
    2. Example : C,S,O combining to form \(\text{CO}_2\), \(\text{SO}_2\), \(\text{CS}_2\)
    3. C : O in \(\text{CO}_2\) = 12 : 32
    4. S : O in \(\text{SO}_2\) = 32 : 32
    5. C : S in \(\text{CS}_2\) = 12 : 64
    6. Here C, and S are combining with a fixed mass (32) of O
    7. The masses of C and S combining with fixed mass of O are 12 and 32 respectively. Expressed as ratio this is 12 : 32
    8. The ratio in which they (C and S) themselves combine to form a third compound \(\text{CS}_2\ is 12 : 64
    9. The two ratios "12 : 32" and "12 : 64" bear a small whole number ratio (2) to each other.

  8. Difference between the laws:

    • Same reactants, only one 1 product => Definite Proportions
    • Same reactants, different products => Multiple Proportions
    • If the reactants and the product are all gases => Gay Lussac's Law
    • Three reactants combining with each other to form three diffent compounds (C,S,O combining to form \(\text{CO}_2\), \(\text{SO}_2\), \(\text{CS}_2\)) => Reciprocal Proportions
