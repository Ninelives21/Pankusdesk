  2. Molarity

    1. Number of moles of a solute present in 1 L of solution.
    2. Formula : \(\dfrac{\text{Number of moles of solute}}{\text{Volume of solution in L}}\) (volume of solution should be in L)
    3. Number of moles = \(\dfrac{\text{Given mass}}{\text{Molecular mass}}\)
    4. Unit of molarity = moles/L = 'M'
    5. Example, molarity of sugar (aq) solution is 2M, \(\implies\) 2 moles of sugar are present in 1L of solution.
    6. Example, molarity of \(\text{H}^+\) ions is 3M, \(\implies\) 3 moles of \(\text{H}^+\) ions are present in 1L of solution.
    7. Molar Solutions :
      1. Molar solution = solution whose molarity = 1 M
      2. Bimolar solution = solution whose molarity = 2 M
      3. Semimolar solution = solution whose molarity = \(\dfrac{1}{2}\) M
      4. Decimolar solution = solution whose molarity = \(\dfrac{1}{10}\) M
      5. Centimolar solution = solution whose molarity = \(\dfrac{1}{100}\) M

    9. Molarity of dilution :
      • Assume I have a solution of molarity \(\text{M}_1\) and volume \(\text{V}_1\).
      • Assume I diluted by adding water, so that it's volume is now \(\text{V}_2\).
      • \(\text{M}_1\) = \(\dfrac{n}{{V}_1}\) \(\implies\) n = \(\text{M}_1\)\(\text{V}_1\)
      • \(\text{M}_2\) = \(\dfrac{n}{{V}_2}\) \(\implies\) n = \(\text{M}_2\)\(\text{V}_2\)

      • \(\therefore\) \(\text{M}_1\)\(\text{V}_1\) = \(\text{M}_2\)\(\text{V}_2\)

      • From the above, \(\text{V}_2\) is greater that \(\text{V}_1\), \(\implies\) \(\text{M}_2\) is less that \(\text{M}_1\)


      • Questions on molarity of dilution
        • \(\text{M}_1\) = 0.2
        • \(\text{V}_1\) = 100 mL = 0.1 L
        • \(\text{M}_2\) = ?
        • \(\text{V}_2\) = 100 + 100 = 200 mL = 0.2 L
        • \(\text{M}_1\)\(\text{V}_1\) = \(\text{M}_2\)\(\text{V}_2\)
        • 0.2 * 0.1 = \(\text{M}_2\) * 0.2
        • \(\text{M}_2\) = 0.1 M


    10. Molarity of Mixture
      • Molarity of Mixture = molarity of a mixture of two (or more) different solutions that have the same solute.
      • Let a solution of molarity \(\text{M}_1\) and volume \(\text{V}_1\) be mixed with another solution (of same solute) of molarity \(\text{M}_2\) and volume \(\text{V}_2\).

      • Number of moles of first solution = \(\text{n}_1\) = \(\text{M}_1\)\(\text{V}_1\)
      • Number of moles of second solution = \(\text{n}_2\) = \(\text{M}_2\)\(\text{V}_2\)
      • Volume of the new mixture of solutions V = \(\text{V}_1\) + \(\text{V}_2\)

      • Molarity of the new mixture of solutions, M = \(\dfrac{n_1 + n_2}{V}\) = \(\dfrac{{M_1V_1 + M_1V_2}}{V_1 + V_2}\)


      • Questions on molarity of mixture
        • Molarity of the mixture, M = \(\dfrac{{M_1V_1 + M_1V_2}}{V_1 + V_2}\)
        • \(\text{M}_1\) = 0.2 M
        • \(\text{V}_1\) = 100 mL = 0.1 L
        • \(\text{M}_2\) = 0.1 M
        • \(\text{V}_2\) = 300 mL = 0.3 L
        • M = \(\dfrac{0.2 X 0.1 + 0.1 * 0.3}{0.1 + 0.3}\)
          = \(\dfrac{0.02 + 0.03}{0.4}\)
          = \(\dfrac{0.02 + 0.03}{0.4}\)
          = \(\dfrac{0.05}{0.4}\)

          = 0.125 M


  3. Molality

    1. Molality = number of moles of solute present in 1 Kg solvent (and not solution).
    2. Molality m = \(\dfrac{\text{Number of moles of solute}}{\text{Mass of solvent in Kg}}\)
    3. Example : Molality of aq HCl is 3 m \(\implies\) 3 moles of HCl are present in 1 Kg of water.

        • Molecular mass of NaOH = 23 + 16 + 1 = 40
        • Moles of NaOH = \(\dfrac{20}{40}\) = 0.5
        • Mass of solvent water = 100 - 20 = 80 gm = 0.08 Kg
        • Molality, m = \(\dfrac{0.5}{0.08}\) = 6.25 m
        • Molecular mass of \(\text{NH}_3\) = 14 + 3 = 17
        • Moles of NaOH = \(\dfrac{0.85}{17}\) = 0.05
        • Volume of solution = 100 mL
        • Mass of solution = volume X density = 120 g
        • Mass of solvent water = 120 - 0.85 = 119.15 gm = 0.11915 Kg
        • Molality, m = \(\dfrac{0.05}{0.11915}\) = 0.42m
