  1. Normality

    1. Normality formula N = \(\dfrac{\text{Num of gm eq of solute}} {\text{Volume of solution in L}}\)
    2. Units = \(\dfrac{\text{Gm eq}} {\text{L}}\)


        • Molecular weight of \(\text{H}_2{SO}_4\) = 2 + 32 + 64 = 98
        • Number of moles = \(\dfrac{0.98}{98}\) = 0.01
        • Valency factor = 2
        • Gram eq = 0.01 X 2 = 0.02 GEW
        • Volume of solution in lites = \(\dfrac{500}{1000}\) = 0.5 L
        • Normality = \(\dfrac{0.02}{0.5}\) = 0.04 N
        • Molecular weight of NaOH = 23 + 16 + 1 = 40
        • Valency factor = 1
        • Equivalent weight = \(\dfrac{40}{1}\) = 40
        • Gram equivalent weight = \(\dfrac{given mass ('x')}{40}\)
        • Normality = 0.5 = \(\dfrac{x}{40 * 0.25}\)
        • x = 0.5 * 10 = 5 gm



  2. Relationship between Molarity and Normality

      • Molarity = M = \(\dfrac{\text{Number of moles of solute}}{\text{Volume of solution in L}}\)
      • Normality N = \(\dfrac{\text{Num of gm eq of solute}} {\text{Volume of solution in L}}\)
      • \(\dfrac{N}{M}\) = \(\dfrac{\text{Num of gm eq of solute}} {\text{Num of moles of solute}}\)

      • \(\dfrac{N}{M}\) = \(\dfrac{\text{\(\dfrac{\text{mass}}{\text{eq mass}}\)}} {\text{\(\dfrac{\text{mass}}{\text{mol wt}}\)}}\)
      • \(\dfrac{N}{M}\) = \(\dfrac{\text{mol mass}} {\text{eq mass}}\) = Valency factor (X)
      • N = M * X


      • \(\dfrac{N}{M}\) = \(\dfrac{\text{mol mass}} {\text{eq mass}}\) = Valency factor (X)
      • M = \(\dfrac{N}{X}\)
      • N = 0.2 N
      • X = 2 (basicity of \(\text{H}_2{SO}_4\))
      • M = \(\dfrac{0.2}{2}\) = 0.1 M

      • N = M * X
      • M = 0.4
      • X = 3 (basicity of \(\text{H}_3{PO}_4\))
      • N = 0.4 * 3 = 1.2



  3. Normality of Mixture : Acid/Acid + Base/Base

      • Let a solution of normality \(\text{N}_1\) and volume \(\text{V}_1\) be mixed with another solution (acid/acid or base/base) of normality \(\text{N}_2\) and volume \(\text{V}_2\).
      • Normality of the new mixture of solutions, N = \(\dfrac{{N_1V_1 + N_2V_2}}{V_1 + V_2}\)
      • Normality of the new mixture of solutions, N = \(\dfrac{{N_1V_1 + N_2V_2}}{V_1 + V_2}\)
      • = \(\dfrac{{0.2 * 200 + 0.3 * 300}}{500}\)
      • = 0.26 N



  4. Normality of Mixture Acid + Base

      • Let an acid solution of normality \(\text{N}_a\) and volume \(\text{V}_a\) be mixed with a basic solution of normality \(\text{N}_b\) and volume \(\text{V}_b\).


          • \(\text{N}_a{V}_a\) > \(\text{N}_b{V}_b\), resultant mixture is acidic
          • N (\(\text{H}^+\)) of the resultant mixture = \(\dfrac{{N_aV_a - N_bV_b}}{V_a + V_b}\)

          • \(\text{N}_a{V}_a\) < \(\text{N}_b{V}_b\), resultant mixture is basic
          • N (\(\text{OH}^-\)) of the resultant mixture = \(\dfrac{{ N_bV_b - N_aV_a}}{V_a + V_b}\)

          • \(\text{N}_a{V}_a\) = \(\text{N}_b{V}_b\), resultant mixture is completely neutralized.

      • \(\text{N}_a{V}_a\) = 0.2 * 100 = 20 GEW
      • \(\text{N}_b{V}_b\) = 0.3 * 200 = 60 GEW
      • \(\implies\) the resultant solution is basic.
      • Normality of the new mixture of solutions, N = \(\dfrac{{N_bV_b - N_aV_a}}{V_a + V_b}\)
      • = \(\dfrac{{60 - 20}}{300}\)
      • = 0.133 N
      • If complete neutralization is occuring, \(\text{N}_a{V}_a\) =\(\text{N}_b{V}_b\)
      • 0.4 * 500 = \(\text{N}_b\) * 300
      • \(\text{N}_b\) = 0.66 N
