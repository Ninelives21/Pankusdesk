Functional Groups with Secondary Suffix

  1. Introduction to functional groups
    1. Functional groups are atoms or group of atoms that give all physical as well as chemical properties to a compound.
    2. Functional Groups come as secondary suffix in a compound.
    3. Terminal Function Group (TFG) is the FG at the end of a carbon chain - ex: Acohol, Aldehyde, Acid. Numbering starts from the carbon in the FG.
    4. As per IUPAC, the following are not funtional groups, even though they actually are as they determine the property of the compound:
      • double bond
      • triple bond
      • Cl, Br, I, \(\text{NO}_2\)
