    2. Naming of Bicyclo compounds
      • Naming rules with an example :


        1. The line joining the two common C-atoms is called the "bridge and the two C-atoms are called "bridge head carbon".
        2. The prefix for Bicyclo is "bicyclo" followed by square brackets ("[ ]").
        3. There are actually three chains: the right, left and the bridge.
        4. Count the number of C-atoms in each chain, excluding the bridge heads.
        5. While naming, go from the largest to the smallest of the above three chains.
        6. In the above figure, the right chain has two, the left has two, and the bridge chain has one.
        7. right = 2, left = 2, bridge = 1, and total number of carbons including the bridge heads is 7.
        8. The name will be bicyclo[2.2.1] heptane
        9. Substituent naming rules : : start numbering from the bridge head carbon, then largest chain, and then smallest chain.
        10. Do not change this rule upon seeing the functional group. It should go from largest to smallest (and then bridge) only.
        11. Within the chain, as usual, number in a way to give the FG/substituent smallest number.
        12. Can pick any bridge head carbon to start the numbering.
        13. Example : question 4 below.

    3. Naming of Spiro compounds
      • Naming rules with an example :


        1. The prefix for spiro is "cyclo" followed by square brackets ("[ ]").
        2. The common C-atom is called the "\(\text{4}^o\) Carbon" as there are 4 C-atoms attached to it.
        3. Count the number of C-atoms in each chain attached to this common C-atom (excluding the common C-atom).
        4. While naming, go from the smallest to largest chain.
        5. In the above figure, the smaller right chain has two and the larger left has three.
        6. The name will be cyclo[3.2] hexane
        7. Numbering of spiro in case of substituents / functional group : numbering starts from the \(\text{4}^o\) Carbon, and goes from smallest to largest.
        8. Within the chain, as usual, number in a way to give the FG/substituent smallest number.
        9. Do not change this rule upon seeing the functional group. It should go from smallest to largest only.
        10. After naming the smallest chain, add number to the \(\text{4}^o\) Carbon.
