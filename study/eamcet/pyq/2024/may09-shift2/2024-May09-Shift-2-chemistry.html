    • Explanation: This is a direct photoelectric effect question. We use Einstein’s photoelectric equation, subtract the given kinetic energy from the incoming photon energy, and then divide by Planck’s constant to get the threshold frequency.
    • Step-by-step explanation:
      • Given:
        • Incident frequency, \( \nu = 1.0 \times 10^{15} \text{ Hz} \)
        • Kinetic energy of emitted electron, \( \text{KE} = 2 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J} \)
        • Planck’s constant, \( h = 6.6 \times 10^{-34} \text{ Js} \)
      • Einstein’s photoelectric equation is:
        • \( h\nu = h\nu_0 + \text{KE} \)
      • Rearrange to find threshold frequency \( \nu_0 \):
        • \( h\nu_0 = h\nu - \text{KE} \)
        • \( \nu_0 = \dfrac{h\nu - \text{KE}}{h} \)
      • Now substitute the values:
        • \( \nu_0 = \dfrac{(6.6 \times 10^{-34})(1.0 \times 10^{15}) - 2 \times 10^{-19}}{6.6 \times 10^{-34}} \)
      • First calculate photon energy:
        • \( h\nu = 6.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J} \)
      • Now subtract kinetic energy:
        • \( h\nu - \text{KE} = 6.6 \times 10^{-19} - 2 \times 10^{-19} = 4.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J} \)
      • Now divide by \(h\):
        • \( \nu_0 = \dfrac{4.6 \times 10^{-19}}{6.6 \times 10^{-34}} \)
        • \( \nu_0 \approx 0.697 \times 10^{15} = 6.97 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz} \)
    • Concept used / theory behind it:
      • In the photoelectric effect, light comes in packets called photons.
      • Each photon has energy \( h\nu \).
      • A part of this energy is used to remove the electron from the metal surface. That minimum required energy is called the work function.
      • The remaining energy appears as the kinetic energy of the emitted electron.
      • The threshold frequency \( \nu_0 \) is the minimum frequency needed to just eject electrons from the metal surface.
      • At threshold, the emitted electron has zero kinetic energy, so \( h\nu_0 = \phi \), where \( \phi \) is the work function.
    • Why the correct option is right:
      • Option (d) matches the correct numerical value obtained from Einstein’s equation.
      • The value is less than the incident frequency, which makes physical sense, because the incoming light is energetic enough to eject electrons and also give them some kinetic energy.

      • (b) is too small and does not match the calculation.

        • \( h\nu = h\nu_0 + \text{KE} \)
      • If electrons are emitted, then the incident frequency must be greater than threshold frequency.
