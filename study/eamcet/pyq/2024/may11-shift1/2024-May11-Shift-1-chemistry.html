    Answer: \((a)\ X_5Y_8\)

    Solution:

    Given:

    • \(X\) atoms are present at the corners
    • \(Y\) atoms are present at the body centre
    • The unit cell is bcc
    • Three corner atoms are missing
    1. Find the contribution of corner atoms

      In any cubic unit cell, each corner atom is shared by \(8\) adjacent unit cells.

      So contribution of one corner atom to one unit cell is:

      \(\dfrac{1}{8}\)

      A bcc unit cell has \(8\) corners, so total contribution of \(X\) atoms from corners would normally be:

      \(8 \times \dfrac{1}{8} = 1\)

    2. Account for the missing corner atoms

      Three corner atoms are missing.

      Since each corner contributes \(\dfrac{1}{8}\), the missing contribution is:

      \(3 \times \dfrac{1}{8} = \dfrac{3}{8}\)

      Therefore, effective number of \(X\) atoms present in the unit cell is:

      \(1 - \dfrac{3}{8} = \dfrac{5}{8}\)

    3. Find the contribution of body-centred atom

      In bcc, the atom at the body centre lies completely inside the unit cell.

      So its contribution is:

      \(1\)

      Therefore, effective number of \(Y\) atoms in the unit cell is:

      \(1\)

    4. Write the ratio and formula

      So,

      \(X:Y = \dfrac{5}{8}:1\)

      Multiply both sides by \(8\):

      \(X:Y = 5:8\)

      Hence the formula of the compound is:

      \(\boxed{X_5Y_8}\)

    Therefore:

    \(\boxed{(a)\ X_5Y_8}\)

    Why this method works:

    • In unit-cell formula questions, we always count effective number of atoms contributed to one unit cell.
    • Corner atoms are shared, so they do not contribute fully.
    • Body-centred atoms are completely inside the cell, so they contribute fully.
    • Once the effective numbers are found, their ratio directly gives the empirical formula.

    JEE / NCERT takeaway:

    • Corner atom contribution:

      \(\dfrac{1}{8}\)

    • Face-centred atom contribution:

      \(\dfrac{1}{2}\)

    • Edge-centred atom contribution:

      \(\dfrac{1}{4}\)

    • Body-centred atom contribution:

      \(1\)

    • In a perfect bcc unit cell:

      \(8 \times \dfrac{1}{8} + 1 = 2\)

    • So a normal bcc structure has \(2\) effective atoms per unit cell.
    • If vacancies are introduced, subtract the missing fractional contribution from the total.

    1-minute solving trick:

    • The moment you see bcc + corners + body centre, recall:

      \(\text{corners} = 1,\quad \text{body centre} = 1\)

    • Then adjust only for the missing corner atoms:

      \(X = 1 - \dfrac{3}{8} = \dfrac{5}{8}\)

    • Since \(Y=1\),

      \(X:Y = \dfrac{5}{8}:1 = 5:8\)

    • So directly:

      \(X_5Y_8\)

    More intuitive shortcut:

    • Think of all \(8\) corner atoms together as contributing exactly \(1\) atom.
    • If \(3\) corners are absent, you have lost \(\dfrac{3}{8}\) of that \(1\) atom.
    • So corner contribution becomes:

      \(\dfrac{5}{8}\)

    • The centre atom remains fully present:

      \(1\)

    • Convert the fractional ratio into whole numbers by multiplying by \(8\).

    Common mistakes to avoid:

    1. Treating each corner atom as a full atom instead of \(\dfrac{1}{8}\).
    2. Subtracting \(3\) directly from \(1\), which is wrong.
    3. Forgetting that body-centred atom contributes fully.
    4. Writing the ratio as \(\dfrac{5}{8}:1\) and stopping there without converting it to whole numbers.
    5. Reversing the order and writing \(Y_8X_5\) when the compound is asked in the order \(X\) then \(Y\).

    Final exam-style answer:

    In bcc lattice, \(X\) atoms are present at corners and \(Y\) atom is present at body centre.

    Contribution of \(8\) corner atoms:

    \(8 \times \dfrac{1}{8} = 1\)

    Since \(3\) corner atoms are missing, effective number of \(X\) atoms becomes:

    \(X = 1 - \dfrac{3}{8} = \dfrac{5}{8}\)

    Body-centred atom contributes fully, so:

    \(Y = 1\)

    Therefore,

    \(X:Y = \dfrac{5}{8}:1 = 5:8\)

    Hence, the formula of the compound is:

    \(\boxed{X_5Y_8}\)

    Quick cheatsheet:

    • Corner atom:

      \(\dfrac{1}{8}\)

    • Face-centred atom:

      \(\dfrac{1}{2}\)

    • Edge-centred atom:

      \(\dfrac{1}{4}\)

    • Body-centred atom:

      \(1\)

    • Perfect bcc:

      \(8 \times \dfrac{1}{8} + 1 = 2\)

    • Vacancy rule:

      \(\text{effective atoms} = \text{normal contribution} - \text{missing contribution}\)

    • Formula rule:

      Convert fractional ratio into smallest whole-number ratio.
