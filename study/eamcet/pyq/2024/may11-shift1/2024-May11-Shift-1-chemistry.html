      • The most probable speed is: \( v_{\mathrm{mp}}=\sqrt{\dfrac{2RT}{M}} \)
      • According to the question: \( v_{\mathrm{rms}} \) of \(SO_2\) at \(400\ \mathrm{K}\) \(=\) most probable speed of \(O_2\) at temperature \(T\).
      • So, \( \sqrt{\dfrac{3R(400)}{M_{SO_2}}}=\sqrt{\dfrac{2RT}{M_{O_2}}} \)
      • Squaring both sides: \( \dfrac{3\times 400}{M_{SO_2}}=\dfrac{2T}{M_{O_2}} \)
      • Molar mass of \(SO_2 = 32 + 2\times 16 = 64\).
      • Molar mass of \(O_2 = 32\).
      • Substitute: \( \dfrac{3\times 400}{64}=\dfrac{2T}{32} \)
      • This becomes: \( \dfrac{1200}{64}=\dfrac{T}{16} \)
      • So, \( 18.75 = \dfrac{T}{16} \)
      • Hence, \( T = 18.75 \times 16 = 300\ \mathrm{K} \)
    • Concept used / theory behind it
      • From kinetic theory of gases, different characteristic molecular speeds are:
        • Most probable speed: \( v_{\mathrm{mp}}=\sqrt{\dfrac{2RT}{M}} \)
        • Average speed: \( v_{\mathrm{avg}}=\sqrt{\dfrac{8RT}{\pi M}} \)
        • RMS speed: \( v_{\mathrm{rms}}=\sqrt{\dfrac{3RT}{M}} \)
      • For the same gas, \( v_{\mathrm{rms}} > v_{\mathrm{avg}} > v_{\mathrm{mp}} \).
      • All these speeds increase with temperature and decrease with molar mass.
      • So whenever two speeds are compared, write the correct formula first. That is the key step.
