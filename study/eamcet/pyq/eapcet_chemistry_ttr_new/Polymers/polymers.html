    1. Identify the polymer from its use

      The question describes a polymer used for making unbreakable cups and laminated sheets.

      • \((a)\) urea and formaldehyde

      • \((b)\) ethylene glycol and phthalic acid

      • \((c)\) phenol and formaldehyde

        This forms bakelite, which is a different thermosetting polymer.

    3. Hence

    • This is a direct polymer identification question.
    • The fastest way is to connect:

      \(\text{polymer name} \leftrightarrow \text{monomers} \leftrightarrow \text{common use}\)

    • Once the polymer is identified as urea-formaldehyde resin, its monomers follow immediately.

    Solution:

    1. Identify compound \(P\)

      Ethylene reacts with cold, dilute alkaline \(KMnO_4\) at \(273\ K\) to give a vicinal diol.

      \(CH_2{=}CH_2 \xrightarrow[\ 273\ K\ ]{\text{cold, dilute alkaline }KMnO_4} HO{-}CH_2{-}CH_2{-}OH\)

      So compound \(P\) is ethylene glycol (ethane-\(1,2\)-diol).

    2. Now recall how dacron is formed

      Dacron is a polyester formed by condensation polymerisation of:

      • a diol: ethylene glycol \(HO{-}CH_2{-}CH_2{-}OH\)
      • a dicarboxylic acid: terephthalic acid \(HOOC{-}C_6H_4{-}COOH\)

      Therefore \(P\) must react with terephthalic acid.

      Among the given options, terephthalic acid is \((b)\).

    3. Polymer formed

      \(n\ HO{-}CH_2{-}CH_2{-}OH + n\ HOOC{-}C_6H_4{-}COOH \rightarrow \left[{-}O{-}CH_2{-}CH_2{-}O{-}CO{-}C_6H_4{-}CO{-}\right]_n + 2nH_2O\)

      This polymer is dacron (also called PET in polymer chemistry context).



    Why \((b)\) is correct:

    • Dacron is always obtained from a diol + dicarboxylic acid.
    • Ethylene gives ethylene glycol on hydroxylation with cold dilute alkaline \(KMnO_4\).
    • The required aromatic dicarboxylic acid for dacron is terephthalic acid, i.e. benzene-\(1,4\)-dicarboxylic acid.

    NCERT / JEE theory behind this question:

    • Cold, dilute alkaline \(KMnO_4\) is used for hydroxylation of alkenes.
    • Alkenes under these mild conditions form vicinal glycols (adjacent carbon atoms each get one \(OH\) group).
    • Thus:

      \(RCH{=}CHR \rightarrow RCH(OH)-CH(OH)R\)

    • Ethylene therefore gives ethylene glycol.
    • Polyesters are formed by condensation polymerisation between:
      • a compound with two \(-OH\) groups
      • a compound with two \(-COOH\) groups
    • Dacron is the classic polyester made from:
      • ethylene glycol
      • terephthalic acid

    • See cold dilute alkaline \(KMnO_4\) \(\Rightarrow\) immediately think: alkene \(\rightarrow\) glycol.
    • Ethylene \(\rightarrow\) ethylene glycol.
    • Now see the word dacron \(\Rightarrow\) remember: ethylene glycol + terephthalic acid.
    • So directly mark the option containing \(HOOC{-}C_6H_4{-}COOH\) in para position.

    Fast memory link:

    • Dacron = Diol + Diacid

      • Next, \( \mathrm{Zn/HCl} \) reduces the nitro group \( (\mathrm{-NO_2}) \) to amino group \( (\mathrm{-NH_2}) \).
      • Then treatment with \( \mathrm{NaNO_2/HCl} \) at \(0{-}5^\circ\mathrm{C}\) diazotises the amino group to form the diazonium salt \(Q\).
      • Reaction of aromatic diazonium salt with \( \mathrm{CuCN} \) replaces the diazonium group by \( \mathrm{-CN} \) through Sandmeyer reaction.
      • Subsequent hydrolysis converts \( \mathrm{-CN} \) into \( \mathrm{-COOH} \).
      • Thus the final product \(R\) is benzene-1,4-dicarboxylic acid, that is terephthalic acid.

      • Dacron is a polyester formed by condensation polymerisation of:
        • terephthalic acid, and
        • ethylene glycol.
      • Since \(R\) is terephthalic acid, the polymer must be dacron.
    • Concept used / theory behind it
      • Side chain oxidation: Alkyl side chains on benzene ring are oxidised by \( \mathrm{KMnO_4} \) to carboxylic acid if benzylic hydrogen is present.
      • Reduction of nitro group: \( \mathrm{-NO_2} \rightarrow \mathrm{-NH_2} \).
      • Diazotisation: Aromatic primary amines react with nitrous acid at low temperature to form diazonium salts.
      • Sandmeyer reaction: Diazonium group can be replaced by \( \mathrm{CN} \), \( \mathrm{Cl} \), \( \mathrm{Br} \), etc.
      • Nitrile hydrolysis: \( \mathrm{-CN} \rightarrow \mathrm{-COOH} \).
