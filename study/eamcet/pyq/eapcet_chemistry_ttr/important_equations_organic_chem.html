IMPORTANT EQUATIONS

    1. Wurtz reaction and aromatisation chain

      \( \mathrm{2R-X + 2Na \xrightarrow{\mathrm{dry\ ether}} R-R + 2NaX} \)

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_{14} \xrightarrow[\mathrm{10{-}20\,atm}]{\mathrm{Cr_2O_3,\ 773\,K}} C_6H_6 + 4H_2} \)

      Wurtz coupling gives higher alkanes; hexane aromatises to benzene.

    2. Friedel-Crafts alkylation and acylation

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_6 + CH_3Cl \xrightarrow{\mathrm{anhy.\ AlCl_3}} C_6H_5CH_3 + HCl} \)

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_6 + CH_3COCl \xrightarrow{\mathrm{anhy.\ AlCl_3}} C_6H_5COCH_3 + HCl} \)

      Alkylation gives toluene; acylation gives acetophenone.

    3. Alkyne formation, trimerisation and hydration

      \( \mathrm{CH_2BrCH_2Br \xrightarrow[\mathrm{then\ NaNH_2}]{\mathrm{alc.\ KOH}} HC{\equiv}CH} \)

      \( \mathrm{3HC{\equiv}CH \xrightarrow[\mathrm{red\ hot\ Fe\ tube}]{873\,K} C_6H_6} \)

      \( \mathrm{CH_3C{\equiv}CH \xrightarrow{\mathrm{H_2O,\ Hg^{2+}/H^+}} CH_3COCH_3} \)

      Terminal alkyne hydration gives a methyl ketone after enol-keto tautomerism.

    4. Peroxide effect and normal HBr addition

      \( \mathrm{CH_3CH{=}CH_2 + HBr \xrightarrow{\mathrm{ROOR}} CH_3CH_2CH_2Br} \)

      \( \mathrm{CH_3CH{=}CH_2 + HBr \rightarrow CH_3CHBrCH_3} \)

      Peroxide effect gives anti-Markovnikov addition only for \(\mathrm{HBr}\).

    5. Ozonolysis and reverse ozonolysis

      \( \mathrm{R_1R_2C{=}CR_3R_4 \xrightarrow[\mathrm{Zn/H_2O}]{O_3} R_1R_2C{=}O + O{=}CR_3R_4} \)

      Reductive ozonolysis gives aldehydes/ketones, not acids.

      Reverse ozonolysis: join the two carbonyl carbons back by \(\mathrm{C{=}C}\).

    6. \(\mathrm{KMnO_4}\): cold hydroxylation vs hot cleavage

      \( \mathrm{CH_2{=}CH_2 \xrightarrow[\mathrm{273\,K}]{\mathrm{cold,\ dil.\ alkaline\ KMnO_4}} HOCH_2CH_2OH} \)

      \( \mathrm{CH_3CH{=}CHCH_3 \xrightarrow[\Delta]{\mathrm{hot,\ acidic\ KMnO_4}} 2CH_3COOH} \)

      Cold dilute alkaline \(\mathrm{KMnO_4}\) gives glycol; hot acidic \(\mathrm{KMnO_4}\) cleaves the double bond.

    7. Hydroboration-oxidation of alkenes

      \( \mathrm{CH_3CH{=}CH_2 \xrightarrow[\mathrm{H_2O_2/OH^-}]{\mathrm{B_2H_6}} CH_3CH_2CH_2OH} \)

      Hydroboration-oxidation gives anti-Markovnikov alcohol.

    8. Kolbe electrolysis of acetate

      \( \mathrm{2CH_3COO^- \xrightarrow{\mathrm{electrolysis}} C_2H_6 + 2CO_2 + 2e^-} \)

      Acetate radicals couple to give ethane, not methane.

    9. Aromatic side-chain oxidation and Etard reaction

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5CH_3 \xrightarrow[\mathrm{H_3O^+}]{\mathrm{CrO_2Cl_2/CS_2}} C_6H_5CHO} \)

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5CH_3 \xrightarrow[\mathrm{then\ H_3O^+}]{\mathrm{KMnO_4/KOH,\ \Delta}} C_6H_5COOH} \)

      Etard stops at benzaldehyde; hot alkaline \(\mathrm{KMnO_4}\) gives benzoic acid.

    10. Soda-lime decarboxylation

      \( \mathrm{RCOONa + NaOH \xrightarrow[\mathrm{CaO}]{\Delta} RH + Na_2CO_3} \)

      \( \mathrm{CH_3COONa + NaOH \xrightarrow[\mathrm{CaO}]{\Delta} CH_4 + Na_2CO_3} \)

      Sodium acetate gives methane; sodium benzoate gives benzene.

    11. Core organic test reactions

      \( \mathrm{RCHO/RCOR' + 2,4\text{-}DNP \rightarrow orange/yellow\ precipitate} \)

      \( \mathrm{R-C{\equiv}C-H + Na \rightarrow R-C{\equiv}CNa + \dfrac{1}{2}H_2} \)

      \( \mathrm{C_2H_5OH/CH_3COCH_3 \xrightarrow{\mathrm{I_2/NaOH}} CHI_3 \downarrow} \)

      \(2,4\)-DNP detects carbonyls; sodium detects terminal alkynes; iodoform test detects \(\mathrm{CH_3CO-}\) or \(\mathrm{CH_3CH(OH)-}\).

    12. Lassaigne nitrogen test and Kjeldahl rule

      \( \mathrm{Na + C + N \rightarrow NaCN} \)

      \( \mathrm{H_2SO_4 + 2NH_3 \rightarrow (NH_4)_2SO_4} \)

      Lassaigne nitrogen test gives Prussian blue through cyanide chemistry.

      Kjeldahl works for amine-type nitrogen, not nitro, azo, diazonium or pyridine-type nitrogen.
