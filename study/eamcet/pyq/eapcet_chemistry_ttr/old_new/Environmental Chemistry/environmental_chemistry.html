Environmental Chemistry

    Smog pattern map
  • Photochemical smog = oxidising smog.
  • Main photochemical smog components repeatedly asked: \(O_3,\ NO,\ NO_2,\ PAN,\) formaldehyde (methanal), acrolein.
  • \(\mathrm{SO_2}\) is not a common component of photochemical smog; it belongs to classical/reducing smog memory map.
  • If the question asks for a lung irritant causing acute respiratory disease in children, think \(\mathrm{NO_2}\).
  • Acrolein structure to memorise: \(\mathrm{CH_2=CH-CHO}\).
  • Carbon-containing components of photochemical smog commonly listed in PYQs: acrolein, methanal, PAN.

    Ozone depletion map
  • Stratospheric ozone depletion is linked mainly with CFCs and nitrogen oxides.
  • \(\mathrm{CF_2Cl_2}\) is a classic ozone-depleting substance.
  • \(\mathrm{CH_4}\) is not primarily responsible for depletion of the ozone layer in the standard NCERT-style framing.
  • If the options mix \(\mathrm{NO}\), CFCs, chlorine species and \(\mathrm{CH_4}\), the “not primarily responsible” choice is usually \(\mathrm{CH_4}\).

    Greenhouse and rain-water facts
  • Direct greenhouse gases used in these PYQs: \(\mathrm{CO_2,\ CH_4,\ H_2O(g)}\).
  • \(\mathrm{H_2O(l)}\) is not counted as a greenhouse gas.
  • \(\mathrm{NO}\) and \(\mathrm{O_2}\) are not counted as greenhouse gases in the given list-based PYQ.
  • Normal rain water is slightly acidic: pH \(\approx 5.6\), not \(7.5\).
  • Why \(5.6\)? Because atmospheric \(\mathrm{CO_2}\) dissolves to form weak carbonic acid.

    Drinking water standards and water pollution
  • Methemoglobinemia (blue baby syndrome) is due to excess nitrate concentration in drinking water.
  • Memorise the standard pair asked here: copper \(= 1\ \mathrm{mg\,dm^{-3}}\), zinc \(= 15\ \mathrm{mg\,dm^{-3}}\).
  • If copper is written as \(x\ \mathrm{mg\,dm^{-3}}\), the zinc expression in that PYQ becomes \(\dfrac{15}{x}\).
  • Eutrophication causes depletion of dissolved oxygen in water.
  • Extra nutrients \(\rightarrow\) algal bloom \(\rightarrow\) decomposition \(\rightarrow\) dissolved oxygen falls.

    Solid waste and industry map
  • Thermal power plants are a standard source of non-biodegradable waste because of fly ash.
  • When the options include paper mills, cotton mills, textile factories, and thermal power plants, choose thermal power plants.

    One-line elimination cues
  • Photochemical smog \(\rightarrow\) sunlight + \(NO_x\) + hydrocarbons \(\rightarrow\) oxidising pollutants.
  • Classical smog \(\rightarrow\) \(\mathrm{SO_2}\) + smoke + fog.
  • Ozone depletion \(\rightarrow\) think CFCs first.
  • Blue baby syndrome \(\rightarrow\) nitrate, not fluoride or sulphate.
  • Eutrophication \(\rightarrow\) dissolved oxygen decreases, not increases.

  1. Scope of Environmental Chemistry
    • Environmental chemistry studies chemical changes in air, water, and soil, and the effect of pollutants on living systems.
    • In the TG EAPCET syllabus, this chapter includes air, water, and soil pollution; smog; greenhouse effect; ozone depletion; drinking-water standards; waste management; and green chemistry.

  2. Air pollution and atmospheric pollutants
    • Air pollution means the presence of undesirable solid, liquid, or gaseous substances in the atmosphere at harmful concentrations.
    • Important gaseous air pollutants in the syllabus are oxides of sulphur, oxides of nitrogen, hydrocarbons, and oxides of carbon such as \(CO\) and \(CO_2\).
    • \(\mathrm{NO_2}\) is a strong respiratory irritant and can cause acute respiratory disease in children.
    • \(\mathrm{SO_2}\) affects both humans and plants; one standard effect asked in PYQs is stiffness of flower buds.

  3. Photochemical smog
    • Photochemical smog forms in warm, dry, sunny conditions.
    • It is produced when sunlight acts on nitrogen oxides and hydrocarbons.
    • It is called oxidising smog because it contains oxidising agents such as \(O_3\), \(NO_x\), and PAN.
    • Common harmful products include ozone, PAN, formaldehyde (methanal), and acrolein.
    • \(\mathrm{SO_2}\) is not a common component of photochemical smog; that is a standard elimination fact.
    • Acrolein is propenal, with structure \(\mathrm{CH_2=CH-CHO}\).

  4. Classical smog versus photochemical smog
    • Classical smog is also called reducing smog.
    • It is associated mainly with cool, humid conditions and contains smoke, fog, and \(\mathrm{SO_2}\).
    • Photochemical smog is oxidising and is associated with \(NO_x\), ozone, PAN, and aldehydes.
    • Many objective questions simply test whether you can separate these two smog types quickly.

  5. Ozone layer depletion and stratospheric pollution
    • The ozone layer in the stratosphere protects life by absorbing harmful ultraviolet radiation.
    • Ozone depletion is mainly linked to chlorofluorocarbons and nitrogen oxides.
    • \(\mathrm{CF_2Cl_2}\) is a classic CFC example repeatedly used in ozone-depletion questions.
    • In the given PYQ, \(\mathrm{CH_4}\) is the compound that is not primarily responsible for depletion of the ozone layer.

  6. Greenhouse effect and global warming
    • Greenhouse gases absorb outgoing infrared radiation and warm the atmosphere.
    • Common greenhouse gases include \(\mathrm{CO_2}\), \(\mathrm{CH_4}\), and \(\mathrm{H_2O(g)}\).
    • Liquid water is not counted as a greenhouse gas.
    • In the list-based PYQ here, \(\mathrm{NO}\) and \(\mathrm{O_2}\) are not counted as greenhouse gases.

  7. Acid rain and normal rain water
    • Normal rain water is slightly acidic, with pH around \(5.6\).
    • This is because atmospheric carbon dioxide dissolves in rain water and forms weak carbonic acid.
    • So any option claiming normal rain water has pH \(7.5\) is false.
    • If the pH falls much below the normal value, the rain is termed acid rain.

  8. Water pollution and drinking-water standards
    • Water pollution arises when harmful substances enter water bodies and make them unfit for use.
    • Safe drinking water must satisfy prescribed concentration limits for harmful ions and pollutants.
    • A direct factual limit used in the PYQ set is: copper \(= 1\ \mathrm{mg\,dm^{-3}}\), zinc \(= 15\ \mathrm{mg\,dm^{-3}}\).
    • These one-line values can be tested as ratio or expression-based questions.

  9. Methemoglobinemia and nitrate pollution
    • Methemoglobinemia is also called blue baby syndrome.
    • It is caused by excess nitrate concentration in drinking water.
    • This is one of the most standard factual questions from Environmental Chemistry and should be memorised directly.

  10. Eutrophication
    • Eutrophication is the excessive enrichment of water bodies with nutrients such as nitrates and phosphates.
    • This causes algal bloom and rapid growth of aquatic plants.
    • When this biomass decomposes, microbes consume dissolved oxygen.
    • Therefore, eutrophication leads to decrease in dissolved oxygen.

  11. Soil pollution and non-biodegradable waste
    • Soil pollution can result from pesticides, industrial wastes, and improper disposal of solids.
    • Non-biodegradable wastes do not break down naturally in a short time.
    • Thermal power plants are a standard source of non-biodegradable waste because they produce fly ash.

  12. Fast recall list for EAPCET
    • Photochemical smog: \(O_3,\ PAN,\ aldehydes,\ NO_x\).
    • Classical smog: smoke + fog + \(\mathrm{SO_2}\).
    • Lung irritant in children: \(\mathrm{NO_2}\).
    • Blue baby syndrome: nitrate in drinking water.
    • Normal rain-water pH: \(5.6\).
    • Ozone depletion: think CFCs first.
    • Acrolein: \(\mathrm{CH_2=CH-CHO}\).
    • Eutrophication: dissolved oxygen decreases.
