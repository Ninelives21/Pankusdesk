General Principles of Metallurgy

    Concentration of ores
  • Leaching is the repeated high-yield fact here: bauxite is concentrated/purified by leaching in Bayer’s process.
  • Silver extraction with cyanide, \[ 4Ag + 8CN^- + 2H_2O + O_2 \rightarrow 4[Ag(CN)_2]^- + 4OH^- \] also represents leaching.
  • If the question asks “ore of which metal is concentrated by leaching?”, think aluminium first.
  • Direct ore mapping repeatedly asked: sphalerite \(=\mathrm{ZnS}\), siderite \(=\mathrm{FeCO_3}\), malachite \(=\mathrm{CuCO_3\cdot Cu(OH)_2}\), calamine \(=\mathrm{ZnCO_3}\), kaolinite \(=\) aluminium silicate mineral.

    Extraction of iron and copper
  • Blast furnace iron extraction: acidic gangue \(=\mathrm{SiO_2}\); added flux \(=\mathrm{CaCO_3}\).
  • Remember the fixed iron-metallurgy pair: \[ \mathrm{CaCO_3 \xrightarrow{\Delta} CaO + CO_2,\quad CaO + SiO_2 \rightarrow CaSiO_3} \]
  • Copper extraction trap: standard slag is ferrous silicate, not calcium silicate.
  • So \[ \mathrm{FeO + SiO_2 \rightarrow FeSiO_3} \] belongs to copper extraction, but \[ \mathrm{CaO + SiO_2 \rightarrow CaSiO_3} \] does not.
  • Copper matte is a mixture of sulphides of copper and iron, mainly \(Cu_2S\) and \(FeS\).
  • In the reaction \[ \mathrm{Cu_2O + Cu_2S \rightarrow 6Cu + SO_2} \] the gas formed is \(SO_2\), so molecule \(Y\) is angular.

    Ellingham diagram
  • Ellingham diagram is a plot of \(\Delta G^\circ\) vs \(T\).
  • It is used to predict feasibility of reduction and choice of reducing agent.
  • Lower line \(\Rightarrow\) more stable oxide; higher line \(\Rightarrow\) less stable oxide.
  • So if a statement says oxide with higher \(\Delta G^\circ\) is more stable, it is false.

    Refining methods map
  • Distillation: used when the metal is volatile / has lower boiling point than impurity; standard example: Zn, Hg.
  • Poling: used for Cu and Sn to remove oxide impurities from molten metal.
  • Liquation: used for low-melting metals such as Sn.
  • Zone refining: used for ultra-pure semiconductors / related metals such as Ga, Si, Ge.
  • Vapour phase refining: based on formation of volatile compound; Mond \(\rightarrow\) Ni, van Arkel \(\rightarrow\) Zr/Ti.

    Fast exam mapping
  • Sphalerite \(\rightarrow\) Zn \(\rightarrow\) distillation.
  • Bauxite \(\rightarrow\) leaching.
  • Mond \(\rightarrow\) Ni; van Arkel \(\rightarrow\) Zr; zone refining \(\rightarrow\) Ga.
  • Low-boiling metal + high-boiling impurity \(\rightarrow\) distillation.
  • Silica impurity in blast furnace \(\rightarrow\) limestone added.
  • Copper matte \(\rightarrow\) sulphides, not oxides/carbonates/silicates.

  1. Overview of metallurgy
    • Metallurgy deals with extraction of metals from ores and purification of the crude metal.
    • Main stages are concentration of ore, conversion to oxide or suitable form, reduction/extraction, and refining.
    • The syllabus emphasizes ore concentration methods, calcination/roasting, flux and slag, Ellingham diagram, and refining methods.

  2. Important ore and mineral names
    • Sphalerite \(=\mathrm{ZnS}\), so it is the chief ore of zinc.
    • Siderite \(=\mathrm{FeCO_3}\), the carbonate ore of iron.
    • Malachite \(=\mathrm{CuCO_3\cdot Cu(OH)_2}\), a copper ore.
    • Calamine \(=\mathrm{ZnCO_3}\), a zinc carbonate mineral.
    • Kaolinite is an aluminium silicate mineral.
    • These direct ore-identification questions are repeatedly asked, so the names should be memorised as fixed pairs.

  3. Concentration of ores
    • Hydraulic washing / levigation is used when there is large difference in density between ore and gangue.
    • Froth flotation is used mainly for sulphide ores.
    • Magnetic separation is used when ore and gangue differ in magnetic properties.
    • Leaching is used when the ore is selectively soluble in a reagent.
    • Bauxite is purified by leaching in Bayer’s process.
    • Silver ore concentration by cyanide solution is also leaching: \[ 4Ag + 8CN^- + 2H_2O + O_2 \rightarrow 4[Ag(CN)_2]^- + 4OH^- \]

  4. Calcination, roasting, flux, and slag
    • Calcination is heating the ore in limited or no air; it is usually used for carbonate and hydrated ores.
    • Roasting is heating the ore strongly in excess air; it is mainly used for sulphide ores.
    • Gangue means unwanted earthy impurities present in the ore.
    • Flux is added to remove gangue.
    • Slag is the fusible product formed by reaction of flux with gangue.
    • In blast furnace extraction of iron, silica is an acidic impurity and limestone is the added flux precursor: \[ \mathrm{CaCO_3 \xrightarrow{\Delta} CaO + CO_2} \] \[ \mathrm{CaO + SiO_2 \rightarrow CaSiO_3} \]
    • In copper extraction, ferrous oxide is removed as ferrous silicate: \[ \mathrm{FeO + SiO_2 \rightarrow FeSiO_3} \]

  5. Extraction of iron and copper
    • In iron extraction, coke acts as fuel and reducing agent, while limestone helps remove silica impurity.
    • The blast furnace question cluster here mainly tests gangue, flux, and slag formation.
    • In copper metallurgy, roasting converts sulphides partly into oxides and removes sulphur as \(SO_2\).
    • Copper matte is a molten mixture of sulphides of copper and iron, mainly \(Cu_2S\) and \(FeS\).
    • During self-reduction / auto-reduction: \[ \mathrm{Cu_2S + 2Cu_2O \rightarrow 6Cu + SO_2} \] Hence the gaseous product is \(SO_2\), which is angular in shape.

  6. Ellingham diagram
    • Ellingham diagram is a plot of standard Gibbs free energy change \(\Delta G^\circ\) versus temperature \(T\).
    • It is used to predict the thermodynamic feasibility of reduction of metal oxides.
    • If the line for oxidation of one metal lies below that of another, the lower oxide is more stable.
    • A reducing agent can reduce the oxide whose line lies above the oxidation line of the reducing agent at that temperature.
    • So “higher \(\Delta G^\circ\) means more stable oxide” is incorrect.

  7. Refining of metals
    • Distillation is used for volatile metals or when the pure metal has lower boiling point than impurities; zinc is a standard example.
    • Liquation is used for low-melting metals such as tin.
    • Poling is used for metals like copper and tin to reduce oxide impurities from the molten state.
    • Zone refining is used for very high-purity materials such as semiconductor-related metals like Ga, Si, and Ge.
    • Vapour phase refining works by forming a volatile compound and decomposing it later.
    • Mond process is used for nickel.
    • Van Arkel process is used for zirconium (and also titanium in general metallurgy context).
    • Matching-type questions repeatedly test these standard method–metal pairs.
