IUPAC NOMENCLATURE

  • Use this exact naming method every time:
    1. Find the principal functional group.
    2. Choose the main chain containing that group and the maximum number of multiple bonds.
    3. Number by: \(\mathrm{Principal\ FG > C=C > C\equiv C > substituents}\).
    4. Write substituents in alphabetical order.
    5. Final order: \(\mathrm{prefixes + parent + en/yn + suffix}\).

  • Functional-group priority ladder: \(\mathrm{COOH > SO_3H > Anhydride > Ester > COX > CONH_2 > CN > CHO > CO > OH > NH_2 > C=C > C\equiv C > substituents}\)

  • Numbering rule: Principal functional group gets first priority. If both double and triple bonds are present, \(\mathrm{ene}\) gets lower locant than \(\mathrm{yne}\) in a tie.

  • Useful instant prefixes: \(\mathrm{-OH \rightarrow hydroxy,\ -NH_2 \rightarrow amino,\ -CHO \rightarrow formyl,\ >C=O \rightarrow oxo,\ -CN \rightarrow cyano,\ -COOH \rightarrow carboxy,\ -OR \rightarrow alkoxy,\ -NO_2 \rightarrow nitro,\ -F/-Cl/-Br/-I \rightarrow fluoro/chloro/bromo/iodo}\)

  • High-yield traps: Do not choose the longest chain blindly; \(\mathrm{-OH}\) beats \(\mathrm{C=C}\) and \(\mathrm{C\equiv C}\); substituents are written in alphabetical order; in esters write \(\mathrm{alkyl\ first,\ alkanoate\ second}\); in \(\mathrm{-COOH}\), \(\mathrm{-CN}\), and \(\mathrm{-CHO}\), the functional carbon is usually carbon \(1\).
