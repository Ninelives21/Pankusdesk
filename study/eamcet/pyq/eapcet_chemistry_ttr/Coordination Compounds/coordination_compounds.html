Coordination Compounds

    Werner theory and ionisable ions
  • Primary valency \(=\) oxidation state; secondary valency \(=\) coordination number / number of groups directly bonded to the central metal. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}
  • Free counter ions outside the coordination sphere precipitate with \(\mathrm{AgNO_3}\); coordinated \(\mathrm{Cl^-}\) ions do not precipitate directly. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}
  • If one mole of complex gives 3 moles of \(\mathrm{AgCl}\), then 3 chlorides are outside the sphere. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
  • Fast rebuild rule: first count ionisable ions, then use coordination number to place the remaining ligands inside the bracket.

    Ligands direct-memory map
  • Ambidentate ligands: \(\mathrm{CN^-}\), \(\mathrm{SCN^-}\), \(\mathrm{NO_2^-}\).
  • \(\mathrm{SO_4^{2-}}\) is not an ambidentate ligand. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
  • \(en\) and oxalate are bidentate ligands.
  • \(\mathrm{CO}\) is a strong-field neutral ligand and gives diamagnetic \(\mathrm{Ni(CO)_4}\). :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

    Isomerism shortcuts
  • Tetrahedral complexes like \([\mathrm{NiCl_4}]^{2-}\) do not show geometrical isomerism. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}
  • Octahedral \(\mathrm{MA_4B_2}\) type shows cis-trans isomerism, for example \([\mathrm{CoCl_2(NH_3)_4}]^+\). :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}
  • Octahedral \(\mathrm{MA_3B_3}\) type shows fac-mer isomerism, for example \([\mathrm{Co(NH_3)_3(NO_2)_3}]\). :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}
  • \([\mathrm{Co(Cl)_2(en)_2}]^+\) is a standard octahedral complex showing geometrical isomerism. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}
  • \(\mathrm{MA_5B}\) octahedral type does not give geometrical isomers.

    Magnetic properties and CFT
  • Always solve in this order: oxidation state \(\rightarrow d\)-count \(\rightarrow\) ligand strength \(\rightarrow\) high spin / low spin \(\rightarrow\) number of unpaired electrons. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}
  • Spin-only magnetic moment: \[ \mu = \sqrt{n(n+2)} \text{ B.M.} \]
  • \(\mathrm{CN^-}\) and \(\mathrm{CO}\) are strong-field ligands; \(\mathrm{F^-}\) and \(\mathrm{Cl^-}\) are weak-field ligands. :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}
  • Tetrahedral complexes are usually high spin.
  • \([\mathrm{Co(ox)_3}]^{3-}\) is treated as diamagnetic in the referenced PYQ set. :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}
  • \([\mathrm{Ni(CO)_4}]\) is diamagnetic; \(\mathrm{Na_2[NiCl_4]}\) is paramagnetic. :contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}

    Fast recurring solved patterns
  • \([\mathrm{Fe(CN)_6}]^{4-}\): \(\mathrm{Fe^{2+}} = d^6\), strong field, low spin, \(n=0\). :contentReference[oaicite:14]{index=14}
  • \([\mathrm{MnCl_4}]^{2-}\): \(\mathrm{Mn^{2+}} = d^5\), tetrahedral weak field, high spin, \(n=5\). :contentReference[oaicite:15]{index=15}
  • \([\mathrm{Mn(CN)_6}]^{4-}\): \(\mathrm{Mn^{2+}} = d^5\), strong field, low spin, \(n=1\). :contentReference[oaicite:16]{index=16}
  • \([\mathrm{Cr(NH_3)_6}]^{3+}\): \(\mathrm{Cr^{3+}} = d^3\), \(n=3\). :contentReference[oaicite:17]{index=17}
  • \([\mathrm{Ti(H_2O)_6}]^{3+}\) gives the lowest spin-only magnetic moment among the referenced aquo complexes. :contentReference[oaicite:18]{index=18}
  • \([\mathrm{Co(NH_3)_6}]^{3+}\) is taken as low-spin \(d^6\) inner-orbital complex. :contentReference[oaicite:19]{index=19}

    Oxidation state and coordination number
  • For \(\mathrm{K_2[PtCl_6]}\), oxidation state of Pt \(=+4\), coordination number \(=6\), so sum \(=10\). :contentReference[oaicite:20]{index=20}
  • Aqua regia forms \([\mathrm{PtCl_6}]^{2-}\); Pt is in \(+4\) oxidation state. :contentReference[oaicite:21]{index=21}
  • Oxalate is bidentate, so three oxalate ligands contribute coordination number \(6\). :contentReference[oaicite:22]{index=22}

  1. Werner’s theory and basic terminology
    • A coordination compound contains a central metal atom/ion and surrounding ligands.
    • Primary valency corresponds to oxidation state.
    • Secondary valency corresponds to the number of groups directly attached to the metal, i.e. coordination number. :contentReference[oaicite:45]{index=45}
    • Ions outside the coordination sphere are ionisation-sphere ions and can show normal ionic reactions such as \(\mathrm{AgCl}\) precipitation. :contentReference[oaicite:46]{index=46}

  2. Ligands and their types
    • Monodentate ligands donate through one donor atom.
    • Bidentate ligands donate through two donor atoms simultaneously; examples: \(en\), oxalate.
    • Ambidentate ligands have two possible donor atoms but use only one at a time; classic examples are \(\mathrm{CN^-}\), \(\mathrm{SCN^-}\), and \(\mathrm{NO_2^-}\). :contentReference[oaicite:47]{index=47}
    • \(\mathrm{SO_4^{2-}}\) is not treated as ambidentate in the standard PYQ framing. :contentReference[oaicite:48]{index=48}

  3. Oxidation state and coordination number in complexes
    • Oxidation state is found by charge balance after accounting for ligand charges.
    • Coordination number equals the total number of donor atoms bonded to the central metal.
    • For \(\mathrm{K_2[PtCl_6]}\), Pt has oxidation state \(+4\) and coordination number \(6\), so the sum is \(10\). :contentReference[oaicite:49]{index=49}
    • For \([\mathrm{PtCl_6}]^{2-}\) formed in aqua regia, Pt is also in \(+4\) oxidation state. :contentReference[oaicite:50]{index=50}

  4. Isomerism in coordination compounds
    • Structural isomerism includes ionisation, hydrate, coordination, and linkage isomerism.
    • Stereoisomerism includes geometrical and optical isomerism. :contentReference[oaicite:51]{index=51}
    • Tetrahedral complexes generally do not show geometrical isomerism because all positions are equivalent.
    • Octahedral \(\mathrm{MA_4B_2}\) gives cis-trans isomerism.
    • Octahedral \(\mathrm{MA_3B_3}\) gives fac-mer isomerism. :contentReference[oaicite:52]{index=52}

  5. Valence bond picture and common hybridisations
    • Octahedral complexes can be inner-orbital \((d^2sp^3)\) or outer-orbital \((sp^3d^2)\) depending on pairing and ligand strength.
    • \([\mathrm{Co(NH_3)_6}]^{3+}\) is treated as a low-spin inner-orbital octahedral complex in the referenced PYQs. :contentReference[oaicite:53]{index=53}
    • \(\mathrm{Ni(CO)_4}\) is tetrahedral and diamagnetic.
    • \([\mathrm{NiCl_4}]^{2-}\) is tetrahedral and paramagnetic. :contentReference[oaicite:54]{index=54}

  6. Crystal field theory and ligand strength
    • In octahedral complexes, \(d\)-orbitals split into lower \(t_{2g}\) and higher \(e_g\) sets.
    • In tetrahedral complexes, splitting is reversed and usually smaller in magnitude.
    • Strong-field ligands produce larger splitting and may cause pairing; weak-field ligands give smaller splitting and often high-spin complexes. :contentReference[oaicite:55]{index=55}
    • Useful memory sequence from the PYQs: \(\mathrm{CN^-}, CO\) strong field; \(\mathrm{F^-}, Cl^-\) weak field. :contentReference[oaicite:56]{index=56}

  7. Magnetic properties of coordination compounds
    • Magnetic behaviour depends on the number of unpaired electrons.
    • Spin-only magnetic moment is \[ \mu = \sqrt{n(n+2)}\ \text{B.M.} \]
    • \([\mathrm{Fe(CN)_6}]^{4-}\) is low-spin \(d^6\) and diamagnetic.
    • \([\mathrm{MnCl_4}]^{2-}\) is high-spin tetrahedral \(d^5\).
    • \([\mathrm{Mn(CN)_6}]^{4-}\) is low-spin \(d^5\).
    • \([\mathrm{Cr(NH_3)_6}]^{3+}\) is \(d^3\) with three unpaired electrons. :contentReference[oaicite:57]{index=57}
    • \([\mathrm{Co(ox)_3}]^{3-}\) is treated as diamagnetic in the linked solutions. :contentReference[oaicite:58]{index=58}

  8. Metal carbonyls
    • Metal carbonyls contain CO as ligand and involve synergic bonding.
    • \(\mathrm{Ni(CO)_4}\) is a standard diamagnetic example in objective questions. :contentReference[oaicite:59]{index=59}
    • Some carbonyls contain bridged CO ligands, which is a repeated factual PYQ pattern. :contentReference[oaicite:60]{index=60}

  9. High-yield last-minute takeaways from the PYQ cluster
    • Werner theory: directly bonded groups \(=\) secondary valency.
    • \(\mathrm{AgNO_3}\) test counts free chlorides outside the sphere.
    • \(\mathrm{SO_4^{2-}}\) is not ambidentate.
    • \([\mathrm{NiCl_4}]^{2-}\) tetrahedral, paramagnetic; \(\mathrm{Ni(CO)_4}\) diamagnetic.
    • \(\mathrm{MA_4B_2}\) octahedral \(\rightarrow\) cis-trans; \(\mathrm{MA_3B_3}\) octahedral \(\rightarrow\) fac-mer.
    • Strong-field ligands pair electrons; weak-field ligands usually do not.
