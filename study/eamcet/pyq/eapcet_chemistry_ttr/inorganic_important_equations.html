HYDROGEN, S-BLOCK, P-BLOCK, D/F BLOCK

    1. Oxidation state of hydrogen in hydrides

      \( \mathrm{LiAlH_4:\ (+1)+(+3)+4x=0 \Rightarrow x=-1} \)

      \( \mathrm{H_2O:\ 2x-2=0 \Rightarrow x=+1} \)

      Hydrogen is \(-1\) in metal/electropositive hydrides.

      Hydrogen is usually \(+1\) with non-metals such as \(\mathrm{O,N,S,Cl}\).

    2. Hydride classification: deficient, precise and rich

      \( \mathrm{Group\ 13:\ B_2H_6,\ AlH_3 \rightarrow electron\ deficient} \)

      \( \mathrm{Group\ 14:\ CH_4,\ SiH_4 \rightarrow electron\ precise} \)

      \( \mathrm{Group\ 15/16/17:\ NH_3,\ H_2O,\ H_2S,\ HCl \rightarrow electron\ rich} \)

      Electron-rich hydrides have lone pairs on the central atom.

    3. Polymeric and ionic hydrides

      \( \mathrm{BeH_2,\ MgH_2 \rightarrow polymeric\ hydrides} \)

      \( \mathrm{LiH,\ NaH,\ KH \rightarrow ionic/saline\ hydrides} \)

      \( \mathrm{NaH + H_2O \rightarrow NaOH + H_2} \)

      Alkali hydride stability decreases down the group: \(\mathrm{LiH > NaH > KH > RbH > CsH}\).

    4. Electrolysis of molten ionic hydrides

      \( \mathrm{2H^- \rightarrow H_2 + 2e^-} \)

      \(\mathrm{H^-}\) is an anion, so it goes to the anode.

      Hydrogen gas is liberated at the anode, not the cathode.

    5. Water as oxidising and reducing agent

      \( \mathrm{2Na + 2H_2O \rightarrow 2NaOH + H_2} \)

      \( \mathrm{2F_2 + 2H_2O \rightarrow 4HF + O_2} \)

      With sodium, water is reduced to \(\mathrm{H_2}\).

      With fluorine, water is oxidised to \(\mathrm{O_2}\).

    6. Temporary hardness removal by boiling

      \( \mathrm{Ca(HCO_3)_2 \xrightarrow{\Delta} CaCO_3 \downarrow + CO_2 + H_2O} \)

      \( \mathrm{Mg(HCO_3)_2 \xrightarrow{\Delta} Mg(OH)_2 \downarrow + 2CO_2} \)

      On boiling, calcium bicarbonate gives \(\mathrm{CaCO_3}\), but magnesium bicarbonate gives \(\mathrm{Mg(OH)_2}\).

    7. Clark's method and lime softening

      \( \mathrm{Ca(HCO_3)_2 + Ca(OH)_2 \rightarrow 2CaCO_3 \downarrow + 2H_2O} \)

      \( \mathrm{Mg(HCO_3)_2 + 2Ca(OH)_2 \rightarrow Mg(OH)_2 \downarrow + 2CaCO_3 \downarrow + 2H_2O} \)

      Clark's method removes temporary hardness using lime, \(\mathrm{Ca(OH)_2}\).

    8. Calgon method and ion exchange

      \( \mathrm{Ca^{2+}/Mg^{2+} + Na_4P_6O_{18}^{2-} \rightarrow soluble\ complex} \)

      \( \mathrm{2NaZ + Ca^{2+} \rightarrow CaZ + 2Na^+} \)

      Calgon is sodium hexametaphosphate; it removes hardness by complexing \(\mathrm{Ca^{2+}}\) and \(\mathrm{Mg^{2+}}\).

      Ion-exchange process is the most complete water-softening method.

    9. \(\mathrm{H_2O_2}\) volume strength formula

      \( \mathrm{2H_2O_2 \rightarrow 2H_2O + O_2} \)

      \( \mathrm{(w/v)\%=\dfrac{17x}{56}} \)

      \(x\)-volume \(\mathrm{H_2O_2}\) means \(1\) volume of solution liberates \(x\) volumes of \(\mathrm{O_2}\) at STP.

      \(20\)-volume \(\mathrm{H_2O_2}\approx 6.06\%\ (w/v)\).

    10. Structure, storage and physical constants of \(\mathrm{H_2O_2}\)

      \( \mathrm{H_2O_2:\ non\text{-}planar,\ open\text{-}book\ structure} \)

      Gas phase dihedral angle \(=111.5^\circ\); solid phase dihedral angle \(=90.2^\circ\).

      Store \(\mathrm{H_2O_2}\) in a dark, cool, impurity-free, wax-lined plastic bottle.

    11. Ordinary water and heavy water constants

      \( \mathrm{H_2O:\ maximum\ density\ at\ about\ 3.98^\circ C} \)

      \( \mathrm{D_2O:\ maximum\ density\ at\ about\ 11.2^\circ C} \)

      \( \mathrm{D_2O:\ freezing\ point\ about\ 3.8^\circ C} \)

      Heavy water is used as a moderator in nuclear reactors.

    12. Hydrogen bonding exception in hydrogen halides

      \( \mathrm{HCl < HBr < HI < HF} \)

      Among \(\mathrm{HCl,HBr,HI}\), boiling point increases down the group due to stronger van der Waals forces.

      \(\mathrm{HF}\) is highest because of intermolecular hydrogen bonding.


    1. Alkali metals in oxygen: oxide/peroxide/superoxide pattern
      \( \mathrm{4Li + O_2 \xrightarrow{\Delta} 2Li_2O} \)

      \( \mathrm{2Na + O_2 \xrightarrow{\Delta,\ excess\ O_2} Na_2O_2} \)

      \( \mathrm{K/Rb/Cs + O_2 \xrightarrow{excess\ O_2} KO_2/RbO_2/CsO_2} \)

      \(\mathrm{Li}\rightarrow\) oxide; \(\mathrm{Na}\rightarrow\) peroxide; \(\mathrm{K/Rb/Cs}\rightarrow\) superoxide.
      Peroxide is diamagnetic; superoxide is paramagnetic.
    2. Nitrate decomposition: lithium exception vs normal alkali metals
      \( \mathrm{4LiNO_3 \xrightarrow{\Delta} 2Li_2O + 4NO_2 + O_2} \)

      \( \mathrm{2KNO_3 \xrightarrow{\Delta} 2KNO_2 + O_2} \)

      \(\mathrm{LiNO_3}\) gives oxide + brown \(\mathrm{NO_2}\) + oxygen.
      Other alkali nitrates give nitrite + oxygen.
    3. Thermal decomposition of lithium carbonate
      \( \mathrm{Li_2CO_3 \xrightarrow{\Delta} Li_2O + CO_2} \)

      \(\mathrm{Li_2CO_3}\) decomposes readily.
      \(\mathrm{Na_2CO_3}\) is thermally stable.
    4. Hydrolysis of potassium superoxide
      \( \mathrm{2KO_2 + 2H_2O \rightarrow 2KOH + H_2O_2 + O_2} \)

      Superoxide hydrolysis gives both \(\mathrm{H_2O_2}\) and \(\mathrm{O_2}\).
    5. Castner-Kellner process for industrial manufacture of \(\mathrm{NaOH}\) \( \mathrm{2NaCl + 2H_2O \xrightarrow[\mathrm{Hg\ cathode,\ carbon/graphite\ anode}]{\mathrm{electrolysis}} 2NaOH + Cl_2 + H_2} \)

      \( \mathrm{2Cl^- \rightarrow Cl_2 + 2e^-} \)

      Mercury is cathode.
      Carbon/graphite is anode.
      Chlorine is liberated at the anode.
    6. Solvay process for industrial manufacture of sodium carbonate (washing soda) \( \mathrm{NaCl + NH_3 + CO_2 + H_2O \rightarrow NaHCO_3 + NH_4Cl} \)

      \( \mathrm{2NaHCO_3 \xrightarrow{\Delta} Na_2CO_3 + CO_2 + H_2O} \)

      Solvay process prepares \(\mathrm{Na_2CO_3}\), not \(\mathrm{NaOH}\).
    7. Amphoteric beryllium hydroxide
      \( \mathrm{Be(OH)_2 + 2HCl \rightarrow BeCl_2 + 2H_2O} \)

      \( \mathrm{Be(OH)_2 + 2NaOH \xrightarrow{\mathrm{excess\ alkali}} Na_2[Be(OH)_4]} \) (sodium beryllate)

      \(\mathrm{Be(OH)_2}\) reacts with both acids and alkalis.
      In excess alkali it forms tetrahydroxoberyllate.
    8. Beryllium and aluminium hydroxides in excess alkali
      \( \mathrm{Be(OH)_2 + 2NaOH \xrightarrow{\mathrm{excess\ alkali}} Na_2[Be(OH)_4]} \)

      \( \mathrm{Al(OH)_3 + NaOH \xrightarrow{\mathrm{excess\ alkali}} Na[Al(OH)_4]} \)

      Both form tetrahydroxo complexes.
      Covalency of the central atom is \(4\).
    9. Nitride formation: Li-Mg diagonal relationship
      \( \mathrm{6Li + N_2 \xrightarrow{\Delta} 2Li_3N} \)

      \( \mathrm{3Mg + N_2 \xrightarrow{\Delta} Mg_3N_2} \)

      Both \(\mathrm{Li}\) and \(\mathrm{Mg}\) form nitrides.
    10. Lime water and caustic soda formation
      \( \mathrm{Ca(OH)_2 + CO_2 \rightarrow CaCO_3 \downarrow + H_2O} \)

      \( \mathrm{Ca(OH)_2 + Na_2CO_3 \rightarrow CaCO_3 \downarrow + 2NaOH} \)

      Lime water means dilute aqueous \(\mathrm{Ca(OH)_2}\) solution; it turns cloudy/milky due to \(\mathrm{CaCO_3}\).
      Slaked lime means calcium hydroxide itself, \(\mathrm{Ca(OH)_2}\); with \(\mathrm{Na_2CO_3}\) it gives \(\mathrm{NaOH}\).
      11. Soda-lime decarboxylation of benzoic acid to benzene
      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5COONa + NaOH \xrightarrow[\mathrm{CaO}]{\Delta} C_6H_6 + Na_2CO_3} \)

      Soda lime \((\mathrm{NaOH + CaO})\) converts sodium benzoate to benzene.
    11. Water oxidation in photosynthesis
      \( \mathrm{2H_2O \rightarrow O_2 + 4H^+ + 4e^-} \)

      Water is oxidised to oxygen in photosynthesis.

    1. Hydrolysis of diborane

      \( \mathrm{B_2H_6 + 6H_2O \rightarrow 2H_3BO_3 + 6H_2} \)

      Diborane gives boric acid and hydrogen.

      \(\mathrm{H_3BO_3}\) is weak, monobasic, and Lewis acidic.

    2. Preparation of diborane from \(\mathrm{BF_3}\)

      \( \mathrm{2BF_3 + 6NaH \xrightarrow{450\,K} B_2H_6 + 6NaF} \)

      \( \mathrm{4BF_3 + 3LiAlH_4 \xrightarrow{\mathrm{ether}} 2B_2H_6 + 3LiF + 3AlF_3} \)

      \(\mathrm{BF_3}\rightarrow\mathrm{B_2H_6}\) is a repeated chain-question pattern.

    3. \(\mathrm{NaBH_4}\), diborane, and hydrogen evolution

      \( \mathrm{2NaBH_4 + I_2 \rightarrow B_2H_6 + 2NaI + H_2} \)

      \( \mathrm{B_2H_6 + 2NaH \xrightarrow{\mathrm{ether}} 2NaBH_4} \)

      \(\mathrm{NaBH_4}\) is a reducing agent; hydrogen in \(\mathrm{BH_4^-}\) is \(-1\).

    4. Aluminium with acid and alkali

      \( \mathrm{2Al + 6HCl \rightarrow 2AlCl_3 + 3H_2} \)

      \( \mathrm{2Al + 2NaOH + 6H_2O \rightarrow 2Na^+[Al(OH)_4]^- + 3H_2} \)

      Aluminium gives \(\mathrm{H_2}\) with both dilute acid and aqueous alkali.

    5. Aluminium hydroxide in excess alkali

      \( \mathrm{Al(OH)_3 + NaOH \rightarrow Na[Al(OH)_4]} \)

      Excess alkali gives tetrahydroxoaluminate.

      Aluminium is bonded to four \(\mathrm{OH}\) groups.

    6. Boric acid as a Lewis acid

      \( \mathrm{B(OH)_3 + 2H_2O \rightleftharpoons [B(OH)_4]^- + H_3O^+} \)

      Boric acid is monobasic because it accepts \(\mathrm{OH^-}\).


    1. Dehydration of formic acid to carbon monoxide

      \( \mathrm{HCOOH \xrightarrow[\mathrm{373\,K}]{\mathrm{conc.\ H_2SO_4}} CO + H_2O} \)

      \(\mathrm{CO}\) is a neutral oxide and carbon is \(sp\)-hybridised.

    2. Preparation of dimethyldichlorosilane

      \( \mathrm{2CH_3Cl + Si \xrightarrow[\mathrm{573\,K}]{\mathrm{Cu}} (CH_3)_2SiCl_2} \)

      This is the key starting step for silicone preparation.

    3. Hydrolysis and condensation to silicone

      \( \mathrm{(CH_3)_2SiCl_2 + 2H_2O \rightarrow (CH_3)_2Si(OH)_2 + 2HCl} \)

      \( \mathrm{n(CH_3)_2Si(OH)_2 \rightarrow [-Si(CH_3)_2-O-]_n + nH_2O} \)

      Silicone has a \(\mathrm{Si-O-Si}\) backbone, not a carbon chain backbone.

    4. Acidic nature of silica

      \( \mathrm{SiO_2 + 2NaOH \xrightarrow{\Delta} Na_2SiO_3 + H_2O} \)

      \(\mathrm{SiO_2}\) is acidic and forms silicates with bases.

    5. Hydrolysis contrast: \(\mathrm{SiCl_4}\) vs \(\mathrm{CCl_4}\)

      \( \mathrm{SiCl_4 + 4H_2O \rightarrow Si(OH)_4 + 4HCl} \)

      \(\mathrm{SiCl_4}\) hydrolyses easily, but \(\mathrm{CCl_4}\) does not hydrolyse easily because carbon has no vacant \(d\)-orbitals.

    6. Redox behaviour of \(\mathrm{Sn^{2+}}\) and \(\mathrm{Pb^{4+}}\)

      \( \mathrm{Sn^{2+} \rightarrow Sn^{4+} + 2e^-} \)

      \( \mathrm{Pb^{4+} + 2e^- \rightarrow Pb^{2+}} \)

      \(\mathrm{Sn^{2+}}\) is reducing; \(\mathrm{Pb^{4+}}\) is oxidising.


    1. Deacon's process for chlorine

      \( \mathrm{4HCl + O_2 \xrightarrow[\mathrm{723\,K}]{\mathrm{CuCl_2}} 2Cl_2 + 2H_2O} \)

      Oxidation of \(\mathrm{HCl}\) gives \(\mathrm{Cl_2}\).

    2. Hydrolysis of phosphorus chlorides

      \( \mathrm{PCl_3 + 3H_2O \rightarrow H_3PO_3 + 3HCl} \)

      \( \mathrm{PCl_5 + 4H_2O \rightarrow H_3PO_4 + 5HCl} \)

      \(\mathrm{H_3PO_3}\) is dibasic; complete hydrolysis of \(\mathrm{PCl_5}\) gives \(\mathrm{H_3PO_4}\).

    3. Preparation of phosphine

      \( \mathrm{P_4 + 3NaOH + 3H_2O \rightarrow PH_3 + 3NaH_2PO_2} \)

      White phosphorus with alkali gives phosphine and sodium hypophosphite.

    4. Disproportionation of phosphorous acid

      \( \mathrm{4H_3PO_3 \xrightarrow{\Delta} 3H_3PO_4 + PH_3} \)

      \(\mathrm{H_3PO_3}\) is dibasic and gives \(\mathrm{H_3PO_4}\) and \(\mathrm{PH_3}\) on heating.

    5. Thermal decomposition giving nitrogen

      \( \mathrm{(NH_4)_2Cr_2O_7 \xrightarrow{\Delta} Cr_2O_3 + N_2 + 4H_2O} \)

      \( \mathrm{Ba(N_3)_2 \xrightarrow{\Delta} Ba + 3N_2} \)

      Ammonium dichromate and barium azide liberate \(\mathrm{N_2}\).

    6. Nitrite with acid: formation of nitrogen oxides

      \( \mathrm{2NaNO_2 + 2HCl \rightarrow NO + NO_2 + H_2O + 2NaCl} \)

      Acidified nitrite gives \(\mathrm{NO}\) and \(\mathrm{NO_2}\).

    7. Nitric acid with metals

      \( \mathrm{3Cu + 8HNO_3(dil.) \rightarrow 3Cu(NO_3)_2 + 2NO + 4H_2O} \)

      \( \mathrm{Zn + HNO_3(conc.) \rightarrow NO_2} \)

      Dilute nitric acid commonly gives \(\mathrm{NO}\); concentrated nitric acid commonly gives \(\mathrm{NO_2}\).

    8. Hydrolysis of xenon fluorides

      \( \mathrm{XeF_6 + H_2O \rightarrow XeOF_4 + 2HF} \)

      \( \mathrm{XeF_6 + 3H_2O \rightarrow XeO_3 + 6HF} \)

      Partial hydrolysis gives \(\mathrm{XeOF_4}\); complete hydrolysis gives \(\mathrm{XeO_3}\).

    9. Oxidising action of hot concentrated sulphuric acid

      \( \mathrm{C + 2H_2SO_4(conc.) \xrightarrow{\Delta} CO_2 + 2SO_2 + 2H_2O} \)

      Carbon is oxidised to \(\mathrm{CO_2}\), and sulphuric acid is reduced to \(\mathrm{SO_2}\).

    10. Chlorine with excess ammonia

      \( \mathrm{8NH_3 + 3Cl_2 \rightarrow N_2 + 6NH_4Cl} \)

      For \(1\) mole of \(\mathrm{Cl_2}\), \(\dfrac{8}{3}\) mole of \(\mathrm{NH_3}\) is oxidised.


    1. Werner test: free chloride gives \(\mathrm{AgCl}\)

      \( \mathrm{Ag^+ + Cl^- \rightarrow AgCl \downarrow} \)

      Only ionisable \(\mathrm{Cl^-}\) outside the coordination sphere gives \(\mathrm{AgCl}\).

      Example: \([\mathrm{Co(NH_3)_6}]\mathrm{Cl_3}\) gives \(3\) moles of \(\mathrm{AgCl}\) per mole of complex.

    2. Thermal decomposition of potassium permanganate

      \( \mathrm{2KMnO_4 \xrightarrow{513\,K} K_2MnO_4 + MnO_2 + O_2} \)

      \(\mathrm{K_2MnO_4}\) contains green \(\mathrm{MnO_4^{2-}}\), with manganese in \(+6\) state.

    3. Oxidising power of transition-metal oxoanions

      \( \mathrm{VO_2^+ < Cr_2O_7^{2-} < MnO_4^-} \)

      Increasing oxidising power follows the higher oxidation-state oxoanion pattern.

      \(\mathrm{MnO_4^-}\) is the strongest oxidising agent in this set.

    4. Aqua regia forms chloroplatinate complex

      \( \mathrm{Pt \xrightarrow{\mathrm{aqua\ regia}} [PtCl_6]^{2-}} \)

      Aqua regia is \(3:1\) concentrated \(\mathrm{HCl:HNO_3}\).

      In \([\mathrm{PtCl_6}]^{2-}\), platinum is in \(+4\) oxidation state.

    5. Aqueous ammonia reactions: \(\mathrm{Fe^{3+}}\), \(\mathrm{AgCl}\), \(\mathrm{Cu^{2+}}\)

      \( \mathrm{Fe^{3+} + 3OH^- \rightarrow Fe(OH)_3 \downarrow} \)

      \( \mathrm{AgCl + 2NH_3 \rightarrow [Ag(NH_3)_2]^+ + Cl^-} \)

      \( \mathrm{Cu^{2+} + 4NH_3 \rightarrow [Cu(NH_3)_4]^{2+}} \)

      \(\mathrm{Fe(OH)_3}\) is brown; \([Ag(NH_3)_2]^+\) is colourless soluble; \([Cu(NH_3)_4]^{2+}\) is deep blue.

    6. Magnetic moment formula for complexes

      \( \mathrm{\mu = \sqrt{n(n+2)}\ BM} \)

      \(n\) is the number of unpaired electrons.

      \(\mu \approx 2.86\,\mathrm{BM}\) means \(n=2\).

    7. Spectrochemical series and spin prediction

      \( \mathrm{F^- < H_2O < NH_3 < CN^-} \)

      Weak-field ligands usually give high-spin complexes.

      Strong-field ligands such as \(\mathrm{CN^-}\) usually give low-spin complexes.
