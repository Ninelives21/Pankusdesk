HYDROGEN, S-BLOCK, P-BLOCK, D/F BLOCK

    1. Oxidation state of hydrogen in hydrides

      \( \mathrm{LiAlH_4:\ (+1)+(+3)+4x=0 \Rightarrow x=-1} \)

      \( \mathrm{H_2O:\ 2x-2=0 \Rightarrow x=+1} \)

      Hydrogen is \(-1\) in metal/electropositive hydrides.

      Hydrogen is usually \(+1\) with non-metals such as \(\mathrm{O,N,S,Cl}\).

    2. Hydride classification: deficient, precise and rich

      \( \mathrm{Group\ 13:\ B_2H_6,\ AlH_3 \rightarrow electron\ deficient} \)

      \( \mathrm{Group\ 14:\ CH_4,\ SiH_4 \rightarrow electron\ precise} \)

      \( \mathrm{Group\ 15/16/17:\ NH_3,\ H_2O,\ H_2S,\ HCl \rightarrow electron\ rich} \)

      Electron-rich hydrides have lone pairs on the central atom.

    3. Polymeric and ionic hydrides

      \( \mathrm{BeH_2,\ MgH_2 \rightarrow polymeric\ hydrides} \)

      \( \mathrm{LiH,\ NaH,\ KH \rightarrow ionic/saline\ hydrides} \)

      \( \mathrm{NaH + H_2O \rightarrow NaOH + H_2} \)

      Alkali hydride stability decreases down the group: \(\mathrm{LiH > NaH > KH > RbH > CsH}\).

    4. Electrolysis of molten ionic hydrides

      \( \mathrm{2H^- \rightarrow H_2 + 2e^-} \)

      \(\mathrm{H^-}\) is an anion, so it goes to the anode.

      Hydrogen gas is liberated at the anode, not the cathode.

    5. Water as oxidising and reducing agent

      \( \mathrm{2Na + 2H_2O \rightarrow 2NaOH + H_2} \)

      \( \mathrm{2F_2 + 2H_2O \rightarrow 4HF + O_2} \)

      With sodium, water is reduced to \(\mathrm{H_2}\).

      With fluorine, water is oxidised to \(\mathrm{O_2}\).

    6. Temporary hardness removal by boiling

      \( \mathrm{Ca(HCO_3)_2 \xrightarrow{\Delta} CaCO_3 \downarrow + CO_2 + H_2O} \)

      \( \mathrm{Mg(HCO_3)_2 \xrightarrow{\Delta} Mg(OH)_2 \downarrow + 2CO_2} \)

      On boiling, calcium bicarbonate gives \(\mathrm{CaCO_3}\), but magnesium bicarbonate gives \(\mathrm{Mg(OH)_2}\).

    7. Clark's method and lime softening

      \( \mathrm{Ca(HCO_3)_2 + Ca(OH)_2 \rightarrow 2CaCO_3 \downarrow + 2H_2O} \)

      \( \mathrm{Mg(HCO_3)_2 + 2Ca(OH)_2 \rightarrow Mg(OH)_2 \downarrow + 2CaCO_3 \downarrow + 2H_2O} \)

      Clark's method removes temporary hardness using lime, \(\mathrm{Ca(OH)_2}\).

    8. Calgon method and ion exchange

      \( \mathrm{Ca^{2+}/Mg^{2+} + Na_4P_6O_{18}^{2-} \rightarrow soluble\ complex} \)

      \( \mathrm{2NaZ + Ca^{2+} \rightarrow CaZ + 2Na^+} \)

      Calgon is sodium hexametaphosphate; it removes hardness by complexing \(\mathrm{Ca^{2+}}\) and \(\mathrm{Mg^{2+}}\).

      Ion-exchange process is the most complete water-softening method.

    9. \(\mathrm{H_2O_2}\) volume strength formula

      \( \mathrm{2H_2O_2 \rightarrow 2H_2O + O_2} \)

      \( \mathrm{(w/v)\%=\dfrac{17x}{56}} \)

      \(x\)-volume \(\mathrm{H_2O_2}\) means \(1\) volume of solution liberates \(x\) volumes of \(\mathrm{O_2}\) at STP.

      \(20\)-volume \(\mathrm{H_2O_2}\approx 6.06\%\ (w/v)\).

    10. Structure, storage and physical constants of \(\mathrm{H_2O_2}\)

      \( \mathrm{H_2O_2:\ non\text{-}planar,\ open\text{-}book\ structure} \)

      Gas phase dihedral angle \(=111.5^\circ\); solid phase dihedral angle \(=90.2^\circ\).

      Store \(\mathrm{H_2O_2}\) in a dark, cool, impurity-free, wax-lined plastic bottle.

    11. Ordinary water and heavy water constants

      \( \mathrm{H_2O:\ maximum\ density\ at\ about\ 3.98^\circ C} \)

      \( \mathrm{D_2O:\ maximum\ density\ at\ about\ 11.2^\circ C} \)

      \( \mathrm{D_2O:\ freezing\ point\ about\ 3.8^\circ C} \)

      Heavy water is used as a moderator in nuclear reactors.

    12. Hydrogen bonding exception in hydrogen halides

      \( \mathrm{HCl < HBr < HI < HF} \)

      Among \(\mathrm{HCl,HBr,HI}\), boiling point increases down the group due to stronger van der Waals forces.

      \(\mathrm{HF}\) is highest because of intermolecular hydrogen bonding.


    1. Alkali metals with excess oxygen

      \( \mathrm{2Na + O_2 \rightarrow Na_2O_2} \)

      \( \mathrm{K + O_2 \rightarrow KO_2} \)

      \( \mathrm{Rb + O_2 \rightarrow RbO_2} \)

      \( \mathrm{Cs + O_2 \rightarrow CsO_2} \)

      \(\mathrm{Na_2O_2}\) contains diamagnetic peroxide ion \(\mathrm{O_2^{2-}}\); \(\mathrm{KO_2}\), \(\mathrm{RbO_2}\), and \(\mathrm{CsO_2}\) contain paramagnetic superoxide ion \(\mathrm{O_2^-}\).

    2. Superoxide hydrolysis

      \( \mathrm{2KO_2 + 2H_2O \rightarrow 2KOH + H_2O_2 + O_2} \)

      Superoxide hydrolysis gives both \(\mathrm{H_2O_2}\) and \(\mathrm{O_2}\).

    3. Lithium nitrate thermal decomposition

      \( \mathrm{4LiNO_3 \xrightarrow{\Delta} 2Li_2O + 4NO_2 + O_2} \)

      Lithium nitrate is the alkali-metal nitrate exception; it gives oxide, brown \(\mathrm{NO_2}\), and oxygen.

    4. Other alkali metal nitrates on heating

      \( \mathrm{2KNO_3 \xrightarrow{\Delta} 2KNO_2 + O_2} \)

      Alkali metal nitrates except lithium generally give nitrite and oxygen, not \(\mathrm{NO_2}\).

    5. Lithium carbonate thermal decomposition

      \( \mathrm{Li_2CO_3 \xrightarrow{\Delta} Li_2O + CO_2} \)

      \(\mathrm{Li_2CO_3}\) decomposes more readily than other alkali metal carbonates because \(\mathrm{Li^+}\) is small and strongly polarising.

    6. Solvay process for sodium carbonate

      \( \mathrm{NaCl + NH_3 + CO_2 + H_2O \rightarrow NaHCO_3 + NH_4Cl} \)

      \( \mathrm{2NaHCO_3 \xrightarrow{\Delta} Na_2CO_3 + CO_2 + H_2O} \)

      Solvay process prepares sodium carbonate/soda ash; Castner-Kellner process prepares caustic soda.

    7. Castner-Kellner process for sodium hydroxide

      \( \mathrm{2Cl^- \rightarrow Cl_2 + 2e^-} \)

      \( \mathrm{Na^+ + e^- \rightarrow Na(Hg)} \)

      \( \mathrm{2Na(Hg) + 2H_2O \rightarrow 2NaOH + H_2 + 2Hg} \)

      In Castner-Kellner cell, carbon/graphite is anode and mercury is cathode.

    8. Beryllium hydroxide amphoteric behaviour

      \( \mathrm{Be(OH)_2 + 2HCl \rightarrow BeCl_2 + 2H_2O} \)

      \( \mathrm{Be(OH)_2 + 2NaOH \rightarrow Na_2[Be(OH)_4]} \)

      \(\mathrm{Be(OH)_2}\) is amphoteric; \(\mathrm{Mg(OH)_2}\) and \(\mathrm{Ca(OH)_2}\) are mainly basic.

    9. Amphoteric hydroxide complex comparison

      \( \mathrm{Be(OH)_2 + 2NaOH \rightarrow Na_2[Be(OH)_4]} \)

      \( \mathrm{Al(OH)_3 + NaOH \rightarrow Na[Al(OH)_4]} \)

      In excess alkali, both \([Be(OH)_4]^{2-}\) and \([Al(OH)_4]^-\) have four hydroxyl groups around the central atom.

    10. Beryllium hydride preparation

      \( \mathrm{2BeCl_2 + LiAlH_4 \rightarrow 2BeH_2 + LiCl + AlCl_3} \)

      \(\mathrm{BeH_2}\) is prepared indirectly; beryllium does not readily form it by direct combination with hydrogen.

    11. Metals forming both oxide and nitride in air

      \( \mathrm{6Li + N_2 \rightarrow 2Li_3N} \)

      \( \mathrm{3Mg + N_2 \rightarrow Mg_3N_2} \)

      \(\mathrm{Li}\), \(\mathrm{Be}\), \(\mathrm{Mg}\), \(\mathrm{Sr}\), and \(\mathrm{Ba}\) are counted for oxide + nitride formation on burning in air; \(\mathrm{Na}\) and \(\mathrm{K}\) are not counted in this set.

    12. Lime water and caustic soda formation

      \( \mathrm{Ca(OH)_2 + CO_2 \rightarrow CaCO_3\downarrow + H_2O} \)

      \( \mathrm{Ca(OH)_2 + Na_2CO_3 \rightarrow CaCO_3\downarrow + 2NaOH} \)

      Lime water is aqueous \(\mathrm{Ca(OH)_2}\) used to detect \(\mathrm{CO_2}\) by milkiness. Slaked lime is solid \(\mathrm{Ca(OH)_2}\); with \(\mathrm{Na_2CO_3}\), it gives \(\mathrm{NaOH}\).

    13. Soda-lime decarboxylation link

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5COONa + NaOH \xrightarrow[\Delta]{CaO} C_6H_6 + Na_2CO_3} \)

      Soda lime \(=\mathrm{NaOH + CaO}\); benzoate gives benzene by decarboxylation.

    14. Water softening by Calgon

      Calgon \(=\) sodium hexametaphosphate, \(\mathrm{(NaPO_3)_6}\).

      It removes permanent hardness by complexing \(\mathrm{Ca^{2+}}\) and \(\mathrm{Mg^{2+}}\).

      Do not confuse Calgon with zeolite-type sodium aluminosilicate.

    15. Portland cement composition anchors

      Major components: lime \(\mathrm{CaO}\) and silica \(\mathrm{SiO_2}\).

      Major phase: tricalcium silicate, \(\mathrm{Ca_3SiO_5}\), also written as \(3\mathrm{CaO\cdot SiO_2}\).

      Gypsum \(\mathrm{CaSO_4\cdot 2H_2O}\) controls setting but is not the major ingredient.

    16. High-frequency uses and alloys

      \(\mathrm{KOH}\): absorbs \(\mathrm{CO_2}\).

      Liquid sodium: coolant.

      Lithium: electrochemical cells.

      \(\mathrm{Mg(OH)_2}\): antacid.

      \(\mathrm{Li-Pb}\): bearings; \(\mathrm{Be-Cu}\): springs; \(\mathrm{Mg-Al}\): aircraft construction; \(\mathrm{Na-Pb}\): tetraethyl lead preparation.


    1. Diborane preparation from sodium borohydride

      \( \mathrm{2NaBH_4 + I_2 \rightarrow B_2H_6 + 2NaI + H_2} \)

      This reaction is repeatedly useful for identifying \(\mathrm{B_2H_6}\) formation and hydrogen evolution.

    2. Diborane hydrolysis to boric acid

      \( \mathrm{B_2H_6 + 6H_2O \rightarrow 2H_3BO_3 + 6H_2} \)

      \(\mathrm{H_3BO_3}\) is a weak monobasic Lewis acid, not a tribasic acid.

    3. Diborane preparation from \(\mathrm{BF_3}\)

      \( \mathrm{4BF_3 + 3LiAlH_4 \xrightarrow{ether} 2B_2H_6 + 3LiF + 3AlF_3} \)

      Also remember: \(\mathrm{BF_3}\) with hydride source gives \(\mathrm{B_2H_6}\).

    4. Diborane from \(\mathrm{BF_3}\) and sodium hydride

      \( \mathrm{2BF_3 + 6NaH \xrightarrow{450\,K} B_2H_6 + 6NaF} \)

      This is the common sequence clue where \(X=\mathrm{B_2H_6}\), followed by hydrolysis to boric acid.

    5. Sodium borohydride from diborane

      \( \mathrm{B_2H_6 + 2NaH \xrightarrow{ether} 2NaBH_4} \)

      \(\mathrm{NaBH_4}\) is a good reducing agent; hydrogen in \(\mathrm{BH_4^-}\) has oxidation state \(-1\).

    6. Lewis-base cleavage of diborane by carbon monoxide

      \( \mathrm{B_2H_6 + 2CO \rightarrow 2BH_3\cdot CO} \)

      The product is the borane carbonyl adduct \(\mathrm{BH_3\cdot CO}\).

    7. Borazine formation from diborane and ammonia

      \( \mathrm{3B_2H_6 + 6NH_3 \xrightarrow{\Delta} 2B_3N_3H_6 + 12H_2} \)

      Borazine \(\mathrm{B_3N_3H_6}\) has \(12\) \(\sigma\)-bonds and \(3\) \(\pi\)-bonds.

    8. Boric acid as Lewis acid

      \( \mathrm{B(OH)_3 + 2H_2O \rightleftharpoons [B(OH)_4]^- + H_3O^+} \)

      Boric acid accepts \(\mathrm{OH^-}\) from water; it does not directly donate three protons.

    9. Borax bead test with cobalt salt

      \( \mathrm{CoO + B_2O_3 \xrightarrow{\Delta} Co(BO_2)_2} \)

      Cobalt metaborate gives a blue bead.

    10. Structural formula anchor for borax

      \( \mathrm{Na_2B_4O_7\cdot 10H_2O = Na_2[B_4O_5(OH)_4]\cdot 8H_2O} \)

      In \(\mathrm{Na_2[B_4O_5(OH)_x]\cdot yH_2O}\), \(x=4\), \(y=8\), so \(x+y=12\).

    11. Aluminium with dilute acid

      \( \mathrm{2Al + 6HCl \rightarrow 2AlCl_3 + 3H_2} \)

      Aluminium with dilute acid liberates \(\mathrm{H_2}\), not \(\mathrm{Cl_2}\).

    12. Aluminium with excess aqueous alkali

      \( \mathrm{2Al + 2NaOH + 6H_2O \rightarrow 2Na^+[Al(OH)_4]^- + 3H_2} \)

      Aluminium is amphoteric; in excess alkali it forms tetrahydroxoaluminate and hydrogen gas.

    13. Amphoteric hydroxides in excess alkali

      \( \mathrm{Al(OH)_3 + NaOH \rightarrow Na[Al(OH)_4]} \)

      \( \mathrm{Be(OH)_2 + 2NaOH \rightarrow Na_2[Be(OH)_4]} \)

      Both aluminium and beryllium form tetrahydroxo complexes in excess alkali.

    14. Aluminium chloride in excess water

      \( \mathrm{AlCl_3 + 6H_2O \rightarrow [Al(H_2O)_6]^{3+} + 3Cl^-} \)

      In excess water, aluminium ion is represented as the strongly hydrated \([Al(H_2O)_6]^{3+}\) ion.

    15. Boron oxidation to acidic oxide

      \( \mathrm{4B + 3O_2 \xrightarrow{\Delta} 2B_2O_3} \)

      \(\mathrm{B_2O_3}\) is acidic, while \(\mathrm{Al_2O_3}\) and \(\mathrm{Ga_2O_3}\) are amphoteric.


    1. Formic acid dehydration to carbon monoxide

      \( \mathrm{HCOOH \xrightarrow[373\,K]{conc.\ H_2SO_4} CO + H_2O} \)

      \(\mathrm{CO}\) is a neutral oxide and carbon in \(\mathrm{CO}\) is \(sp\)-hybridised.

    2. Silicone preparation from methyl chloride and silicon

      \( \mathrm{2CH_3Cl + Si \xrightarrow[573\,K]{Cu} (CH_3)_2SiCl_2} \)

      \( \mathrm{(CH_3)_2SiCl_2 + 2H_2O \rightarrow (CH_3)_2Si(OH)_2 + 2HCl} \)

      \( \mathrm{n(CH_3)_2Si(OH)_2 \rightarrow [-Si(CH_3)_2-O-]_n + nH_2O} \)

      Silicone has a \(\mathrm{Si-O-Si}\) backbone, not a carbon-chain backbone.

    3. Hydrolysis contrast of \(\mathrm{CCl_4}\) and \(\mathrm{SiCl_4}\)

      \(\mathrm{CCl_4}\) does not hydrolyse easily because carbon lacks vacant \(d\)-orbitals.

      \( \mathrm{SiCl_4 + 4H_2O \rightarrow Si(OH)_4 + 4HCl} \)

      \(\mathrm{SiCl_4}\) hydrolyses easily because silicon can expand coordination.

    4. Silica as acidic oxide

      \( \mathrm{SiO_2 + 2NaOH \xrightarrow{\Delta} Na_2SiO_3 + H_2O} \)

      \(\mathrm{SiO_2}\) is acidic; \(\mathrm{SnO}\), \(\mathrm{SnO_2}\), \(\mathrm{PbO}\), and \(\mathrm{PbO_2}\) are amphoteric.

    5. Carbon dioxide as acidic oxide

      \( \mathrm{CO_2 + 2NaOH \rightarrow Na_2CO_3 + H_2O} \)

      \( \mathrm{CO_2 + NaOH \rightarrow NaHCO_3} \)

      \(\mathrm{CO_2}\) is acidic, while \(\mathrm{CO}\) is neutral.

    6. Amphoteric behaviour of tin and lead oxides

      \( \mathrm{SnO_2 + 2NaOH \xrightarrow{\Delta} Na_2SnO_3 + H_2O} \)

      \( \mathrm{PbO_2 + 2NaOH \xrightarrow{\Delta} Na_2PbO_3 + H_2O} \)

      Tin and lead oxides are repeatedly tested as amphoteric, not purely acidic or purely basic.

    7. Redox behaviour from inert pair effect

      \( \mathrm{Sn^{2+} \rightarrow Sn^{4+} + 2e^-} \)

      \( \mathrm{Pb^{4+} + 2e^- \rightarrow Pb^{2+}} \)

      \(\mathrm{Sn^{2+}}\) is reducing, while \(\mathrm{Pb^{4+}}\) is oxidising; \(\mathrm{Pb^{2+}}\) is relatively stable.

    8. Hexafluorosilicate formation

      \( \mathrm{SiF_4 + 2F^- \rightarrow [SiF_6]^{2-}} \)

      \([SiF_6]^{2-}\) exists because fluoride is small; \([SiCl_6]^{2-}\) does not exist due to steric crowding by large chloride ions.

    9. Zeolite composition anchor

      Zeolites are hydrated sodium aluminosilicates.

      A useful representation is \( \mathrm{Na_x[(AlO_2)_x(SiO_2)_y]\cdot zH_2O} \).

      The key ions to remember are \(\mathrm{Na^+}\), \(\mathrm{Al^{3+}}\), and the silicate framework.

    10. Group 14 electron-precise hydrides

      \( \mathrm{CH_4,\ SiH_4,\ GeH_4,\ SnH_4} \) are electron-precise hydrides.

      They have just enough electrons to form normal covalent bonds, unlike electron-deficient Group 13 hydrides.


    1. \(\mathrm{PCl_5}\) formation and complete hydrolysis

      \( \mathrm{P_4 + 10SO_2Cl_2 \rightarrow 4PCl_5 + 10SO_2} \)

      \( \mathrm{PCl_5 + 4H_2O \rightarrow H_3PO_4 + 5HCl} \)

      Complete hydrolysis of \(\mathrm{PCl_5}\) gives orthophosphoric acid, not \(\mathrm{H_3PO_3}\).

    2. \(\mathrm{PCl_3}\) formation and hydrolysis

      \( \mathrm{P_4 + 8SOCl_2 \rightarrow 4PCl_3 + 4SO_2 + 2S_2Cl_2} \)

      \( \mathrm{PCl_3 + 3H_2O \rightarrow H_3PO_3 + 3HCl} \)

      \(\mathrm{H_3PO_3}\) is dibasic because it has two ionisable \(\mathrm{O-H}\) hydrogens.

    3. Disproportionation of phosphorous acid

      \( \mathrm{4H_3PO_3 \xrightarrow{\Delta} 3H_3PO_4 + PH_3} \)

      Dibasic \(\mathrm{H_3PO_3}\) gives phosphoric acid and phosphine on heating.

    4. Phosphine preparation from white phosphorus

      \( \mathrm{P_4 + 3NaOH + 3H_2O \rightarrow PH_3 + 3NaH_2PO_2} \)

      \( \mathrm{4PH_3 + 6CuSO_4 \rightarrow 2Cu_3P_2 + 6H_2SO_4} \)

      White phosphorus with alkali gives phosphine; phosphine with copper sulphate gives copper phosphide.

    5. Phosphorus oxides to oxoacids

      \( \mathrm{P_2O_3 + 3H_2O \rightarrow 2H_3PO_3} \)

      \( \mathrm{P_4O_{10} + 6H_2O \rightarrow 4H_3PO_4} \)

      Each \(\mathrm{H_3PO_3}\) and \(\mathrm{H_3PO_4}\) molecule has one terminal \(\mathrm{P=O}\) bond.

    6. Reducing nature of hypophosphorous acid

      \( \mathrm{H_3PO_2 \rightarrow H_3PO_4} \)

      \(\mathrm{H_3PO_2}\) is strongly reducing because it has \(\mathrm{P-H}\) bonds; it can reduce \(\mathrm{Ag^+}\) to metallic \(\mathrm{Ag}\).

    7. Zinc with nitric acid

      \( \mathrm{Zn + 4HNO_3(conc.) \rightarrow Zn(NO_3)_2 + 2NO_2 + 2H_2O} \)

      \( \mathrm{4Zn + 10HNO_3(dil.) \rightarrow 4Zn(NO_3)_2 + N_2O + 5H_2O} \)

      Nitric acid with metals usually gives nitrogen oxides, not \(\mathrm{H_2}\).

    8. Copper with dilute nitric acid

      \( \mathrm{3Cu + 8HNO_3(dil.) \rightarrow 3Cu(NO_3)_2 + 2NO + 4H_2O} \)

      Dilute nitric acid gives nitrogen(II) oxide, \(\mathrm{NO}\), with copper.

    9. Acidification of sodium nitrite

      \( \mathrm{NaNO_2 + HCl \rightarrow HNO_2 + NaCl} \)

      \( \mathrm{2HNO_2 \rightarrow NO + NO_2 + H_2O} \)

      Overall: \( \mathrm{2NaNO_2 + 2HCl \rightarrow NO + NO_2 + H_2O + 2NaCl} \)

    10. Thermal decomposition giving dinitrogen

      \( \mathrm{(NH_4)_2Cr_2O_7 \xrightarrow{\Delta} Cr_2O_3 + N_2 + 4H_2O} \)

      \( \mathrm{Ba(N_3)_2 \xrightarrow{\Delta} Ba + 3N_2} \)

      \( \mathrm{2NaNO_3 \xrightarrow{\Delta} 2NaNO_2 + O_2} \)

      Sodium nitrate gives oxygen, not dinitrogen.

    11. Deacon's process for chlorine

      \( \mathrm{4HCl + O_2 \xrightarrow[723\,K]{CuCl_2} 2Cl_2 + 2H_2O} \)

      This is catalytic oxidation of hydrogen chloride to chlorine.

    12. Chlorine with excess ammonia

      \( \mathrm{8NH_3 + 3Cl_2 \rightarrow N_2 + 6NH_4Cl} \)

      \(1\) mole of \(\mathrm{Cl_2}\) oxidises \(\dfrac{8}{3}\) moles of \(\mathrm{NH_3}\).

    13. Phosgene and carbon monoxide estimation

      \( \mathrm{CO + Cl_2 \rightarrow COCl_2} \)

      \( \mathrm{5CO + I_2O_5 \rightarrow I_2 + 5CO_2} \)

      \(\mathrm{Cl_2}\) is used in phosgene preparation; \(\mathrm{I_2O_5}\) is used for CO estimation.

    14. Sulphuryl chloride formation

      \( \mathrm{SO_2 + Cl_2 \xrightarrow{charcoal} SO_2Cl_2} \)

      \(\mathrm{SO_2Cl_2}\) acts as a chlorinating agent in phosphorus chloride preparation.

    15. Hot concentrated sulphuric acid with carbon

      \( \mathrm{C + 2H_2SO_4(conc.) \xrightarrow{\Delta} CO_2 + 2SO_2 + 2H_2O} \)

      Carbon is oxidised to \(\mathrm{CO_2}\); sulphuric acid is reduced to \(\mathrm{SO_2}\).

    16. \(\mathrm{SO_2}\) as reducing agent with permanganate

      \( \mathrm{SO_2 \rightarrow SO_4^{2-}} \)

      \( \mathrm{MnO_4^- \rightarrow Mn^{2+}} \) in acidic medium.

      \(\mathrm{SO_2}\) is oxidised from sulphur \(+4\) to \(+6\), while manganese is reduced to \(\mathrm{Mn^{2+}}\).

    17. Peroxydisulphuric and pyrosulphuric acid linkages

      \(\mathrm{H_2S_2O_8}\): contains an \(\mathrm{O-O}\) peroxy bond.

      \(\mathrm{H_2S_2O_7}\): contains an \(\mathrm{S-O-S}\) bridge.

      In \(\mathrm{H_2S_2O_8}\), sulphur is \(+6\) and there are two \(\mathrm{S-OH}\) bonds.

    18. Partial and complete hydrolysis of \(\mathrm{XeF_6}\)

      \( \mathrm{XeF_6 + H_2O \rightarrow XeOF_4 + 2HF} \)

      \( \mathrm{XeF_6 + 3H_2O \rightarrow XeO_3 + 6HF} \)

      \(\mathrm{XeOF_4}\) is square pyramidal; \(\mathrm{XeO_3}\) is pyramidal.

    19. Hydrolysis/disproportionation of \(\mathrm{XeF_4}\)

      \( \mathrm{6XeF_4 + 12H_2O \rightarrow 4Xe + 2XeO_3 + 24HF + 3O_2} \)

      This is a high-value balanced equation for xenon fluoride hydrolysis questions.

    20. Xenon fluoride oxidation and fluorinating strength

      \( \mathrm{XeF_4 + O_2F_2 \rightarrow XeF_6 + O_2} \)

      Decreasing oxidising/fluorinating strength: \(\mathrm{XeF_6 > XeF_4 > XeF_2}\).

    21. Interhalogen hydrolysis and bromine fluoride formation

      \( \mathrm{ICl + H_2O \rightarrow HOI + HCl} \)

      \( \mathrm{Br_2 + 5F_2 \rightarrow 2BrF_5} \)

      Excess fluorine gives \(\mathrm{BrF_5}\), which is square pyramidal.

    22. Basicity of phosphorus oxoacids

      \(\mathrm{H_3PO_2=HOP(=O)H_2}\): monobasic.

      \(\mathrm{H_3PO_3=HP(=O)(OH)_2}\): dibasic.

      \(\mathrm{H_3PO_4=P(=O)(OH)_3}\): tribasic.

      Basicity depends on ionisable \(\mathrm{O-H}\) hydrogens, not total hydrogens.

    23. Acidity order of hypohalous acids

      \( \mathrm{HClO > HBrO > HIO} \)

      For same-type oxyacids \(\mathrm{HOX}\), acidity increases with electronegativity of \(X\).


    1. Werner test with \(\mathrm{AgNO_3}\): free chloride only

      \( \mathrm{[Co(NH_3)_6]Cl_3 + 3AgNO_3 \rightarrow [Co(NH_3)_6](NO_3)_3 + 3AgCl\downarrow} \)

      Only ionisable chloride outside the coordination sphere gives \(\mathrm{AgCl}\).

      If \(3\) moles of \(\mathrm{AgCl}\) form per mole of complex, the formula is \(\mathrm{[Co(NH_3)_6]Cl_3}\).

    2. Hydrate ionisation formula from \(\mathrm{AgNO_3}\) test

      \( \mathrm{[Co(H_2O)_6]Cl_3 + 3AgNO_3 \rightarrow [Co(H_2O)_6](NO_3)_3 + 3AgCl\downarrow} \)

      For \(\mathrm{CoH_{12}O_6Cl_3}\), if all three chlorides precipitate, the complex is \(\mathrm{[Co(H_2O)_6]Cl_3}\).

    3. Aluminium chloride hydration in excess water

      \( \mathrm{AlCl_3 + 6H_2O \rightarrow [Al(H_2O)_6]^{3+} + 3Cl^-} \)

      Small, highly charged \(\mathrm{Al^{3+}}\) is represented as the hexaaqua ion in excess water.

    4. Aqueous ammonia reactions of common metal ions

      \( \mathrm{FeCl_3 + 3NH_4OH \rightarrow Fe(OH)_3\downarrow + 3NH_4Cl} \)

      \( \mathrm{AgCl + 2NH_3 \rightarrow [Ag(NH_3)_2]^+ + Cl^-} \)

      \( \mathrm{Cu^{2+} + 4NH_3 \rightarrow [Cu(NH_3)_4]^{2+}} \)

      \(\mathrm{Fe(OH)_3}\) is brown, \(\mathrm{AgCl}\) dissolves in ammonia, and copper(II) gives a deep-blue ammine complex.

    5. Permanganate thermal decomposition

      \( \mathrm{2KMnO_4 \xrightarrow{513\,K} K_2MnO_4 + MnO_2 + O_2} \)

      \(\mathrm{MnO_4^{2-}}\) is green, contains manganese in \(+6\) oxidation state, and is paramagnetic.

    6. Ammonium dichromate thermal decomposition

      \( \mathrm{(NH_4)_2Cr_2O_7 \xrightarrow{\Delta} Cr_2O_3 + N_2 + 4H_2O} \)

      This gives \(\mathrm{N_2}\), \(\mathrm{Cr_2O_3}\), and water.

    7. Aqua regia and platinum chloro-complex

      Aqua regia \(=3:1\) mixture of concentrated \(\mathrm{HCl}\) and concentrated \(\mathrm{HNO_3}\).

      Platinum dissolves to form \(\mathrm{[PtCl_6]^{2-}}\).

      In \(\mathrm{[PtCl_6]^{2-}}\), platinum oxidation state is \(+4\).

    8. Transition-metal oxoanion oxidising power

      \( \mathrm{VO_2^+ \lt Cr_2O_7^{2-} \lt MnO_4^-} \)

      Oxidation states: \(\mathrm{V}=+5\), \(\mathrm{Cr}=+6\), \(\mathrm{Mn}=+7\).

      Higher oxidation state in these oxoanions gives stronger oxidising power.

    9. Redox stability of \(\mathrm{Cr^{2+}}\), \(\mathrm{Mn^{3+}}\), \(\mathrm{Mn^{2+}}\), and \(\mathrm{Fe^{3+}}\)

      \( \mathrm{Cr^{2+} \rightarrow Cr^{3+} + e^-} \)

      \( \mathrm{Mn^{3+} + e^- \rightarrow Mn^{2+}} \)

      \(\mathrm{Mn^{2+}=3d^5}\) and \(\mathrm{Fe^{3+}=3d^5}\) are especially stable due to half-filled \(d^5\) configuration.

    10. Lanthanoid redox anchors

      \(\mathrm{Ce^{4+}}\) and \(\mathrm{Tb^{4+}}\) act as oxidising agents because they tend to reduce to stable \(+3\).

      \(\mathrm{Eu^{2+}}\) and \(\mathrm{Yb^{2+}}\) act as reducing agents because they tend to oxidise to \(+3\).

      \(\mathrm{Eu^{2+}}\) and \(\mathrm{Gd^{3+}}\) are isoelectronic and paramagnetic.

    11. \(f\)-electron count in lanthanoid ions

      \(\mathrm{Gd=[Xe]\,4f^7\,5d^1\,6s^2}\), so \(\mathrm{Gd^{3+}=[Xe]\,4f^7}\).

      \(\mathrm{Yb=[Xe]\,4f^{14}\,6s^2}\), so \(\mathrm{Yb^{2+}=[Xe]\,4f^{14}}\).

      Remove outer \(6s\) electrons first in ordinary lanthanoid ion formation.

    12. Spin-only magnetic moment

      \( \mu=\sqrt{n(n+2)}\ \mathrm{BM} \)

      \(n=\) number of unpaired electrons.

      \(\mu\approx2.86\ \mathrm{BM}\) corresponds to \(n=2\), as in tetrahedral \(\mathrm{[NiCl_4]^{2-}}\).

    13. Strong-field and weak-field ligand memory

      Increasing field strength: \(\mathrm{F^- \lt H_2O \lt NH_3 \lt CN^-}\).

      \(\mathrm{CN^-}\), \(\mathrm{CO}\), and often oxalate with high-charge metal ions favour pairing.

      \(\mathrm{F^-}\), \(\mathrm{Cl^-}\), and \(\mathrm{H_2O}\) generally favour high spin in common \(3d\) complexes.

    14. High-repeat magnetic complexes

      \(\mathrm{[Fe(CN)_6]^{4-}}\): low-spin \(d^6\), \(n=0\), diamagnetic.

      \(\mathrm{[MnCl_4]^{2-}}\): tetrahedral high-spin \(d^5\), \(n=5\).

      \(\mathrm{[Co(ox)_3]^{3-}}\): low-spin \(\mathrm{Co^{3+}}\), \(d^6\), diamagnetic.

      \(\mathrm{[CoF_6]^{3-}}\): high-spin \(d^6\), paramagnetic.

    15. Nickel complex magnetic contrast

      \(\mathrm{[NiCl_4]^{2-}}\): tetrahedral, weak-field chloride, \(d^8\), \(2\) unpaired electrons, paramagnetic.

      \(\mathrm{[Ni(CN)_4]^{2-}}\): square planar, strong-field cyanide, diamagnetic.

      \(\mathrm{Ni(CO)_4}\): tetrahedral \(d^{10}\), diamagnetic.

    16. Werner's theory: primary and secondary valency

      Primary valency \(=\) oxidation state and is ionisable.

      Secondary valency \(=\) coordination number and is non-ionisable.

      In octahedral cobalt(III) ammine complexes, secondary valency is usually \(6\).

    17. Coordination isomerism and geometrical-isomer anchors

      Octahedral \(\mathrm{MA_4B_2}\): \(2\) geometrical isomers, cis and trans.

      Octahedral \(\mathrm{MA_3B_3}\): \(2\) geometrical isomers, fac and mer.

      \(\mathrm{MA_5B}\) and tetrahedral \(\mathrm{MA_4}\): no geometrical isomerism.

      \(\mathrm{[CoCl_2(en)_2]^+}\) shows cis-trans geometrical isomerism.

    18. Ambidentate ligand anchors

      Ambidentate ligands have two possible donor atoms but coordinate through only one at a time.

      Common examples: \(\mathrm{CN^-}\), \(\mathrm{SCN^-}\), \(\mathrm{NCS^-}\), and \(\mathrm{NO_2^-}\).

      \(\mathrm{SO_4^{2-}}\) is not treated as a standard ambidentate ligand in this PYQ pattern.

    19. Bridged carbonyl complexes

      Complexes with bridged \(\mu\)-CO: \(\mathrm{[Co_2(CO)_8]}\), \(\mathrm{[Fe_2(CO)_9]}\), and \(\mathrm{[Fe_3(CO)_{12}]}\).

      \(\mathrm{[Mn_2(CO)_{10}]}\) has terminal carbonyls only in the standard comparison.

    20. Lanthanoid contraction consequences

      Poor shielding by \(4f\)-electrons causes gradual decrease in lanthanoid size from La to Lu.

      It makes \(\mathrm{Zr}\) and \(\mathrm{Hf}\) nearly equal in size.

      It also decreases basicity and reactivity of lanthanoid hydroxides down the series.

    21. Zero magnetic moment ions

      \(d^0\) and \(d^{10}\) ions are diamagnetic.

      Common zero-moment ions: \(\mathrm{Sc^{3+}}\), \(\mathrm{Ti^{4+}}\), \(\mathrm{Zn^{2+}}\), \(\mathrm{Cu^+}\), \(\mathrm{Cd^{2+}}\), and \(\mathrm{Hg^{2+}}\).
