IMPORTANT EQUATIONS - ORGANIC

    1. Aldol condensation

      \( \mathrm{2CH_3CHO \xrightarrow{\mathrm{dil.\ NaOH}} CH_3CH(OH)CH_2CHO} \)

      \( \mathrm{CH_3CH(OH)CH_2CHO \xrightarrow{\Delta} CH_3CH{=}CHCHO + H_2O} \)

      Aldehydes/ketones with at least one \(\alpha\)-hydrogen give \(\beta\)-hydroxy carbonyl compound, then dehydrated product.

    2. Cannizzaro reaction

      \( \mathrm{2HCHO \xrightarrow{\mathrm{conc.\ NaOH}} CH_3OH + HCOONa} \)

      Aldehydes without \(\alpha\)-hydrogen undergo self oxidation-reduction to alcohol and carboxylate salt.

    3. Decarboxylation with soda lime

      \( \mathrm{RCOONa + NaOH \xrightarrow[\mathrm{CaO}]{\Delta} RH + Na_2CO_3} \)

      \( \mathrm{CH_3COONa + NaOH \xrightarrow[\mathrm{CaO}]{\Delta} CH_4 + Na_2CO_3} \)

      Sodium salt of carboxylic acid gives hydrocarbon with one carbon less.

    4. Williamson synthesis

      \( \mathrm{R-X + R'O^-Na^+ \rightarrow R-O-R' + NaX} \)

      \( \mathrm{CH_3I + C_2H_5ONa \rightarrow CH_3OC_2H_5 + NaI} \)

      Alkyl halide with sodium alkoxide gives ether.

    5. Reimer-Tiemann reaction

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5OH + CHCl_3 + 3NaOH \xrightarrow{345\,K} o\text{-}HOC_6H_4CHO + 3NaCl + 2H_2O} \)

      Phenol gives mainly salicylaldehyde.

    6. Kolbe-Schmitt reaction

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5ONa + CO_2 \xrightarrow[\mathrm{4{-}7\,atm}]{136^\circ C} o\text{-}HOC_6H_4COONa} \)

      \( \mathrm{o\text{-}HOC_6H_4COONa \xrightarrow{H^+} o\text{-}HOC_6H_4COOH} \)

      Sodium phenoxide gives salicylic acid after acidification.

    7. Carbylamine reaction

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5NH_2 + CHCl_3 + 3KOH \xrightarrow[\Delta]{\mathrm{alc.}} C_6H_5NC + 3KCl + 3H_2O} \)

      Primary amine gives foul-smelling isocyanide.

    8. Diazotisation

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5NH_2 + NaNO_2 + 2HCl \xrightarrow{0{-}5^\circ C} C_6H_5N_2^+Cl^- + NaCl + 2H_2O} \)

      Aniline gives benzene diazonium chloride at \(0{-}5^\circ\mathrm{C}\).

    9. Sandmeyer reaction

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5N_2^+Cl^- \xrightarrow{\mathrm{Cu_2Cl_2/HCl}} C_6H_5Cl + N_2} \)

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5N_2^+Cl^- \xrightarrow{\mathrm{Cu_2Br_2/HBr}} C_6H_5Br + N_2} \)

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5N_2^+Cl^- \xrightarrow{\mathrm{CuCN/KCN}} C_6H_5CN + N_2} \)

      Diazonium salt gives aryl chloride, bromide or cyanide.

    10. Wurtz-Fittig reaction

      \( \mathrm{Ar-X + R-X + 2Na \xrightarrow{\mathrm{dry\ ether}} Ar-R + 2NaX} \)

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5Cl + C_2H_5Cl + 2Na \xrightarrow{\mathrm{dry\ ether}} C_6H_5C_2H_5 + 2NaCl} \)

      Aryl halide and alkyl halide give alkyl arene.

    11. HVZ reaction

      \( \mathrm{RCH_2COOH \xrightarrow[\mathrm{then\ H_2O}]{X_2/\mathrm{red\ P}} RCHXCOOH} \)

      \( \mathrm{X=Cl\ or\ Br} \)

      Carboxylic acids with \(\alpha\)-hydrogen give \(\alpha\)-halo acids.

    12. Wurtz reaction

      \( \mathrm{2R-X + 2Na \xrightarrow{\mathrm{dry\ ether}} R-R + 2NaX} \)

      \( \mathrm{2CH_3I + 2Na \xrightarrow{\mathrm{dry\ ether}} C_2H_6 + 2NaI} \)

      Alkyl halides give alkanes with double the carbon skeleton.

    13. Friedel-Crafts alkylation

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_6 + CH_3Cl \xrightarrow{\mathrm{anhy.\ AlCl_3}} C_6H_5CH_3 + HCl} \)

      Benzene gives methylbenzene/toluene.

    14. Friedel-Crafts acylation

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_6 + CH_3COCl \xrightarrow{\mathrm{anhy.\ AlCl_3}} C_6H_5COCH_3 + HCl} \)

      Benzene gives acetophenone.

    15. Hydroboration-oxidation

      \( \mathrm{6CH_3CH{=}CH_2 + B_2H_6 \rightarrow 2(CH_3CH_2CH_2)_3B} \)

      \( \mathrm{2(CH_3CH_2CH_2)_3B \xrightarrow{\mathrm{H_2O_2/OH^-}} 6CH_3CH_2CH_2OH + 2B(OH)_3} \)

      Alkene gives anti-Markovnikov alcohol.

    16. Clemmensen reduction

      \( \mathrm{R_2C{=}O \xrightarrow{\mathrm{Zn{-}Hg/conc.\ HCl}} R_2CH_2} \)

      Aldehydes and ketones are reduced to methylene compounds.

    17. Wolff-Kishner reduction

      \( \mathrm{R_2C{=}O + NH_2NH_2 \rightarrow R_2C{=}NNH_2 + H_2O} \)

      \( \mathrm{R_2C{=}NNH_2 \xrightarrow[\Delta]{\mathrm{KOH/ethylene\ glycol}} R_2CH_2 + N_2} \)

      Carbonyl group is reduced to \(\mathrm{-CH_2-}\).

    18. Ozonolysis

      \( \mathrm{H_2C{=}CH_2 + O_3 \rightarrow ethylene\ ozonide} \)

      \( \mathrm{ethylene\ ozonide \xrightarrow{\mathrm{Zn/H_2O}} 2HCHO + H_2O_2} \)

      \( \mathrm{HC{\equiv}CH + O_3 \rightarrow acetylene\ ozonide \xrightarrow{\mathrm{Zn/H_2O}} OHC{-}CHO + H_2O_2} \)

      Unsaturated hydrocarbons give carbonyl compounds after ozonide hydrolysis.

    19. Cross aldol condensation

      \( \mathrm{CH_3CHO + CH_3CH_2CHO \xrightarrow{\mathrm{dil.\ NaOH}} aldol\ products \xrightarrow{\Delta} \alpha,\beta\text{-}unsaturated\ carbonyls} \)

      Two different carbonyl compounds give self-aldol and cross-aldol products if both have \(\alpha\)-hydrogen.

    20. Esterification

      \( \mathrm{RCOOH + R'OH \xrightleftharpoons[\ ]{H^+} RCOOR' + H_2O} \)

      \( \mathrm{CH_3COOH + C_2H_5OH \xrightleftharpoons[\ ]{H^+} CH_3COOC_2H_5 + H_2O} \)

      Carboxylic acid and alcohol give ester.

    21. Friedel-Crafts alkylation of anisole

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5OCH_3 + CH_3Cl \xrightarrow{\mathrm{AlCl_3}} o\text{-}CH_3C_6H_4OCH_3 + p\text{-}CH_3C_6H_4OCH_3} \)

      Para product is major.

    22. Friedel-Crafts acylation of anisole

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5OCH_3 + CH_3COCl \xrightarrow{\mathrm{AlCl_3}} o\text{-}CH_3COC_6H_4OCH_3 + p\text{-}CH_3COC_6H_4OCH_3} \)

      \(p\)-methoxyacetophenone is major.

    23. Gattermann reaction

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5N_2^+Cl^- \xrightarrow{\mathrm{Cu/HCl}} C_6H_5Cl + N_2 + CuCl} \)

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5N_2^+Cl^- \xrightarrow{\mathrm{Cu/HBr}} C_6H_5Br + N_2 + CuCl} \)

      Diazonium salt gives chloro- or bromobenzene.

    24. Coupling reaction

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5N_2^+Cl^- + C_6H_5OH \xrightarrow{OH^-} p\text{-}HOC_6H_4N{=}NC_6H_5 + HCl} \)

      Diazonium salt couples with phenol to give \(p\)-hydroxyazobenzene dye.

    25. Hofmann bromamide reaction

      \( \mathrm{RCONH_2 + Br_2 + 4NaOH \xrightarrow{\Delta} RNH_2 + Na_2CO_3 + 2NaBr + 2H_2O} \)

      Amide gives primary amine with one carbon less.

    26. Balz-Schiemann reaction

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5N_2^+Cl^- + HBF_4 \rightarrow C_6H_5N_2^+BF_4^- + HCl} \)

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5N_2^+BF_4^- \xrightarrow{\Delta} C_6H_5F + BF_3 + N_2} \)

      Diazonium fluoroborate gives fluorobenzene on heating.

    27. Crossed Wurtz reaction

      \( \mathrm{R-X + R'-X + 2Na \xrightarrow{\mathrm{dry\ ether}} R-R + R'-R' + R-R' + 2NaX} \)

      Two different alkyl halides give a mixture of alkanes.

    28. Fittig reaction

      \( \mathrm{2Ar-X + 2Na \xrightarrow{\mathrm{dry\ ether}} Ar-Ar + 2NaX} \)

      \( \mathrm{2C_6H_5Cl + 2Na \xrightarrow{\mathrm{dry\ ether}} C_6H_5-C_6H_5 + 2NaCl} \)

      Aryl halides give biaryl compounds.

    29. Kolbe electrolysis

      \( \mathrm{2CH_3COOK + 2H_2O \xrightarrow{\mathrm{electrolysis}} C_2H_6 + 2CO_2 + 2KOH + H_2} \)

      Acetate salt gives ethane at anode.

    30. Sabatier-Senderens reaction

      \( \mathrm{H_2C{=}CH_2 + H_2 \xrightarrow[\mathrm{300^\circ C}]{\mathrm{Ni}} C_2H_6} \)

      Catalytic hydrogenation of alkene gives alkane.

    31. Markovnikov rule

      \( \mathrm{CH_3CH{=}CH_2 + HBr \rightarrow CH_3CHBrCH_3\ (major)} \)

      In normal HX addition, halide goes to the double-bond carbon having fewer hydrogens.

    32. Anti-Markovnikov rule / peroxide effect

      \( \mathrm{CH_3CH{=}CH_2 + HBr \xrightarrow{ROOR} CH_3CH_2CH_2Br\ (major)} \)

      In peroxide effect, bromine goes to the double-bond carbon having more hydrogens.

    33. Polymerisation of ethene

      \( \mathrm{n(CH_2{=}CH_2) \xrightarrow[\mathrm{high\ pressure,\ 100^\circ C}]{O_2} (-CH_2-CH_2-)_n} \)

      Ethene polymerises to polyethylene.


    1. Wurtz reaction and aromatisation chain

      \( \mathrm{2R-X + 2Na \xrightarrow{\mathrm{dry\ ether}} R-R + 2NaX} \)

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_{14} \xrightarrow[\mathrm{10{-}20\,atm}]{\mathrm{Cr_2O_3,\ 773\,K}} C_6H_6 + 4H_2} \)

      Wurtz coupling gives higher alkanes; hexane aromatises to benzene.

    2. Friedel-Crafts alkylation and Friedel-Crafts acylation

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_6 + CH_3Cl \xrightarrow{\mathrm{anhy.\ AlCl_3}} C_6H_5CH_3 + HCl} \)

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_6 + CH_3COCl \xrightarrow{\mathrm{anhy.\ AlCl_3}} C_6H_5COCH_3 + HCl} \)

      Alkylation gives toluene; acylation gives acetophenone.

    3. Alkyne formation, trimerisation and hydration

      \( \mathrm{CH_2BrCH_2Br \xrightarrow[\mathrm{then\ NaNH_2}]{\mathrm{alc.\ KOH}} HC{\equiv}CH} \)

      \( \mathrm{3HC{\equiv}CH \xrightarrow[\mathrm{red\ hot\ Fe\ tube}]{873\,K} C_6H_6} \)

      \( \mathrm{CH_3C{\equiv}CH \xrightarrow{\mathrm{H_2O,\ Hg^{2+}/H^+}} CH_3COCH_3} \)

      Terminal alkyne hydration gives a methyl ketone after enol-keto tautomerism.

    4. Peroxide effect and normal HBr addition

      \( \mathrm{CH_3CH{=}CH_2 + HBr \xrightarrow{\mathrm{ROOR}} CH_3CH_2CH_2Br} \)

      \( \mathrm{CH_3CH{=}CH_2 + HBr \rightarrow CH_3CHBrCH_3} \)

      Peroxide effect gives anti-Markovnikov addition only for \(\mathrm{HBr}\).

    5. Ozonolysis and reverse ozonolysis

      \( \mathrm{R_1R_2C{=}CR_3R_4 \xrightarrow[\mathrm{Zn/H_2O}]{O_3} R_1R_2C{=}O + O{=}CR_3R_4} \)

      Reductive ozonolysis gives aldehydes/ketones, not acids.

      Reverse ozonolysis: join the two carbonyl carbons back by \(\mathrm{C{=}C}\).

    6. \(\mathrm{KMnO_4}\): cold hydroxylation vs hot cleavage

      \( \mathrm{CH_2{=}CH_2 \xrightarrow[\mathrm{273\,K}]{\mathrm{cold,\ dil.\ alkaline\ KMnO_4}} HOCH_2CH_2OH} \)

      \( \mathrm{CH_3CH{=}CHCH_3 \xrightarrow[\Delta]{\mathrm{hot,\ acidic\ KMnO_4}} 2CH_3COOH} \)

      Cold dilute alkaline \(\mathrm{KMnO_4}\) gives glycol; hot acidic \(\mathrm{KMnO_4}\) cleaves the double bond.

    7. Hydroboration-oxidation of alkenes

      \( \mathrm{CH_3CH{=}CH_2 \xrightarrow[\mathrm{H_2O_2/OH^-}]{\mathrm{B_2H_6}} CH_3CH_2CH_2OH} \)

      Hydroboration-oxidation gives anti-Markovnikov alcohol.

    8. Kolbe electrolysis of acetate

      \( \mathrm{2CH_3COO^- \xrightarrow{\mathrm{electrolysis}} C_2H_6 + 2CO_2 + 2e^-} \)

      Acetate radicals couple to give ethane, not methane.

    9. Aromatic side-chain oxidation and Etard reaction

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5CH_3 \xrightarrow[\mathrm{H_3O^+}]{\mathrm{CrO_2Cl_2/CS_2}} C_6H_5CHO} \)

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5CH_3 \xrightarrow[\mathrm{then\ H_3O^+}]{\mathrm{KMnO_4/KOH,\ \Delta}} C_6H_5COOH} \)

      Etard stops at benzaldehyde; hot alkaline \(\mathrm{KMnO_4}\) gives benzoic acid.

    10. Soda-lime decarboxylation

      \( \mathrm{RCOONa + NaOH \xrightarrow[\mathrm{CaO}]{\Delta} RH + Na_2CO_3} \)

      \( \mathrm{CH_3COONa + NaOH \xrightarrow[\mathrm{CaO}]{\Delta} CH_4 + Na_2CO_3} \)

      Sodium acetate gives methane; sodium benzoate gives benzene.

    11. Core organic test reactions

      \( \mathrm{RCHO/RCOR' + 2,4\text{-}DNP \rightarrow orange/yellow\ precipitate} \)

      \( \mathrm{R-C{\equiv}C-H + Na \rightarrow R-C{\equiv}CNa + \dfrac{1}{2}H_2} \)

      \( \mathrm{C_2H_5OH/CH_3COCH_3 \xrightarrow{\mathrm{I_2/NaOH}} CHI_3 \downarrow} \)

      \(2,4\)-DNP detects carbonyls; sodium detects terminal alkynes; iodoform test detects \(\mathrm{CH_3CO-}\) or \(\mathrm{CH_3CH(OH)-}\).

    12. Lassaigne nitrogen test and Kjeldahl rule

      \( \mathrm{Na + C + N \rightarrow NaCN} \)

      \( \mathrm{H_2SO_4 + 2NH_3 \rightarrow (NH_4)_2SO_4} \)

      Lassaigne nitrogen test gives Prussian blue through cyanide chemistry.

      Kjeldahl works for amine-type nitrogen, not nitro, azo, diazonium or pyridine-type nitrogen.


    1. \(S_\mathrm{N}1\) reactivity of haloalkanes

      \( \mathrm{R-X \rightarrow R^+ + X^-} \)

      \( \mathrm{R^+ + OH^- \rightarrow R-OH} \)

      \(S_\mathrm{N}1\) rate follows carbocation stability: benzylic/allylic/tertiary > secondary > primary.

      Aryl and vinylic halides do not undergo normal \(S_\mathrm{N}1\) easily.

    2. \(S_\mathrm{N}2\) reactivity and hydrolysis

      \( \mathrm{R-X + OH^- \rightarrow R-OH + X^-} \)

      \(S_\mathrm{N}2\) favours backside attack: methyl > \(1^\circ\) > \(2^\circ\) >> \(3^\circ\).

      Neopentyl halides are slow despite being primary because of steric hindrance.

    3. Elimination with alcoholic \(\mathrm{KOH}\)

      \( \mathrm{R-CH_2-CH_2X \xrightarrow[\Delta]{alc.\ KOH} R-CH{=}CH_2 + KX + H_2O} \)

      Alcoholic \(\mathrm{KOH}\) gives dehydrohalogenation to alkene.

      Aqueous \(\mathrm{KOH}\) favours substitution to alcohol.

    4. Swarts reaction

      \( \mathrm{R-Cl/R-Br \xrightarrow{\mathrm{AgF,\ Hg_2F_2,\ SbF_3,\ or\ CoF_2}} R-F} \)

      Example: \( \mathrm{CH_3CH_2Br \xrightarrow{CoF_2} CH_3CH_2F} \)

      Alkyl chloride/bromide is converted into alkyl fluoride.

    5. Wurtz-Fittig reaction

      \( \mathrm{Ar-X + R-X + 2Na \xrightarrow{\mathrm{dry\ ether}} Ar-R + 2NaX} \)

      Example: \( \mathrm{C_6H_5Cl + CH_3Cl + 2Na \xrightarrow{\mathrm{dry\ ether}} C_6H_5CH_3 + 2NaCl} \)

      Aryl halide and alkyl halide give alkyl arene.

    6. Fittig reaction

      \( \mathrm{2Ar-X + 2Na \xrightarrow{\mathrm{dry\ ether}} Ar-Ar + 2NaX} \)

      Example: \( \mathrm{2C_6H_5Cl + 2Na \xrightarrow{\mathrm{dry\ ether}} C_6H_5-C_6H_5 + 2NaCl} \)

      Aryl halides couple to give biaryl compounds such as biphenyl.

    7. Sandmeyer reaction

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5N_2^+Cl^- \xrightarrow{\mathrm{Cu_2Cl_2/HCl}} C_6H_5Cl + N_2} \)

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5N_2^+Cl^- \xrightarrow{\mathrm{Cu_2Br_2/HBr}} C_6H_5Br + N_2} \)

      Diazonium salt gives aryl chloride or bromide.

    8. Aryl halide to Grignard reagent, acid and decarboxylation

      \( \mathrm{ArBr \xrightarrow{\mathrm{Mg/dry\ ether}} ArMgBr} \)

      \( \mathrm{ArMgBr \xrightarrow[\mathrm{H_3O^+}]{CO_2} ArCOOH} \)

      \( \mathrm{ArCOONa + NaOH \xrightarrow[\mathrm{CaO}]{\Delta} ArH + Na_2CO_3} \)

      Grignard carboxylation adds \(-\mathrm{COOH}\); soda-lime decarboxylation removes it.

    9. \(\mathrm{KCN}\) versus \(\mathrm{AgCN}\)

      \( \mathrm{R-X + KCN \rightarrow R-CN + KX} \)

      \( \mathrm{R-X + AgCN \rightarrow R-NC + AgX} \)

      \(\mathrm{KCN}\) gives nitrile; \(\mathrm{AgCN}\) mainly gives isocyanide.

      Nitrile hydrolysis gives carboxylic acid.

    10. One-carbon chain extension using \(\mathrm{KCN}\)

      \( \mathrm{R-X + KCN \rightarrow R-CN + KX} \)

      \( \mathrm{R-CN \xrightarrow[\Delta]{aq.\ acid} R-COOH} \)

      \(\mathrm{KCN}\) increases the carbon chain by one carbon before hydrolysis to acid.

    11. Williamson synthesis versus elimination

      \( \mathrm{R-X + R'O^-Na^+ \rightarrow R-O-R' + NaX} \)

      \( \mathrm{(CH_3)_3CBr + CH_3ONa \xrightarrow{\Delta} CH_2{=}C(CH_3)_2 + NaBr + CH_3OH} \)

      Primary halides favour ether formation; tertiary halides with strong base favour elimination.

    12. Benzylic halogenation and hydrolysis

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5CH_3 \xrightarrow{Br_2/h\nu} C_6H_5CH_2Br + HBr} \)

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5CH_2Br \xrightarrow{aq.\ KOH} C_6H_5CH_2OH} \)

      \(\mathrm{Br_2/h\nu}\) gives side-chain bromination, not ring bromination.

    13. Activated nucleophilic substitution in aryl halides

      \( \mathrm{p\text{-}ClC_6H_4NO_2 \xrightarrow[\mathrm{443\,K}]{NaOH} p\text{-}HOC_6H_4NO_2} \)

      \(p\)-\(\mathrm{NO_2}\) activates aryl chloride towards replacement of \(\mathrm{Cl}\) by \(\mathrm{OH}\).

      Halogens are deactivating but ortho-para directing in electrophilic substitution.

    14. Benzene addition to hexachlorocyclohexane

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_6 + 3Cl_2 \xrightarrow{h\nu} C_6H_6Cl_6} \)

      In UV light, benzene gives addition product BHC/hexachlorocyclohexane.

      With \(\mathrm{Cl_2/FeCl_3}\), benzene gives substitution product chlorobenzene.


    1. Alcohol vapours over heated copper

      \( \mathrm{RCH_2OH \xrightarrow{Cu,\ 573\,K} RCHO + H_2} \)

      \( \mathrm{R_2CHOH \xrightarrow{Cu,\ 573\,K} R_2C{=}O + H_2} \)

      \( \mathrm{R_3COH \xrightarrow{Cu,\ 573\,K} alkene + H_2O} \)

      \(1^\circ\) alcohol gives aldehyde, \(2^\circ\) gives ketone, \(3^\circ\) gives alkene.

    2. Grignard addition to carbonyl compounds

      \( \mathrm{RCHO + R'MgX \xrightarrow[\mathrm{H_3O^+}]{\mathrm{dry\ ether}} RCH(OH)R'} \)

      \( \mathrm{R_2C{=}O + R'MgX \xrightarrow[\mathrm{H_3O^+}]{\mathrm{dry\ ether}} R_2C(OH)R'} \)

      Aldehydes give \(2^\circ\) alcohols; ketones give \(3^\circ\) alcohols.

    3. Ozonolysis followed by carbonyl identification

      \( \mathrm{R_1R_2C{=}CR_3R_4 \xrightarrow[\mathrm{Zn/H_2O}]{O_3} R_1R_2C{=}O + O{=}CR_3R_4} \)

      Reductive ozonolysis gives aldehydes/ketones, not acids.

      Reconstruct the alkene by joining the two carbonyl carbons back by \(\mathrm{C{=}C}\).

    4. Aldol condensation

      \( \mathrm{2CH_3CHO \xrightarrow{\mathrm{dil.\ NaOH}} CH_3CH(OH)CH_2CHO} \)

      \( \mathrm{CH_3CH(OH)CH_2CHO \xrightarrow{\Delta} CH_3CH{=}CHCHO + H_2O} \)

      Carbonyl compounds with \(\alpha\)-hydrogen give \(\beta\)-hydroxy carbonyls, then dehydrated \(\alpha,\beta\)-unsaturated products.

    5. Cannizzaro reaction

      \( \mathrm{2C_6H_5CHO + NaOH \rightarrow C_6H_5CH_2OH + C_6H_5COONa} \)

      \( \mathrm{2HCHO + NaOH \rightarrow CH_3OH + HCOONa} \)

      Aldehydes without \(\alpha\)-hydrogen give alcohol and carboxylate salt.

    6. Lucas test for alcohols

      \( \mathrm{ROH + HCl \xrightarrow{\mathrm{anhyd.\ ZnCl_2}} RCl + H_2O} \)

      \(3^\circ\) alcohol: immediate turbidity.

      \(2^\circ\) alcohol: turbidity after some time.

      \(1^\circ\) alcohol: little/no turbidity at room temperature.

    7. Iodoform test

      \( \mathrm{CH_3COCH_3 + 3I_2 + 4NaOH \rightarrow CHI_3 \downarrow + CH_3COONa + 3NaI + 3H_2O} \)

      \( \mathrm{CH_3CH_2OH \xrightarrow{I_2/NaOH} CHI_3 \downarrow} \)

      Positive for \(\mathrm{CH_3CO-}\) group and compounds oxidisable to it, such as ethanol.

    8. Reimer-Tiemann reaction

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5OH + CHCl_3 + 3NaOH \xrightarrow{\Delta} o\text{-}HOC_6H_4CHO + 3NaCl + 2H_2O} \)

      Phenol gives mainly salicylaldehyde \((o\)-hydroxybenzaldehyde).

    9. Kolbe-Schmitt reaction

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5ONa + CO_2 \xrightarrow[\mathrm{pressure}]{\Delta} o\text{-}HOC_6H_4COONa} \)

      \( \mathrm{o\text{-}HOC_6H_4COONa \xrightarrow{H^+} o\text{-}HOC_6H_4COOH} \)

      Sodium phenoxide gives salicylic acid after acidification.

    10. Cumene process for phenol

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5CH(CH_3)_2 \xrightarrow{O_2} cumene\ hydroperoxide} \)

      \( \mathrm{cumene\ hydroperoxide \xrightarrow{H^+} C_6H_5OH + CH_3COCH_3} \)

      Cumene gives phenol and acetone; this is the main industrial preparation of phenol.

    11. Important phenol reactions

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5OH \xrightarrow[\Delta]{Zn} C_6H_6} \)

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5OH \xrightarrow{\mathrm{Na_2Cr_2O_7/H_2SO_4}} p\text{-}benzoquinone} \)

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5OH + Br_2/CHCl_3 \xrightarrow{273\,K} p\text{-}bromophenol\ (major)} \)

      Phenol gives benzene with Zn, quinone on oxidation, and controlled para-bromination in non-aqueous medium.

    12. Ether cleavage with hydrogen halides

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5OCH_3 + HI \rightarrow C_6H_5OH + CH_3I} \)

      \( \mathrm{R-O-R' + HI \rightarrow R-I + R'OH} \)

      Aryl-alkyl ethers cleave at the alkyl-\(\mathrm{O}\) bond, not the aryl-\(\mathrm{O}\) bond.

    13. Etard reaction and full side-chain oxidation

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5CH_3 \xrightarrow[\mathrm{H_3O^+}]{\mathrm{CrO_2Cl_2/CS_2}} C_6H_5CHO} \)

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5CH_3 \xrightarrow[\mathrm{then\ H_3O^+}]{\mathrm{KMnO_4/KOH,\ \Delta}} C_6H_5COOH} \)

      Etard stops at benzaldehyde; hot alkaline \(\mathrm{KMnO_4}\) gives benzoic acid.

    14. Rosenmund reduction

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5COCl + H_2 \xrightarrow{\mathrm{Pd{-}BaSO_4}} C_6H_5CHO + HCl} \)

      Acid chloride is selectively reduced to aldehyde.

    15. Clemmensen reduction and Wolff-Kishner reduction

      \( \mathrm{R_2C{=}O \xrightarrow{\mathrm{Zn{-}Hg/conc.\ HCl}} R_2CH_2} \)

      \( \mathrm{R_2C{=}O \xrightarrow[\Delta]{\mathrm{NH_2NH_2/KOH}} R_2CH_2 + N_2} \)

      Both reduce aldehydes/ketones to hydrocarbons; Clemmensen is acidic, Wolff-Kishner is basic.

    16. Nitrile reduction: complete vs controlled

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5CN \xrightarrow[\mathrm{H_2O}]{\mathrm{Na/Hg,\ C_2H_5OH}} C_6H_5CH_2NH_2} \)

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5CN \xrightarrow[\mathrm{H_2O}]{\mathrm{DIBAL{-}H}} C_6H_5CHO} \)

      Complete reduction gives benzylamine; controlled DIBAL-H reduction gives benzaldehyde.

    17. Gattermann-Koch reaction

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_6 + CO + HCl \xrightarrow{\mathrm{AlCl_3/CuCl}} C_6H_5CHO} \)

      Benzene is formylated to benzaldehyde.

    18. HVZ reaction

      \( \mathrm{RCH_2COOH \xrightarrow[\mathrm{then\ H_2O}]{X_2/\mathrm{red\ P}} RCHXCOOH} \)

      \( \mathrm{X=Cl\ or\ Br} \)

      Carboxylic acids with \(\alpha\)-hydrogen give \(\alpha\)-halo acids.

    19. Hofmann bromamide reaction

      \( \mathrm{RCONH_2 + Br_2 + 4NaOH \xrightarrow{\Delta} RNH_2 + Na_2CO_3 + 2NaBr + 2H_2O} \)

      Amide gives primary amine with one carbon less.


    1. Hofmann bromamide reaction

      \( \mathrm{RCONH_2 + Br_2 + 4NaOH \xrightarrow{\Delta} RNH_2 + Na_2CO_3 + 2NaBr + 2H_2O} \)

      Amide gives primary amine with one carbon less.

      Benzamide gives aniline, while \(\mathrm{LiAlH_4}\) reduction of benzamide gives benzylamine.

    2. Amide reduction with \(\mathrm{LiAlH_4}\)

      \( \mathrm{RCONH_2 \xrightarrow[\mathrm{H_2O}]{LiAlH_4} RCH_2NH_2} \)

      \(\mathrm{LiAlH_4}\) retains the carbonyl carbon as \(\mathrm{-CH_2-}\).

      Example: \(\mathrm{C_6H_5CONH_2 \rightarrow C_6H_5CH_2NH_2}\).

    3. Carbylamine test

      \( \mathrm{RNH_2 + CHCl_3 + 3KOH \xrightarrow[\Delta]{alc.} RNC + 3KCl + 3H_2O} \)

      Only \(1^\circ\) aliphatic and aromatic amines give foul-smelling isocyanides.

      \(2^\circ\), \(3^\circ\) amines and amides do not give this test.

    4. Hinsberg test

      \( \mathrm{RNH_2 + C_6H_5SO_2Cl \rightarrow C_6H_5SO_2NHR + HCl} \)

      \( \mathrm{R_2NH + C_6H_5SO_2Cl \rightarrow C_6H_5SO_2NR_2 + HCl} \)

      Hinsberg reagent is benzene sulphonyl chloride, \(\mathrm{C_6H_5SO_2Cl}\).

      \(1^\circ\) amines give alkali-soluble sulphonamides; \(2^\circ\) amines give alkali-insoluble sulphonamides.

    5. Diazotisation of aniline and diazonium reactions

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5NH_2 + NaNO_2 + 2HCl \xrightarrow{273{-}278\,K} C_6H_5N_2^+Cl^- + NaCl + 2H_2O} \)

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5N_2^+Cl^- \xrightarrow{H_2O,\ warm} C_6H_5OH + N_2 + HCl} \)

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5N_2^+Cl^- \xrightarrow{H_3PO_2/H_2O} C_6H_6 + N_2 + HCl} \)

      Diazonium salt gives phenol on hydrolysis and benzene on reduction with hypophosphorous acid.

    6. Sandmeyer reaction through diazonium salt

      \( \mathrm{ArN_2^+Cl^- \xrightarrow{\mathrm{Cu_2Cl_2/HCl}} ArCl + N_2} \)

      \( \mathrm{ArN_2^+Cl^- \xrightarrow{\mathrm{CuCN/KCN}} ArCN + N_2} \)

      Diazonium salt gives aryl chloride or aryl nitrile.

      Aryl nitrile on hydrolysis gives aromatic carboxylic acid.

    7. Nitrile hydrolysis and reduction

      \( \mathrm{R-CN \xrightarrow[\Delta]{H_3O^+} R-COOH} \)

      \( \mathrm{R-CN \xrightarrow[\mathrm{H_2O}]{LiAlH_4} R-CH_2NH_2} \)

      Nitrile gives carboxylic acid on hydrolysis and primary amine on complete reduction.

    8. Selective reduction of nitrile to aldehyde

      \( \mathrm{R-CN \xrightarrow[\mathrm{H_2O}]{DIBAL{-}H} R-CHO} \)

      \( \mathrm{R-CN \xrightarrow[\mathrm{H_3O^+}]{SnCl_2/HCl} R-CHO} \)

      DIBAL-H and Stephen reduction stop nitrile at aldehyde stage.

    9. \(\mathrm{AgCN}\) products: nitrile versus isocyanide

      \( \mathrm{R-X + AgCN \rightarrow R-NC + AgX} \)

      \( \mathrm{R-X + KCN \rightarrow R-CN + KX} \)

      \(\mathrm{AgCN}\) mainly gives isocyanide; \(\mathrm{KCN}\) gives nitrile.

      \(\mathrm{R-CN}\) reduction gives \(1^\circ\) amine; \(\mathrm{R-NC}\) reduction gives \(2^\circ\) amine.

    10. Aniline protection before nitration/bromination

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5NH_2 + (CH_3CO)_2O \rightarrow C_6H_5NHCOCH_3 + CH_3COOH} \)

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5NHCOCH_3 \xrightarrow{HNO_3/H_2SO_4} p\text{-}NO_2C_6H_4NHCOCH_3} \)

      \( \mathrm{C_6H_5NHCOCH_3 \xrightarrow{Br_2/CH_3COOH} p\text{-}BrC_6H_4NHCOCH_3} \)

      Acetylation protects \(\mathrm{-NH_2}\), controls activation, and favours para substitution.

    11. Schiff base formation

      \( \mathrm{RCHO + R'NH_2 \rightarrow RCH{=}NR' + H_2O} \)

      Aldehyde plus primary amine gives imine/Schiff base.

      To convert benzonitrile to Schiff base, first reduce partially to benzaldehyde using \(\mathrm{DIBAL{-}H/H_2O}\).
