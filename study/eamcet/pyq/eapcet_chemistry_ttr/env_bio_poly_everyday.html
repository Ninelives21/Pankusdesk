ENVIRONMENTAL, POLYMERS, BIOMOLECULES, CHEMISTRY IN EVERYDAY LIFE

    1. Ozone depletion agents

      Important ozone-depleting species include \(\mathrm{NO}\), chlorine radicals \(\mathrm{Cl\cdot}\), and CFCs such as \(\mathrm{CF_2Cl_2}\).

      \(\mathrm{CH_4}\) is mainly a greenhouse gas, not a primary ozone-depleting substance.

      Ozone depletion questions often contrast CFCs/\(\mathrm{NO_x}\) with greenhouse gases.

    2. Photochemical smog

      Photochemical smog is oxidising smog.

      It forms when \(\mathrm{NO_x}\) and hydrocarbons react in sunlight.

      Major components: \(\mathrm{O_3}\), \(\mathrm{NO}\), \(\mathrm{NO_2}\), PAN, aldehydes, methanal and acrolein.

      \(\mathrm{SO_2}\) is mainly associated with classical smog, not photochemical smog.

    3. Classical smog

      Classical smog contains smoke, fog and \(\mathrm{SO_2}\).

      It is reducing in nature and is common in cool, humid conditions.

      High \(\mathrm{SO_2}\) exposure can cause plant injury such as stiffness of flower buds.

    4. Normal rainwater and acid rain clue

      Normal rainwater has pH about \(5.6\).

      This is due to dissolved \(\mathrm{CO_2}\) forming weak carbonic acid.

      Rainwater with pH lower than about \(5.6\) is treated as acid rain.

    5. Photochemical smog irritants

      Acrolein \(=\mathrm{CH_2{=}CH{-}CHO}\).

      Methanal \(=\mathrm{HCHO}\).

      PAN and aldehydes are irritating oxidants in photochemical smog.

      Acrolein contains both a carbon-carbon double bond and an aldehyde group.

    6. Greenhouse gases

      Common greenhouse gases include \(\mathrm{CO_2}\), \(\mathrm{CH_4}\), and water vapour \(\mathrm{H_2O(g)}\).

      Liquid water \(\mathrm{H_2O(l)}\), \(\mathrm{O_2}\), and \(\mathrm{NO}\) are not counted as greenhouse gases in the given PYQ pattern.

    7. Health effect of \(\mathrm{NO_2}\)

      \(\mathrm{NO_2}\) is a lung irritant.

      It can contribute to acute respiratory disease, especially in children.

      In moist respiratory passages, it can form acidic species such as \(\mathrm{HNO_2}\) and \(\mathrm{HNO_3}\).

      \(\mathrm{CO}\) is toxic mainly because it forms carboxyhaemoglobin.

    8. Nitrate pollution and methemoglobinemia

      Excess nitrate in drinking water can cause methemoglobinemia, especially in infants.

      It interferes with oxygen transport in blood.

      This is the key disease link for nitrate contamination.

    9. Drinking-water metal ion limits

      Maximum prescribed concentration of copper: \(1\,\mathrm{mg\,dm^{-3}}\).

      Maximum prescribed concentration of zinc: \(15\,\mathrm{mg\,dm^{-3}}\).

      Memorise the pair as \(\mathrm{Cu}=1\), \(\mathrm{Zn}=15\).

    10. DDT chlorine count

      DDT is dichloro diphenyl trichloroethane.

      It has \(2\) chlorine atoms on the two phenyl rings and \(3\) chlorine atoms on the ethane part.

      Total chlorine atoms in DDT \(=2+3=5\).

    11. Eutrophication

      Eutrophication is nutrient enrichment of water bodies.

      It causes excessive algal growth.

      Decomposition of algal biomass consumes dissolved oxygen.

      Final effect: dissolved oxygen decreases and aquatic life is harmed.

    12. Non-biodegradable industrial waste

      Thermal power plants generate fly ash and related solid residues.

      These are treated as non-biodegradable industrial wastes in the PYQ pattern.

      This is the expected choice when compared with cotton mills or paper mills.

    13. Water-softening method

      Ion-exchange process is the most effective water-softening method among the listed choices.

      Boiling removes only temporary hardness.

      Permutit is useful, but ion exchange is the more complete method in this comparison.


    1. Polymer class anchors

      Dacron/terylene: fibre and polyester.

      Nylon-6,6: fibre and polyamide.

      Neoprene: elastomer.

      Bakelite and urea-formaldehyde resin: thermosetting polymers.

      PVC and polystyrene: thermoplastics.

    2. Addition versus condensation polymers

      Addition polymers form from unsaturated monomers without loss of small molecules.

      Polystyrene, PVC, teflon, neoprene and polythene are addition polymers.

      Condensation polymers form from bifunctional monomers with loss of small molecules such as water.

      Nylon-6,6, dacron/terylene, bakelite, glyptal and urea-formaldehyde resin are condensation polymers.

    3. Urea-formaldehyde resin

      Urea-formaldehyde resin is formed from urea and formaldehyde.

      It is used for making unbreakable cups and laminated sheets.

      Bakelite also uses formaldehyde, but its other monomer is phenol.

    4. Dacron / terylene

      Dacron or terylene is a polyester.

      Monomers: ethylene glycol and terephthalic acid.

      \( \mathrm{HO{-}CH_2{-}CH_2{-}OH+HOOC{-}C_6H_4{-}COOH\rightarrow dacron/terylene} \)

      Terephthalic acid is benzene-\(1,4\)-dicarboxylic acid.

    5. Ethene to ethylene glycol

      \( \mathrm{CH_2{=}CH_2\xrightarrow[273\,K]{cold,\ dilute,\ alkaline\ KMnO_4}HO{-}CH_2{-}CH_2{-}OH} \)

      Ethylene glycol is then used with terephthalic acid to form dacron/terylene.

      Mild alkaline \(\mathrm{KMnO_4}\) gives hydroxylation, not oxidative cleavage.

    6. Glyptal

      Glyptal is formed from ethylene glycol and phthalic acid.

      Phthalic acid is benzene-\(1,2\)-dicarboxylic acid.

      Glyptal is used in paints.

      Keep phthalic acid for glyptal and terephthalic acid for dacron/terylene.

    7. Nylon-6,6

      Nylon-6,6 is a polyamide and fibre-forming polymer.

      Monomers: hexamethylenediamine and adipic acid.

      \( \mathrm{H_2N(CH_2)_6NH_2+HOOC(CH_2)_4COOH\rightarrow nylon{-}6,6} \)

      Polyamide chains contain \(\mathrm{-CONH-}\) linkages and are held strongly by hydrogen bonding.

    8. Nylon-6

      Nylon-6 is formed from caprolactam.

      Caprolactam undergoes ring-opening polymerisation on heating with water at about \(533{-}543\,\mathrm{K}\).

      Repeating unit: \(\mathrm{(-CO-(CH_2)_5-NH-)_n}\).

      Nylon-6 comes from caprolactam; nylon-6,6 comes from hexamethylenediamine and adipic acid.

    9. Neoprene

      Neoprene is an elastomer.

      Monomer: chloroprene, \(\mathrm{CH_2=C(Cl)-CH=CH_2}\).

      IUPAC name of chloroprene: \(2\)-chloro-\(1,3\)-butadiene.

      Natural rubber monomer is isoprene, \(2\)-methyl-\(1,3\)-butadiene.

    10. Natural rubber and elastomer examples

      Natural rubber is formed from isoprene.

      Isoprene \(=\) \(2\)-methyl-\(1,3\)-butadiene.

      Elastomers: natural rubber, vulcanized rubber, buna-N and neoprene.

      Polyethene and PVC are thermoplastics; bakelite is thermosetting; nylon-6 is a fibre.

    11. Bakelite and melamine polymer

      Bakelite is phenol-formaldehyde resin.

      Melamine-formaldehyde resin is formed from melamine and formaldehyde.

      Common monomer in bakelite and melamine polymer: formaldehyde, also called methanal.

      Bakelite is hard, insulating and thermosetting; it is used in electrical switches.

    12. Teflon, PVC and polystyrene monomers

      Teflon monomer: \(\mathrm{CF_2=CF_2}\).

      PVC monomer: vinyl chloride, \(\mathrm{CH_2=CHCl}\).

      Polystyrene monomer: styrene.

      PVC and neoprene both contain chlorine, but their monomers and polymer classes are different.

    13. Silicones

      Silicones have repeating \(\mathrm{-Si(R)_2-O-}\) units.

      They are used for waterproofing fabrics.

      Waterproofing is mainly due to hydrophobic organic groups such as \(\mathrm{-CH_3}\), which make the surface water-repellent.

    14. Biodegradable polymer anchor

      Nylon-2-nylon-6 is a biodegradable polyamide.

      It is formed from glycine and aminocaproic acid.

      This is a standard biodegradable polymer example in the chapter.

    15. Ziegler-Natta catalyst and HDPE

      Ziegler-Natta catalysts such as \((\mathrm{C_2H_5})_3\mathrm{Al}/\mathrm{TiCl_4}\) are used to manufacture high-density polythene.

      HDPE is more linear and less branched.

      LDPE is associated with free-radical polymerisation under high pressure and has more branching.

    16. Weight-average molecular mass

      \( \overline{M}_w=\dfrac{\sum n_iM_i^2}{\sum n_iM_i} \)

      Number-average molecular mass:

      \( \overline{M}_n=\dfrac{\sum n_iM_i}{\sum n_i} \)

      Use \(\overline{M}_w\) when weight-average molecular mass is asked.

    17. Organic conversion to terephthalic acid and dacron

      A conversion sequence ending in terephthalic acid points to dacron/terylene.

      Terephthalic acid is a monomer of dacron.

      Dacron is not nylon-6, nylon-6,6 or bakelite because their monomer sets are different.


    1. Sucrose hydrolysis

      \( \mathrm{sucrose+H_2O\xrightarrow{H^+} glucose+fructose} \)

      Sucrose gives glucose and fructose on hydrolysis.

      Glucose is an aldohexose; fructose is a ketohexose.

      Maltose gives glucose + glucose, while lactose gives glucose + galactose.

    2. Glucose oxidation to gluconic acid and saccharic acid

      Open-chain glucose: \( \mathrm{CHO-(CHOH)_4-CH_2OH} \)

      \( \mathrm{glucose\xrightarrow{Br_2/H_2O} gluconic\ acid} \)

      \( \mathrm{glucose\xrightarrow{conc.\ HNO_3} saccharic\ acid} \)

      \(\mathrm{Br_2/H_2O}\) oxidises only \(\mathrm{-CHO}\) to \(\mathrm{-COOH}\).

      Concentrated \(\mathrm{HNO_3}\) oxidises both terminal \(\mathrm{-CHO}\) and \(\mathrm{-CH_2OH}\).

    3. Glucose reduction with HI

      \( \mathrm{glucose\xrightarrow[\Delta]{HI} n\text{-}hexane} \)

      This shows that glucose has a straight six-carbon chain.

      To get \(n\)-hexane by Wurtz reaction, use \(1\)-chloropropane:

      \( \mathrm{2CH_3CH_2CH_2Cl+2Na\xrightarrow{dry\ ether}CH_3CH_2CH_2CH_2CH_2CH_3+2NaCl} \)

    4. Disaccharide composition and linkages

      Sucrose \(=\alpha\)-D-glucose + \(\beta\)-D-fructose.

      Maltose \(=\) two D-glucose units with \(\alpha(1\rightarrow4)\)-glycosidic linkage.

      Lactose \(=\beta\)-D-galactose + \(\beta\)-D-glucose.

      Maltose contains no fructose.

    5. Cellulose structure

      Cellulose is a polysaccharide of \(\beta\)-D-glucose units.

      The units are joined by \(\beta(1\rightarrow4)\)-glycosidic linkages.

      Starch is based on \(\alpha\)-D-glucose, while cellulose is based on \(\beta\)-D-glucose.

    6. Diastase and maltase

      Malt contains diastase.

      Diastase converts starch into maltose.

      Maltase converts maltose into glucose.

      Keep the enzyme-substrate-product sequence separate: starch \(\rightarrow\) maltose \(\rightarrow\) glucose.

    7. Kiliani-Fischer chain lengthening

      Cyanohydrin formation followed by hydrolysis increases an aldose chain by one carbon.

      A six-carbon aldose framework gives a seven-carbon hydroxy acid.

      Product name pattern: \(2,3,4,5,6,7\)-hexahydroxyheptanoic acid.

    8. Amino acid side-chain anchors

      Tyrosine: \(\mathrm{-CH_2-C_6H_4-OH}\).

      Cysteine: \(\mathrm{-CH_2-SH}\).

      Asparagine: \(\mathrm{-CH_2-CONH_2}\).

      Serine: \(\mathrm{-CH_2-OH}\).

      Methionine: \(\mathrm{-CH_2-CH_2-S-CH_3}\).

    9. Amino acid functional-group identification

      Tyrosine contains phenolic \(\mathrm{-OH}\), because \(\mathrm{-OH}\) is directly attached to an aromatic ring.

      Serine and threonine contain alcoholic \(\mathrm{-OH}\), not phenolic \(\mathrm{-OH}\).

      Glutamine contains amide side chain \(\mathrm{-CH_2-CH_2-CONH_2}\).

      Asparagine also contains an amide group, but with side chain \(\mathrm{-CH_2-CONH_2}\).

    10. Hormones by chemical nature

      Insulin is a polypeptide hormone.

      Epinephrine is amine-derived.

      Estrogen and androgen are steroid hormones.

      Not all hormones are proteins or peptides.

    11. Aspartame

      Aspartame is a methyl ester of a dipeptide.

      It contains aspartic acid and phenylalanine units.

      Saccharin and sucralose are artificial sweeteners, but they are not aspartic-acid/phenylalanine based.

      Sucralose is a chlorinated sucrose derivative.

    12. Vitamin B-complex names

      Vitamin \(\mathrm{B_1}\): thiamine.

      Vitamin \(\mathrm{B_2}\): riboflavin.

      Vitamin \(\mathrm{B_6}\): pyridoxine.

      Vitamin \(\mathrm{B_{12}}\): cobalamin.

      Pyridoxine \(=\) vitamin \(\mathrm{B_6}\).


    1. Common everyday uses of selected compounds

      \(\mathrm{KOH}\): absorbs \(\mathrm{CO_2}\).

      Liquid sodium: coolant.

      Lithium: electrochemical cells.

      \(\mathrm{Mg(OH)_2}\): antacid; neutralises stomach acid.

    2. Aspartame

      Aspartame is an artificial sweetener.

      It is a methyl ester of a dipeptide containing aspartic acid and phenylalanine units.

      Saccharin and sucralose are also artificial sweeteners, but they are not aspartic-acid/phenylalanine based.

      Sucralose is a chlorinated sucrose derivative.

    3. Antibiotics

      Chloramphenicol is a broad-spectrum antibiotic.

      It is primarily bacteriostatic, meaning it inhibits bacterial growth.

      It has been used for typhoid treatment in the standard syllabus context.

      In structure-based questions, chloramphenicol is recognised by a nitrobenzene ring, hydroxyl-containing side chain and dichloroacetamide group.

    4. Antiseptic, sweetener, preservative and antifertility drug

      Bithionol: antiseptic.

      Saccharin: artificial sweetener.

      Sodium benzoate: food preservative.

      Norethindrone: antifertility drug.

    5. Other high-frequency drug/use matches

      BHT: antioxidant in food.

      Alitame: artificial sweetener.

      Ranitidine and cimetidine: antacid-related drugs.

      Equanil: tranquilizer used for depression and hypertension in the syllabus pattern.

      Paracetamol: analgesic-antipyretic.

    6. Antihistamines

      Dimetapp and Seldane are antihistamines.

      Iproniazid/Nardil are antidepressant-type drugs.

      Veronal/Valium are sedative-tranquilizer examples.

      Heroin/codeine are narcotic analgesics.

    7. Aspirin

      Aspirin is acetylsalicylic acid.

      Its structure has an aromatic ring bearing \(\mathrm{-COOH}\) and an acetylated phenoxy group \(\mathrm{-OCOCH_3}\).

      Aspirin shows anti-inflammatory, antipyretic and anticoagulant effects.

      It helps in prevention of heart attacks because it reduces platelet aggregation and acts as an anti-blood-clotting agent.

    8. Sodium lauryl sulphate

      Lauryl alcohol is dodecyl alcohol: \(\mathrm{CH_3-(CH_2)_{10}-CH_2OH}\).

      Sodium lauryl sulphate: \(\mathrm{CH_3-(CH_2)_{10}-CH_2OSO_3Na}\).

      In \(\mathrm{CH_3-(CH_2)_n-CH_2OSO_3Na}\), \(n=10\).

    9. Soap lathering additive

      Sodium rosinate is added to soaps to improve lathering or foaming property.

      Sodium stearate is the basic soap component.

    10. Tranquilizer-related PYQ pair

      Valium and serotonin are the tranquilizer-related pair in the given PYQ key.

      Dimetapp/Seldane belong to antihistamines.

      Heroin/codeine belong to narcotic analgesics.

      Cimetidine/ranitidine belong to antacid-related drugs.
